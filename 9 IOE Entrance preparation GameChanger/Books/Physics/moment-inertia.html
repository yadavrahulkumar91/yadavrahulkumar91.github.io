Moment of Inertia

Introduction

Moment of inertia is a property of a rigid body that describes its resistance to rotational motion about a particular axis. It depends on the mass distribution and the axis of rotation.

Moment of Inertia Formula

The moment of inertia ($I$) of a body is calculated using the formula:

$I = \sum{m_i \times r_i^2}$

where $m_i$ is the mass of each small element in the body and $r_i$ is the perpendicular distance of each element from the axis of rotation.

Units of Moment of Inertia

The SI unit of moment of inertia is kilogram-meter squared (kg·m²). However, other commonly used units include gram-centimeter squared (g·cm²) and kilogram-centimeter squared (kg·cm²).

Parallel Axis Theorem

The parallel axis theorem is used to calculate the moment of inertia of a body about an axis parallel to and at a distance from the axis passing through the center of mass. It is given by:

$I = I_{\text{cm}} + M \times d^2$

where $I_{\text{cm}}$ is the moment of inertia about the center of mass, $M$ is the mass of the body, and $d$ is the distance between the parallel axes.

Examples of Moment of Inertia

The moment of inertia depends on the shape and mass distribution of the body. Here are a few examples:

  • A thin rod rotating about its end: $I = \frac{1}{3} \times m \times L^2$
  • A disc rotating about its central axis: $I = \frac{1}{2} \times m \times r^2$
  • A hoop rotating about its central axis: $I = m \times r^2$

Answer Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs)

  1. Question 1: What is moment of inertia?

    • a) A force that causes rotational motion
    • b) A property of a body that describes its resistance to rotational motion
    • c) A measure of the linear motion of a body
    • d) A measure of the body's acceleration

    Answer: b) A property of a body that describes its resistance to rotational motion

  2. Question 2: Which formula is used to calculate moment of inertia?

    • a) $I = m \times r$
    • b) $I = \sum{m_i \times r_i}$
    • c) $I = \sum{m_i \times r_i^2}$
    • d) $I = \frac{1}{2} \times m \times r^2$

    Answer: c) $I = \sum{m_i \times r_i^2}$

  3. Question 3: Which unit is used to measure moment of inertia?

    • a) Newton (N)
    • b) Joule (J)
    • c) Kilogram (kg)
    • d) Kilogram-meter squared (kg·m²)

    Answer: d) Kilogram-meter squared (kg·m²)

  4. Question 4: What does the parallel axis theorem state?

    • a) The moment of inertia of a body is independent of its mass
    • b) The moment of inertia of a body about an axis parallel to the center of mass axis is zero
    • c) The moment of inertia of a body about an axis parallel to the center of mass axis is equal to the moment of inertia about the center of mass plus the product of mass and distance squared
    • d) The moment of inertia of a body depends only on its shape, not its mass distribution

    Answer: c) The moment of inertia of a body about an axis parallel to the center of mass axis is equal to the moment of inertia about the center of mass plus the product of mass and distance squared
