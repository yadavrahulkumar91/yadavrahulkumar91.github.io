Fluid Pressure

Introduction

In this chapter, we will explore the concept of fluid pressure. Fluid pressure is the force per unit area exerted by a fluid on the surfaces in contact with it. Understanding fluid pressure is essential in various fields, such as hydrodynamics, engineering, and everyday applications involving fluids.

Pascal's Law

Pascal's law states that when a pressure is applied to an enclosed fluid, the pressure is transmitted undiminished to all portions of the fluid and to the walls of its container. Mathematically, this can be expressed as:

$$P = \frac{F}{A}$$

  • $$P$$ is the fluid pressure
  • $$F$$ is the force applied to the fluid
  • $$A$$ is the area over which the force is applied

Fluid Pressure in a Liquid

The pressure exerted by a liquid at a given depth is given by:

$$P = P_0 + \rho gh$$

  • $$P$$ is the pressure at depth $$h$$
  • $$P_0$$ is the atmospheric pressure or pressure at the surface of the liquid
  • $$\rho$$ is the density of the liquid
  • $$g$$ is the acceleration due to gravity
  • $$h$$ is the depth below the surface

Archimedes' Principle

Archimedes' principle states that when an object is submerged in a fluid, it experiences an upward buoyant force equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by the object. The buoyant force can be calculated using:

$$F_b = \rho_{\text{fluid}} V_{\text{displaced}} g$$

  • $$F_b$$ is the buoyant force
  • $$\rho_{\text{fluid}}$$ is the density of the fluid
  • $$V_{\text{displaced}}$$ is the volume of the fluid displaced by the object
  • $$g$$ is the acceleration due to gravity

Real-Life Applications

  • Fluid pressure plays a crucial role in the functioning of hydraulic systems, such as car brakes and lifts.
  • Understanding fluid pressure is essential in the design and operation of underwater structures, submarines, and diving equipment.
  • Fluid pressure is involved in the measurement of blood pressure using a sphygmomanometer.
  • Fluid pressure determines the performance of wings and aerodynamic forces on aircraft.

MCQs and Answers

  1. The pressure exerted by a fluid at a given depth depends on:
    1. The density of the fluid
    2. The acceleration due to gravity
    3. The depth below the surface
    4. All of the above

    Answer: D

  2. When an object is submerged in a fluid, the buoyant force is equal to:
    1. The weight of the object
    2. The weight of the fluid
    3. The weight of the fluid displaced by the object
    4. The weight of the object minus the weight of the fluid

    Answer: C

Tricks

  • Trick 1: The pressure exerted by a fluid depends only on the depth and the density of the fluid, not on the shape or size of the container.
  • Trick 2: Objects float or sink in a fluid based on the relationship between their densities and the density of the fluid.

    1. Increases with increasing velocity

    Answer: C

    1. Half the force exerted on the input piston
    2. Equal to the force exerted on the input piston
    3. Twice the force exerted on the input piston
