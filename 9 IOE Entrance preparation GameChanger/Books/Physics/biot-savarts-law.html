Introduction

Biot-Savart's law is a fundamental principle in electromagnetism that relates the magnetic field produced by a steady current to the current element. It provides a mathematical expression for calculating the magnetic field at a point due to a small segment of a current-carrying wire. Biot-Savart's law is essential for understanding the behavior of magnetic fields generated by current-carrying conductors and plays a crucial role in various applications in electromagnetism. In this section, we will explore the principles, equations, and applications of Biot-Savart's law.

Key Concepts

Understanding the key concepts related to Biot-Savart's law is essential. These include:

  • Magnetic Field: Biot-Savart's law helps calculate the magnetic field at a point in space due to a current-carrying conductor.
  • Current Element: A small segment of a current-carrying wire, represented by the vector product of the current element and the displacement vector.
  • Magnetic Field Vector: The magnetic field at a point is determined by integrating the contributions from all current elements along the conductor.
  • Permeability of Free Space: The magnetic field depends on the permeability of free space, denoted by the symbol μ₀.

Biot-Savart's Law Equation

Biot-Savart's law is expressed by the following equation:

B = (μ₀/4π) * ∫ (I * dl × r) / r³

where:

  • B is the magnetic field at a point (in Tesla).
  • μ₀ is the permeability of free space (4π × 10⁻⁷ T m/A).
  • I is the current flowing through the wire (in Amperes).
  • dl is an infinitesimally small length element along the wire.
  • r is the displacement vector from the current element to the point where the magnetic field is calculated.

Applications

Biot-Savart's law has various practical applications, including:

  • Magnetic Field of a Straight Wire: Biot-Savart's law helps calculate the magnetic field around a long, straight current-carrying wire.
  • Magnetic Field of a Circular Loop: The law is used to determine the magnetic field at the center of a circular loop carrying current.
  • Magnetic Field of a Solenoid: Biot-Savart's law is applied to calculate the magnetic field inside and outside a long solenoid.
  • Magnetic Field of a Current-Carrying Coil: The law helps determine the magnetic field produced by a current-carrying coil of wire.
  • Magnetic Field of a Straight Current-Carrying Conductor: Biot-Savart's law is used to calculate the magnetic field produced by a straight current-carrying conductor at a point in space.
