Introduction

Stokes' law is a fundamental principle in fluid mechanics that describes the motion of small particles through a viscous fluid. It was derived by the Irish physicist George Gabriel Stokes and is particularly applicable to particles that have a negligible effect on the fluid flow.

Statement of Stokes' Law

Stokes' law states that the drag force experienced by a small spherical particle moving through a viscous fluid is directly proportional to its velocity and the viscosity of the fluid, and is also dependent on the radius of the particle.

The mathematical expression for the drag force can be written as:

$F_d = 6 \pi \eta r v$

where:

  • $F_d$ is the drag force
  • $\eta$ is the viscosity of the fluid
  • $r$ is the radius of the particle
  • $v$ is the velocity of the particle

Applications of Stokes' Law

Stokes' law has several practical applications, including:

  • Understanding the settling of small particles in a fluid, such as sedimentation processes
  • Estimating the size and properties of colloidal particles and emulsions
  • Calculating the terminal velocity of small particles falling through a fluid
  • Characterizing the behavior of particles in microfluidic systems and lab-on-a-chip devices

Example

Let's work through an example to understand the concept of Stokes' law:

Example: A small spherical particle with a radius of 0.001 meters is moving through a fluid with a viscosity of 0.01 Pa·s. If the velocity of the particle is 0.1 m/s, calculate the drag force acting on the particle.

Using Stokes' law and the formula for drag force:

$F_d = 6 \pi \eta r v$

Substituting the given values:

$F_d = 6 \pi \times 0.01 \, \text{Pa·s} \times 0.001 \, \text{m} \times 0.1 \, \text{m/s} \approx 0.00188 \, \text{N}$

Therefore, the drag force acting on the particle is approximately 0.00188 N.

Answer Questions

  1. Question 1: What is Stokes' law?

    Answer: Stokes' law describes the motion of small particles through a viscous fluid and relates the drag force experienced by the particle to its velocity, viscosity of the fluid, and the particle's radius.

  2. Question 2: How is the drag force calculated in Stokes' law?

    Answer: The drag force ($F_d$) is calculated using the formula $F_d = 6 \pi \eta r v$, where $\eta$ is the viscosity of the fluid, $r$ is the radius of the particle, and $v$ is the velocity of the particle.

  3. Question 3: What are some applications of Stokes' law?

    Answer: Stokes' law is applied in studying the settling of small particles, estimating particle size and properties, calculating terminal velocity, and analyzing microfluidic systems.
