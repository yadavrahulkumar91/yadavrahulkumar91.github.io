Glenohumeral (Shoulder) Joint

  • Joint:
    • Glenohumeral joint
  • Components:
    • Humerus (roughly hemispherical head)
    • Scapula (shallow glenoid fossa)
  • Anatomy:
    • Most mobile joint in the body
    • Most frequently dislocated joint
    • Compromise between motion and stability
    • Dependent on surrounding muscular and soft tissue envelope
  • Stabilizing Mechanism:
    • Rotator cuff muscles
  • Joint Structure:
    • Musculotendinous envelope
    • Articular fibrous capsule
  • Anatomical Boundaries:
    • Coracoid (anteriorly)
    • Coraco-acromial ligament (anterosuperiorly)
    • Acromion and spine of scapula (posteriorly, posterosuperiorly, and posterolaterally)
    • Deep surface of deltoid (anterolaterally and laterally)
  • Gliding Planes:
    • Internal gliding plane (glenohumeral articular gliding plane)
    • External gliding plane (subcoracoid-subacromial-subdeltoid plane)
    • Continuous with scapulothoracic gliding plane
  • Gliding and Restrictions:
    • Synovial lined cavities of the joint and bursae facilitate gliding
    • Infection, inflammation, and injury can cause adhesions and fibrosis
    • Adhesions and fibrosis lead to a restriction of motion
    • Fibrosis is restricting due to extensive surfaces of gliding planes
