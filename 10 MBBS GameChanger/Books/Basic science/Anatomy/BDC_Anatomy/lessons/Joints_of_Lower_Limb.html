Hip Joint

1. Type

The hip joint is a synovial ball-and-socket joint.

2. Articular Surface

The articular surface of the hip joint consists of the acetabulum of the pelvis and the head of the femur.

3. Ligaments

The hip joint is reinforced by several ligaments, including:

  • Iliofemoral ligament
  • Pubofemoral ligament
  • Ischiofemoral ligament
  • Transverse acetabular ligament

4. Bursae related to Joint

Several bursae are associated with the hip joint, including:

  • Trochanteric bursa
  • Iliopsoas bursa
  • Ischial bursa
  • Pectineal bursa

5. Relation

The hip joint is closely related to the following structures:

  • Sacroiliac joint
  • Pubic symphysis
  • Sacrospinous ligament
  • Sacrotuberous ligament

6. Blood supply

The blood supply to the hip joint is primarily provided by the medial and lateral circumflex femoral arteries.

7. Nerve supply

The hip joint receives nerve supply from the following nerves:

  • Femoral nerve
  • Obturator nerve
  • Superior gluteal nerve
  • Inferior gluteal nerve

Movements of Hip Joint and Muscles Causing These

The hip joint allows the following movements:

  • Flexion: Muscles involved include the iliopsoas, rectus femoris, and sartorius.
  • Extension: Muscles involved include the gluteus maximus and hamstrings (biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus).
  • Abduction: Muscles involved include the gluteus medius and gluteus minimus.
  • Adduction: Muscles involved include the adductor magnus, adductor longus, and adductor brevis.
  • Internal rotation: Muscles involved include the gluteus medius, gluteus minimus, and tensor fasciae latae.
  • External rotation: Muscles involved include the piriformis and gemellus muscles.

Knee Joint

1. Type

The knee joint is a synovial hinge joint.

2. Articular Surface

The articular surface of the knee joint consists of the femoral condyles and the tibial plateau.

3. Ligaments

The knee joint is reinforced by several ligaments, including:

  • Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL)
  • Posterior cruciate ligament (PCL)
  • Medial collateral ligament (MCL)
  • Lateral collateral ligament (LCL)

4. Menisci

The knee joint contains two menisci, namely:

  • Medial meniscus
  • Lateral meniscus

5. Bursae related to Joint

Several bursae are associated with the knee joint, including:

  • Prepatellar bursa
  • Infrapatellar bursa
  • Suprapatellar bursa

6. Blood supply

The blood supply to the knee joint is primarily provided by the genicular arteries.

7. Nerve supply

The knee joint receives nerve supply from the following nerves:

  • Femoral nerve
  • Tibial nerve
  • Common fibular nerve
  • Obturator nerve

Movements of Knee Joint and Muscles Causing These

The knee joint allows the following movements:

  • Flexion: Muscles involved include the hamstrings (biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus) and the gastrocnemius.
  • Extension: Muscles involved include the quadriceps femoris (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius).
  • Medial rotation: Muscles involved include the semitendinosus, semimembranosus, and popliteus.
  • Lateral rotation: Muscles involved include the biceps femoris and popliteus.
