Shoulder Joint

1. Type

The shoulder joint is a synovial ball-and-socket joint.

2. Articular Surface

The articular surface of the shoulder joint consists of the glenoid fossa of the scapula and the head of the humerus.

3. Ligaments

The shoulder joint is reinforced by several ligaments, including:

  • Coracohumeral ligament
  • Glenohumeral ligaments (superior, middle, and inferior)
  • Transverse humeral ligament

4. Bursae related to Joint

Several bursae are associated with the shoulder joint, including:

  • Subacromial bursa
  • Subscapular bursa
  • Subcoracoid bursa

5. Relation

The shoulder joint is closely related to the following structures:

  • Acromioclavicular joint
  • Sternoclavicular joint

6. Blood supply

The blood supply to the shoulder joint is primarily provided by the suprascapular artery and the circumflex scapular artery.

7. Nerve supply

The shoulder joint receives nerve supply from the following nerves:

  • Axillary nerve
  • Suprascapular nerve
  • Lateral pectoral nerve

Movements of Shoulder Joint and Muscles Causing These

The shoulder joint allows the following movements:

  • Flexion: Muscles involved include the anterior deltoid, coracobrachialis, and pectoralis major.
  • Extension: Muscles involved include the posterior deltoid and latissimus dorsi.
  • Abduction: Muscles involved include the middle deltoid and supraspinatus.
  • Adduction: Muscles involved include the pectoralis major, latissimus dorsi, and teres major.
  • Internal rotation: Muscles involved include the subscapularis and anterior deltoid.
  • External rotation: Muscles involved include the infraspinatus and teres minor.

Knee Joint

1. Type

The knee joint is a synovial hinge joint.

2. Articular Surface

The articular surface of the knee joint consists of the femoral condyles and the tibial plateau.

3. Ligaments

The knee joint is reinforced by several ligaments, including:

  • Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL)
  • Posterior cruciate ligament (PCL)
  • Medial collateral ligament (MCL)
  • Lateral collateral ligament (LCL)

4. Menisci

The knee joint contains two menisci, namely:

  • Medial meniscus
  • Lateral meniscus

5. Bursae related to Joint

Several bursae are associated with the knee joint, including:

  • Prepatellar bursa
  • Infrapatellar bursa
  • Suprapatellar bursa

6. Blood supply

The blood supply to the knee joint is primarily provided by the genicular arteries.

7. Nerve supply

The knee joint receives nerve supply from the following nerves:

  • Femoral nerve
  • Tibial nerve
  • Common fibular nerve
  • Obturator nerve

Movements of Knee Joint and Muscles Causing These

The knee joint allows the following movements:

  • Flexion: Muscles involved include the hamstrings (biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus) and the gastrocnemius.
  • Extension: Muscles involved include the quadriceps femoris (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius).
  • Medial rotation: Muscles involved include the semitendinosus, semimembranosus, and popliteus.
  • Lateral rotation: Muscles involved include the biceps femoris and popliteus.
