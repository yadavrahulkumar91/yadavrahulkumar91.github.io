Introduction

The development of the heart and circulation in human beings involves a series of complex processes that occur during embryonic and fetal development. This chapter explores the key stages and events that contribute to the formation and functioning of the cardiovascular system.

Formation of the Heart Tube

During early embryonic development, the heart starts as a linear structure called the heart tube. It forms from a pair of heart fields in the lateral plate mesoderm and undergoes folding and fusion to assume its tubular shape.

Cardiac Septation

As the heart tube elongates and bends, it undergoes septation, which involves the formation of partitions that separate the heart into distinct chambers. The septation process results in the formation of the atria, ventricles, and the separation of the pulmonary and systemic circulations.

Valve Development

Simultaneously with septation, the development of heart valves takes place. Endocardial cushions form and undergo remodeling to give rise to the atrioventricular (AV) valves and the semilunar valves, which ensure unidirectional blood flow.

Development of Blood Vessels

Blood vessels, including arteries, veins, and capillaries, develop through a process known as vasculogenesis and angiogenesis. Vasculogenesis involves the de novo formation of blood vessels, while angiogenesis refers to the sprouting and branching of new vessels from pre-existing ones.

Formation of Major Arteries and Veins

During development, major arteries and veins emerge and undergo specific remodeling. The aortic arches, which arise from the heart's arterial system, transform into the mature arteries of the head and neck. The vitelline and umbilical veins undergo changes to establish proper circulation.

Fetal Circulation

The fetal circulation is unique and allows for oxygen and nutrient exchange between the fetal and maternal bloodstreams. It involves structures such as the placenta, umbilical cord, and shunts like the ductus arteriosus and foramen ovale.

Postnatal Changes

At birth, the cardiovascular system undergoes significant changes as the newborn transitions from a fetal to an independent circulatory system. The closure of shunts, increased pulmonary blood flow, and establishment of a fully functional four-chambered heart are essential postnatal adaptations.

Conclusion

The development of the heart and circulation in human beings is a complex and tightly regulated process. Understanding the sequential events and transformations that occur during embryonic and fetal development is crucial for comprehending the normal and abnormal functioning of the cardiovascular system.
