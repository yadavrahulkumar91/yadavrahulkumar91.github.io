            Sexual Reproduction

            Isogamy

            When similar structure present and fuse with one another, it is isogamy. Example: Spirogyra

            Anisogamy

            When dissimilar structure produced and fused, it is anisogamy.

            Oogamy

            When male and female gametes are produced in reproductive cell and fuses with one another, the process is called Oogamy.

            Note:

            Isogamy is most primitive where genetic variation is very low and Oogamy is most advanced due to high genetic variation.

            Chlamydomonas
            1. Unicellular, motile, biflagellate. Flagella are developed from 'Blepharoplast' also known as 'basal granule'.
            2. Single large cup shaped chloroplast with a pyrenoid present in it.
            3. Pyrenoid is made of protein surrounded by starch plate or starch grains and it is the center of starch formation.
            4. A tiny spot of an orange or reddish coloured spot known as stigma or eye spot (contains carotenoids) lies the anterior end.
            5. It is a photoreceptive organ associated with the direction of the movement.
            6. Autotrophic nutrition is found in Chlamydomonas.
            7. The various modes of asexual reproduction are:
            a. By zoospore:
            i. zoospores are biflagellate. [IOM 2001]
            b. By aplanospore
            c. By hypnospores
            d. By palmella stage
            e. By akinetes
            8. Sexual reproduction: Gametic fusion may be isogamous, anisogamous and Oogamous i.e. sex reproduction ranging from isogamy to anisogamy and to Oogamy occurs in the same genus Chlamydomonas.
            9. Note: When unicellular organism produces two types of gametes, then such condition is called as relative sexuality (discovered by Hertman)
            10. Life cycle in Chlamydomonas is haplontic.
            11. If the zygote formed undergoes resting stage, it forms thick covering and is called as Zygospore.
            12. At the time of germination, meiosis takes place in the zygospore and 4 haploid spores are developed each giving a new thallus.

            Volvox Characteristics

            A single colony of volvox looks like a ball of ~0.5 mm in diameter

            The plant body of volvox is a hollow sphere called coenobium, thousands of cells are arranged in the periphery of the sphere

            The cells of coenobium are of two types, germ cells and flagellated somatic cells

            Extracellular matrix of coenobium is made up of glycoproteins

            Individual cells are spherical in shape

            They have a cup-shaped chloroplast

            Chloroplast contains pyrenoids

            The cell has a nucleus, vacuoles and an eyespot.

            Two equal-sized flagella are present in each cell anteriorly. The coordinated movement of flagella enables the colony to move in the water.

            Each cell performs all the metabolic functions independently such as respiration, photosynthesis, excretion, etc.

            Cytoplasmic strands formed during cell division connects adjacent cells

            Eye spot:

            1. Polarity exists in the coenobium as cells of the anterior region have bigger eyespots than cells of the posterior region
            2. Eyespot is used for light reception, cells with larger eyespot are grouped together, and they facilitate phototaxis movement

            Volvox significantly contribute to the production of oxygen and also many aquatic organisms feed on them

            Volvox aureus may multiply rapidly, resulting in the harmful algal bloom (HAB). HAB is more frequent in warm waters having a high nitrogen content

            Volvox Life Cycle

            • The life cycle of Volvox is haplontic, i.e. the dominant stage is free-living haploid (n) gametophyte and the sporophyte is represented only by the diploid zygote (2n)
            • Volvox show cell differentiation in terms of reproductive and somatic cells
            • Most of the species reproduce by both mechanism, asexual under the favourable condition and sexual reproduction during the unfavourable condition
            • In asexual reproduction, reproductive cells known as gonidia produce daughter colonies, which on maturing, are released from the parent
              • The gonidium undergoes multiple division to form a colony of around 3200 cells
            • Sexual reproduction is oogamous
              1. Each coenobia may be monoecious or dioecious
              2. Sexual reproduction is by male and female reproductive cells known as spermatozoa and ova respectively
              3. An ovum is produced inside the oogonium. Ova are large and non-motile. They are green in colour due to the presence of chloroplasts. It contains pyrenoids and has food storage
              4. Spermatozoa are produced inside the antheridium in a bunch. Each sperm is spindle-shaped having a pair of apical cilia
              5. Biflagellated antherozoids are released either in a group or individually
              6. Antherozoids get attracted towards oogonium by the chemotactic movement. They break the wall of oogonia by the action of proteolytic enzyme
              7. Out of many sperms that enter, only one antherozoid fertilises the egg
              8. Ova and spermatozoa undergo fertilization to form a diploid zygote
              9. Zygote forms cyst and becomes red due to the accumulation of hematochrome
              10. The zygote detaches from the parent after the disintegration of the parent and remains dormant for a longer duration
              11. The zygote undergoes meiotic cell division to form four haploid cells.
              12. They further undergo multiple mitotic cell division to form a colony and the life cycle continues

              • Scalariform Conjugation:
                1. Generally in heterothallic species.
                2. Between (+ve) and (-ve) filament conjugation tube forms by the help of Cystage enzyme.
                3. Both filaments come together due to Progammone hormone.
                4. Ladder-like stage is formed.

              • Lateral indirect conjugation
                1. Takes place in homothallic species.
                2. Between active and passive cell, lateral tube forms by the help of which male gamete passes to passive cell.
                3. Note: Gametes are non-motile (no flagella), they move by activity of contractile vacuole.

                1. Takes place in homothallic species.
                2. Between active and passive cell membrane dissolves and male gamete directly passes to passive cell and fertilize.

              • Sexual reproduction of Spirogyra is morphologically Isogamy, but physiologically anisogamy.

            ULOTHRIX

            1. Plant body is represented by an unbranched filament attached to the substratum with the help of 'holdfast'

            • a. Holdfast is hyaline (colourless) as it lacks chlorophyll.

            2. Cell wall consists of two layers:

            • a. Inner layer is made up of cellulose and
            • b. outer layer is mostly made up of protopectin which is insoluble in water.

            3. Filament is wetty due to protopectin.

            4. Cell consists of single girdle shaped chloroplast with 1 or more pyrenoids.

            5. Asexual reproduction by (Under favourable condition)

            • a. Ζoospores:
              • i. Zoospores of Ulothrix are quadriflagellate.
              • ii. Quadriflagellate zoospores may be: microzoospore or macrozoospore.
              • iii. Sometimes, it also produces biflagellate microzoospores.
              • iv. All cells except holdfast are capable of producing zoospores.
            • b. Αplanospores: Thin
            • c. Ηypnospores: Thick
            • d. Αkinetes
            • e. Palmella stage

            6. Sexual reproduction

            • a. Isogamous type. Ulothrix is heterothallic.
            • b. Fertilization takes place on water surface (external fertilization)
            • c. The gametes are motile and biflagellate.
            • d. When two gametes of (+) and (-) strain come together, they fuse and a quadriflagellate zygote formed.
            • e. When tetraflagellated zygote goes to bottom, it loses flagella and forms thick covering called a zygospore.
            • f. After undergoing a period of rest, the zygospore undergoes meiosis to produce haploid meiozoospore which under favourable conditions germinate to produce new filaments.
            • g. All spores germinate. Each spore germinates into 1 plant.

            7. Life cycle in Ulolthrix is haplontic.
