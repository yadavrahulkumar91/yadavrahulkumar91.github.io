Describe primary and secondary immune responses.

The primary immune response is the initial immune response to an antigen. When an antigen enters the body for the first time, it is recognized as foreign by the immune system, and B cells and T cells are activated to produce antibodies and eliminate the antigen. The primary response takes time to develop, usually 7-10 days, and the antibody levels are relatively low. The secondary response is the basis of immunological memory, which provides long-term protection against re-infection with the same pathogen.
