Culture Media

Nutrient Agar

Constituents

  1. Peptones
  2. Distilled water
  3. Beef extract
  4. NaCl

Uses

  1. To obtain bacteria in pure form
  2. To prepare bacteria for biochemical test
  3. To study pigmentation produced by bacteria and identify the bacteria
    • Staphylococcus aureus$\rightarrow$ Golden yellow, Non-diffusible
    • Pseudomona$\rightarrow$ Green, Diffusible

Blood Agar (Solid media)

Constituents

  1. Nutrient agar
  2. $5-10%$ sheep or horse blood

Uses

  1. To grow fastidious organism
    1. Streptococcus pneumonia
    2. Streptococcus pyogenes
  2. To see the pigmentation produced by bacteria
  3. To study satellitism phenomenon
  4. To see the hemolytic reaction
    1. α-hemolysis (Parital hemolysis): Greenish discoloration around the colonies.
      1. Streptococcus pneumonia
    2. β-hemolysis (Complete hemolysis): Clear halo around the colonies.
      1. Streptococcus pyogenes
    3. γ-hemolysis (No hemolysis):
        4. Enterococcus faecalis
  1. Use
    1. General Culture
  2. Example of Bacteria
    1. Staphylococcus aureus (β-haemolysis)
    2. Streptococcus

Chocolate Agar (Solid media- Enriched)

Constituents

  1. Heated blood agar (70$^\circ $ C)

Uses

  1. To grow fastidious organisms
    1. Haemophilus influenzae
    2. Neisseria gonorrhoeae

Mac Conkey Agar Lactose Fermenter

Constituents

  1. Basal media
  2. Neutral red
  3. Crystal violet
  4. Bile salt
  5. Lacotse

Uses

  1. Used as Indicator medium (Contain indicator: Neutral red)
  2. Used as Selective medium (Bile salt inhibit growth of Gram +ve bacteria and allow the growh of gram -ve bacteria)
  3. Used as Differential medium: Differentiate bacteria in 2 grops
    1. Lactose fermenting $\rightarrow$ form Pink colonies
      1. E. coli
      2. Klebsiella
    2. Non-lactose fermenter
      1. Salmonells
      2. Shigella

CLED (cysteine-, lactose- and electrolyte-deficient) Agar

Mueller–Hinton agar

Features

  1. Constituents of medium are well-defined.
  2. No enrichment or selective components are included.
  3. Supports the growth of all types of pathogens.
  4. Appropriate for adding blood and serum.
  5. As a result, Muller Hinton agar is preferred over nutrient agar for Antibiotic Sensitivity tests (AST).

Sulfide Indole Motility (SIM) Medium Positive

  1. Tests following things
    1. Sulphide ($H_2S$) $\rightarrow$ Dark color
    2. Indole $\rightarrow$ Red ring at top
    3. Motility test $\rightarrow$ Turbid is due to motility

Triple Sugar Iron Test

  1. Normal color (No bacterial growth) $\rightarrow $ Red (Alkaline)
  2. Three things is tested
    1. Fermentation
      1. Slant $\rightarrow $ Acid(A) or Alkaline(K)
      2. Butt $\rightarrow $ Acid(A) or Alkaline(K)
    2. Gas production
    3. $H_2S$ production

Citrate Utilization Test

  1. Normal Dark green
  2. Bacteria utilizes citrate to convert into Red

Urea Hydrolysis Test (Positive)

  1. Normal is Yellow which is negative
  2. Urea is utilized to convert into $NH_3$ and $CO_2$ which converts into Pink Color

Robertson's Cooked Meat (RCM) Medium

Constituents

  1. Heart muscle
  2. Peptone

  4. Glucose

Uses

  1. To culture of anaerobic bacteria. Examples
    1. Clostridium perfringnes
  2. As transfer medium
  3. To see whether the bacteria is proteolytic or not
    • Clostridia produce proteolysis $\rightarrow$ Black coloured meat
