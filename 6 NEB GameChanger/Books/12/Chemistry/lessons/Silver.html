7.5 Silver

7.5.1 Occurrence and Extraction of Silver by Cyanide Process

Silver is a precious metal that occurs naturally in various ores and minerals. One of the main ores of silver is argentite (Ag2S). The extraction of silver from argentite involves the following steps using the cyanide process:

  1. Grinding and Concentration: The argentite ore is first crushed into small pieces and then concentrated by froth flotation or gravity separation methods to obtain a silver-rich concentrate.
  2. Roasting: The concentrated ore is then roasted in the presence of air, converting silver sulfide (Ag2S) to silver oxide (Ag2O) and sulfur dioxide (SO2) gas.
  3. Leaching: The roasted ore is subjected to leaching with a dilute solution of sodium cyanide (NaCN) in the presence of air. Silver cyanide (AgCN) forms as a soluble complex.
  4. Precipitation: The silver cyanide solution is treated with a suitable reagent, such as zinc (Zn), to precipitate metallic silver (Ag) while regenerating sodium cyanide for reuse.
  5. Refining: The obtained metallic silver is further purified through processes such as electrolysis or chemical refining to remove impurities and obtain high-quality silver.

7.5.2 Preparation and Uses of Silver Chloride and Silver Nitrate

Silver chloride (AgCl) and silver nitrate (AgNO3) are two important compounds of silver with various applications:

  • Silver Chloride (AgCl):
    • Preparation: Silver chloride can be prepared by the reaction of silver nitrate with a chloride source, such as hydrochloric acid (HCl). The resulting white precipitate is silver chloride.
    • Uses: Silver chloride has been historically used in photography as a light-sensitive material. It is also used in certain medical applications and as a component in some silver-based electrical conductive pastes.
  • Silver Nitrate (AgNO3):
    • Preparation: Silver nitrate can be prepared by dissolving metallic silver in nitric acid (HNO3). The resulting solution is silver nitrate.
    • Uses: Silver nitrate has various applications, including its use in photography, silver plating, and as a reagent in chemical laboratories. It is also used in the production of certain mirrors, jewelry, and medical products.
