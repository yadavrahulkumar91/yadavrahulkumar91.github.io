7.3 Mercury

7.3.1 Occurrence and Extraction of Mercury from Cinnabar

Mercury is a naturally occurring element that is often found in the form of cinnabar (HgS), a red ore. The extraction of mercury from cinnabar involves the following steps:

  1. Crushing and Roasting: The cinnabar ore is first crushed into a fine powder and then heated in the presence of air. The roasting process converts cinnabar into mercury vapor and sulfur dioxide gas.
  2. Condensation: The mercury vapor is then cooled and condensed to form liquid mercury. The condensation is usually done in a condenser or a series of condensers.
  3. Purification: The obtained liquid mercury is further purified to remove impurities such as sulfur and other metals.

7.3.2 Properties of Mercury

Mercury possesses several unique properties:

  • Physical State: Mercury is the only metal that is a liquid at room temperature and standard pressure.
  • Density: It is a dense liquid with a high density of about 13.6 g/cm3, making it over 13 times denser than water.
  • Low Melting and Boiling Point: Mercury has a low melting point of -38.83°C and a boiling point of 356.73°C.
  • Poor Electrical and Thermal Conductivity: Mercury is a poor conductor of both electricity and heat compared to other metals.
  • High Surface Tension: It exhibits a high surface tension, allowing it to form spherical droplets.

7.3.3 Chemistry (Preparation, Properties, and Uses) of Calomel and Corrosive Sublimate

Calomel and corrosive sublimate are two important compounds of mercury with distinct properties and applications:

  • Calomel (Mercurous Chloride, Hg2Cl2):
    • Preparation: Calomel can be prepared by the reaction of mercuric chloride (HgCl2) with a reducing agent such as metallic mercury.
    • Properties: Calomel is a white crystalline powder that is insoluble in water. It is used as a mild laxative and in certain electrochemical applications.
    • Uses: Calomel has been historically used in medicine as a purgative and as an electrode in certain types of electrochemical cells.
  • Corrosive Sublimate (Mercuric Chloride, HgCl2):
    • Preparation: Corrosive sublimate can be prepared by the reaction of mercury with chlorine gas.
    • Properties: Corrosive sublimate is a white crystalline solid that is highly toxic. It is soluble in water and has strong antiseptic properties.
    • Uses: Corrosive sublimate has been used as an antiseptic, disinfectant, and pesticide. However, due to its toxicity, its use has been restricted in many countries.
