(a) To calculate the amount of photons per second, we can use the formula: Amount of photons per second = Power / Energy of one photon. Energy of one photon can be calculated using the formula: E = hf, where E is the energy, h is Planck's constant (6.626 x 10^(-34) J·s), and f is the frequency. Substituting the given values, we have E = (6.626 x 10^(-34) J·s) * (220 x 10^3 Hz). The power of the emitter is 66 MW, which is equivalent to 66 x 10^6 W. Dividing the power by the energy of one photon, we get (66 x 10^6 W) / [(6.626 x 10^(-34) J·s) * (220 x 10^3 Hz)] = 6 x 10^32 photons per second.
