  4. ABCD is a parallelogram. Which one of the following represents area of the parallelogram?

    2. Magnitude of vector product of two vectors along AB and DC.
    3. Magnitude of vector product of two vectors along AC and BC.
    4. Magnitude of vector product of two vectors along AB and AD.
    (b) The area of the parallelogram is equal to the magnitude of the vector product of two adjacent sides. Here, AB and DC are two adjacent sides of the parallelogram, so the magnitude of the vector product of AB and DC represents the area of the parallelogram.

  8. The edge of a cube increases from 10 cm to 10.025 cm. What would be the approximate increment in volume?
    1. 103 cm^3
    2. 10.0253 cm^3
    3. 7.5187 cm^3
    4. 7.5 cm^3
    (c) The volume of a cube is given by V = a^3, where a is the edge length. The change in volume is approximately given by ΔV ≈ 3a^2Δa, where Δa is the change in the edge length. Plugging in the given values, we have ΔV ≈ 3(10 cm)^2(0.025 cm) ≈ 7.5 cm^3.
  9. What is the integrating factor of the differential equation cos'× dy + y =1°
    1. tan x
    2. e^tanx
    3. sec^2 x
    4. e^sec1x
    (c) The given differential equation is cos(x)dy/dx + y = 1. The integrating factor is e^(∫cos(x)dx), which is e^(sin(x)). Multiplying both sides of the differential equation by this integrating factor, we get e^(sin(x))cos(x)dy/dx + e^(sin(x))y = e^(sin(x)). The left-hand side can be written as the derivative of the product e^(sin(x))y with respect to x, so the solution to the differential equation is e^(sin(x))y = ∫e^(sin(x))dx + C, where C is the constant of integration. This integral does not have a closed-form solution in terms of elementary functions.
  10. What is the number of solutions of the system of linear equations × + y = 5 and x + y = 7?
    1. One solution
    2. No solution
    3. Infinite solutions
    4. More than one solution
    (c) The given system of linear equations × + y = 5 and x + y = 7 can be solved to give the unique solution (x,y) = (1,4). However, it can also be observed that the two equations represent two parallel lines with the same slope, so they intersect at every point along the lines. Therefore, the system has infinite solutions.
