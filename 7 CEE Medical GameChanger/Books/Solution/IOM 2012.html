    (a) The temperature when the thermometer reads 25°C can be calculated using the proportion of the temperature scale between the freezing and boiling points.     1. 1:8
    2. 1:4
    3. 8:1
    4. 1:1
    (b) The amount of heat conducted per unit time is proportional to the product of the cross-sectional area and the temperature difference. In this case, the diameters of the rods are in the ratio 1:1, so their cross-sectional areas are also in the ratio 1:1. The lengths of the rods are in the ratio 2:1, so the temperature difference along the length is also in the ratio 2:1. Therefore, the ratio of the amount of heat induced through them per unit time is (1:1) * (2:1) = 1:4.

    1. 0.3845/day
    2. 0.0234/day
    3. 0.467/day
    4. 0.0467/day
    (d) The decay constant represents the fraction of radioactive material that decays per unit time. To find the decay constant, we can use the formula: decay constant = -ln(Nf/Ni) / t, where Nf is the final amount, Ni is the initial amount, and t is the time. In this case, Nf/Ni = 12.5% = 0.125 and t = 90 days. Plugging these values into the formula, we get decay constant = -ln(0.125) / 90 ≈ 0.0467/day.
