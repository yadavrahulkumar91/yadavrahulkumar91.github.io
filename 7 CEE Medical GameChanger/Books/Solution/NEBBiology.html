1. What type of development of an embryo from an unfertilized egg is called?
    1. Parthenocarpy
    2. Parthenogenesis
    3. Polyembryogeny
    4. Polycarpy
    (b) The development of an embryo from an unfertilized egg is called parthenogenesis.
  2. Which one of the following parts is not responsible for transpiration in plants?
    1. Root
    2. Stem
    3. Leaves
    4. All aerial parts
    (a) The root is not responsible for transpiration in plants, as it is not an aerial part.
  3. Which type of mutation is caused by the addition or deletion of a single nitrogenous base?
    1. Transition
    2. Transversion
    3. Frame shift
    4. Substitution
    (c) The addition or deletion of a single nitrogenous base causes frame shift mutation which leads to the insertion or deletion of amino acids in the protein sequence, thereby changing the structure and function of the protein.
  4. What basis would you apply to distinguish a meristematic tissue from a permanent tissue?
    1. Supporting plant
    2. Providing food
    3. Activating cell division
    4. Losing ability of cell division
    (c) Meristematic tissues are responsible for the growth and development of the plant and are characterized by their ability to undergo cell division and differentiate into various types of permanent tissues. Permanent tissues, on the other hand, have lost their ability to divide and are specialized for specific functions such as support, conduction, and storage.
  5. Some of the C4 plants possess diagnostic features of Kranz anatomy. Select which of the following plant contains such features?
    1. Wheat
    2. Maize
    3. Barley
    4. Oats
    (b) Maize contains diagnostic features of Kranz anatomy.
  6. Which of the following tissue lacks blood supply?
    1. Areolar
    2. Muscular
    3. Bone
    4. Cartilage
    (d) Cartilage is a connective tissue that lacks blood supply and obtains nutrients through diffusion.
  7. In frog, coelom is derived from which embryonic layer?
    1. Ectoderm
    2. Mesoderm
    3. Endoderm
    4. Mesogloea
    (b) In frog, the coelom is derived from the mesoderm embryonic layer.
  8. What kind of enzyme is needed for digestion of milk protein in human beings?
    1. Rennin
    2. Lactase
    3. Pepsin
    4. Sucrase
    (a) Rennin is the enzyme needed for digestion of milk protein in human beings.
  9. Which one of the following sets contains the bacterial disease only?
    1. Hepatitis, influenza, cholera
    2. Cholera, typhoid, candidiasis
    3. Tuberculosis, typhoid, cholera
    4. Tuberculosis, cholera, candidiasis
    (b) The set containing only bacterial diseases is Cholera, typhoid, candidiasis.
  10. If the cerebellum of a person gets injured, it will have an effect on which of the following physiological function?
    1. Respiratory ability
    2. Body movement
    3. Memory function
    4. Smelling power
    (b) If the cerebellum of a person gets injured, it will have an effect on body movement.
  11. Vas deferens transport spermatozoa from cauda epididymis to ejaculatory duct. What happens when the vas deferens of a male is surgically disconnected?
    1. Sperms become non-motile
    2. Sperms lack nuclei
    3. Seminal fluid is without sperms
    4. No spermatogenesis occurs
    (c) When the vas deferens of a male is surgically disconnected, the seminal fluid is without sperms.
