    1. a. Isogamy (Iso=same; gamy= fuse)

      1. When similar structures are present and fuse with one another, it is isogamy.
      2. Eg: Spirogyra

      When male and female gametes are produced in reproductive cells and fuse with one another, the process is called Oogamy.

    Note: Isogamy is the most primitive where genetic variation is very low, and Oogamy is the most advanced due to high genetic variation.

    Nostoc

    1. It is a member of Cyanophyceae class of order Nostocales.
    2. Generally present in water but some live on land.
      • Land:
        • Rice field
        • Coralloid root of cycas
        • Leaf of Azolla
    3. It is a filamentous algae which aggregates to form a rounded or oval-shaped colony. So, it is colonial algae.
    4. Heterocyst differs from another cell of the filament in
      • Large size
      • Presence of thick covering (Helps in Perennation)
      • Presence of Polar nodule and (Hormogonia formation and Asexual reproduction)
      • Consisting Nitrogen fixing enzyme (Helps in Nitrogen fixation)
    5. Cell structure is prokaryotic type.
    6. In the centroplasm region, circular DNA or genophore is present which consists of non-histone protein.
    7. Photosynthesis takes place at Photosynthetic lamella.
    8. ATP synthesis takes place at Structural granules.
    9. Reproduction:
      • Vegetative reproduction
        1. It is the most common method of reproduction.
        2. Takes place in favourable conditions.
        3. Takes place by hormogonium formation.
      • Asexual reproduction:
        1. Takes place in unfavourable conditions.
        2. Takes place by the formation of Akinetes.
        3. Note point: Due to the presence of a very thick covering in heterocyst, it also takes place in asexual reproduction and helps in Perennation.
      • Sexual reproduction is absent.

    Chlamydomonas

    1. Unicellular, motile, biflagellate. Flagella are developed from 'Blepharoplast' also known as 'basal granule'.
    2. Single large cup-shaped chloroplast with a pyrenoid present in it.
    3. Pyrenoid is made of protein surrounded by starch plate or starch grains and it is the center of starch formation.
    4. A tiny spot of an orange or reddish-coloured spot known as stigma or eye spot (contains carotenoids) lies at the anterior end.
    5. It is a photoreceptive organ associated with the direction of movement.
    6. Autotrophic nutrition is found in Chlamydomonas.
    7. The various modes of asexual reproduction are:
      • By zoospore:
        • zoospores are biflagellate.
      • By aplanospore
      • By hypnospores
      • By palmella stage
      • By akinetes
    8. Sexual reproduction: Gametic fusion may be isogamous, anisogamous and Oogamous i.e. sex reproduction ranging from isogamy to anisogamy and to Oogamy occurs in the same genus Chlamydomonas.
    9. Note: When a unicellular organism produces two types of gametes, then such a condition is called relative sexuality (discovered by Hertman).
    10. Life cycle in Chlamydomonas is haplontic.
    11. If the zygote formed undergoes a resting stage, it forms a thick covering and is called a Zygospore.
    12. At the time of germination, meiosis takes place in the zygospore and 4 haploid spores are developed, each giving a new thallus.

    Spirogyra

    1. Found in stagnant fresh water.
    2. Also known as
      • Pond scum
      • Pond silk
      • Water silk
      • Hair of princess
    3. It is filamentous algae which consists of cylindrical-shaped cells.
      • Due to the pectinous layer of the outer wall, the filament is slippery.
      • 1 to 16 ribbon-shaped chloroplasts are spirally coiled.
      • Inside chloroplast, many pyrenoids are found. Pyrenoid is a Protein surrounded by Starch.

      4. Reproduction

      • Most common method of reproduction in Spirogyra is Fragmentation.

        • Vegetative reproduction

        • It takes place in favourable conditions.
        • Takes place by the Fragmentation method.
        • It is the most common method of reproduction.

        Asexual reproduction

        • Takes place in unfavourable conditions (So never Zoospore).
        • It is a rare method of reproduction.
        • Takes place by the formation of Akinete or Hypnospore but never by Zoospore.

        Sexual reproduction

        • It takes place at the end of favourable conditions.
        • Isogamy
        • Scalariform Conjugation:
          • Generally in heterothallic species.
          • Between (+ve) and (-ve) filament, a conjugation tube forms by the help of Cystage enzyme.
          • Both filaments come together due to Progammone hormone.
          • Ladder-like stage is formed.
          • Male gamete from (+ve) filament passes to (-ve) filament by the help of the conjugation tube and fertilization occurs.
        • Lateral indirect conjugation
          • Takes place in homothallic species.
          • Between active and passive cell, a lateral tube forms by the help of which male gamete passes to the passive cell.
          • Note: Gametes are non-motile (no flagella), they move by the activity of the contractile vacuole.
        • Lateral direct conjugation
          • Takes place in homothallic species.
          • Between active and passive cell, membrane dissolves and male gamete directly passes to the passive cell and fertilizes.
        • Among all the above methods, when the zygote forms, then unfavourable conditions come and the zygote forms the thick covering called Zygospore (2n).
        • At the time of germination of zygospore, meiosis takes place, four haploid spores are formed, 3 degenerate and 1 develops into new Spirogyra.
        • Sexual reproduction of Spirogyra is morphologically Isogamy, but physiologically anisogamy.
