A. Chemical Equation of Respiration

a. Aerobic Respiration (Complete Oxidation)

\(C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 \rightarrow 6CO_2 + 6H_2O + 36/38 \, ATP\)

b. Complete Oxidation of Glucose in Aerobic Respiration

\(C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 + 10H_2O \rightarrow 6CO_2 + 16H_2O + 36/38 \, ATP \, (686 \, kCal / 673 \, kCal \, earlier)\)

  1. 10 molecules of \(H_2O\) are used (2 in glycolysis and 8 in Kreb's cycle), while 16 molecules of \(H_2O\) are produced in one complete cycle of respiration.

  2. 1 ATP gives 8.15 kCal (A/C new concept). Hence efficiency of respiration in aerobes is

    \(\eta = \frac{{38 \times 8.15 \, \text{kCal}}}{{686 \, \text{kCal}}} \times 100\% = 45.14\%\)

Note: Previously 1 ATP = 7.3 kCal

F. Respiratory Quotient

a. Definition of Respiratory Quotient

Respiratory quotient is defined as the ratio of the volume of \(CO_2\) evolved to \(O_2\) consumed in respiration.

\(R.Q. = \frac{{\text{Volume of } CO_2 \text{ evolved}}}{{\text{Volume of } O_2 \text{ consumed}}}\)

b. Measurement of Respiratory Quotient

Respiratory quotient is measured by Ganong’s respirometer.

c. Respiratory Quotient and Respiratory Substrates

Respiratory quotient depends on the respiratory substrate being used.

  1. RQ = 1 for carbohydrates. [Floating respiration]

  2. RQ < 1 for lipids, fats (0.7), and proteins (0.8-0.9). [Protoplasmic respiration]

  3. RQ > 1 for organic acids like:

    • Malic acid (\(COOH\))
    • Oxalic acid

  4. RQ = 0 for succulent plants like:

    • Bryophyllum
    • Opuntia
    • Agave
    • Aloe

  5. RQ = ∞ for anaerobic respiration because \(O_2\) is not used. Example: Yeast
