Pteridophyta

Pteridophyta is the first vascular land plant.

Vascular Bundle

  • Due to the absence of Cambium, secondary growth is absent except in Isoetes.
  • Phloem is without companion cells.
  • Sieve tube is negative and sieve cell is positive.

Main Plant Body

The main plant body is diploid and sporophytic (both homosporous and heterosporous).

Habitat

It is found on land except:

  • Azolla
    • Smallest pteridophyta
    • Leaf is used as a biofertilizer
  • Marsellia
  • Salvinia
    • Salvinia is a rootless pteridophyta.

Pteridophyta Characteristics

Pteridophyta is known as the "Reptiles of the plant kingdom" or "Botanical snakes".

Size

Most pteridophytes are small-shaped herb-like, except:

  • Alsophylla - The largest/tallest pteridophyta
  • Cythea - Tree fern

Plant Structure

  • Root
    • Root is generally adventitious type.
  • Stem
    • Underground Rhizome.
    • Rhizomorph = Root-like structure that helps in absorption found in Fungi.
    • Rhizines = Lichen
    • Rhizoid = Root-like structure found in Bryophyta.
    • Rhizophore = Axis present between root and stem, found in Selaginella.
      • Rhizophore is an organ of Suigeneris.
  • Leaf
    • Tropophyll = Photosynthetic leaf
    • Sporophyll = Spore-producing leaf
      • Sporophyll contains Sporangium.
      • Leptosporangiate - Sporangium develops from a single-cell initial.
      • Eusporangiate - Sporangium develops from a group of cells.

Life Cycle of Fern (Dryopteris)

Plant Characteristics

  • Dryopteris is a member of the Pteropsida group of Pteridophyta and is called a Fern.
  • The plant is found in shaded areas (Sciophytic condition).
  • The main plant body is diploid and sporophytic (homosporous).

Plant Structure

  • Root
    • Root is of adventitious type.
  • Stem
    • Stem is underground and dichotomously branched, known as Rhizome.
    • Stele is Dictyostele.
  • Leaf
    • Leaf is large-sized, feather-like pinnately compound type, called Fronds.
    • Brown-colored hair-like structures (brownish scaly leaf) present on rhizome and rachis, called Ramenta.
    • Young leaf shows twisting known as "Circinate vernation" or "Circinate ptyxis".

Reproduction

Vegetative Reproduction

  • Fragmentation
  • Apogamy
    • Apogamous sporophyte of fern is haploid.

Sexual Reproduction

Plant is sporophytic. Sporangia are produced on leaves.

  • Sporangia are found in groups called Sori.
  • Sorus is protected by a kidney-shaped outgrowth of placenta called Indusium.
    • In the case of Pteris, Coenosporus is present, protected by a false inducium.
  • Inside the leptosporangiate sporangium, homosporous spores are found.
  • Sporangium wall breaks from the stomium region due to the annulus.
  • When spores come out, they germinate to produce a heart-shaped prothallus.
    • Prothallus is green in color except for Rhizoids, which are brown.
    • Prothallus is a monoecious gametophyte.
    • Antheridium and Archegonium:
      • Near the rhizoid, a dome-shaped antheridium is present, producing multi-flagellated and coiled male gametes.
      • Just below the apical notch, a flask-shaped archegonium is present with a bent neck.
      • Protandrous condition is found.
      • Chemotactic movement of male gametes is due to malic acid.
      • Archegonium consists of 1 egg cell, 1 Venter canal cell (VCC), and 1 binucleate Neck canal cell (NCC).
      • Antheridium and Archegonium Characteristics
      Antheridium Archegonium
      1. Near rhizoid, dome shaped antheridium is present which produces multi-flagellated and coiled male gamete. 1. Just below the apical notch, flask shaped archegonium is present with bent neck.
