Growth

  1. Sequential process involved in growth:
    Cell division → Cell enlargement → Cell differentiation
  2. Maximum cell elongation occurs in the region behind the root/shoot.
  3. Growth is a Quantitative change.
    1. But, Development is Qualitative + Quantitative
  4. Growth is primarily affected by two climatic factors which are light and temperature.
  5. During growth, there is an increase in dry weight, permanent change in size, volume, number of cells.
  6. Growth curve → 'S' shaped sigmoid curve.
    1. Plant growth
    2. Bacterial growth in lab
  7. Growth is measured by Auxanometer, Horizontal microscope, Crescograph.
  8. The classical experiments in growth were performed by Boysen-Jensen and Darwin.

Phases of Growth = 4

  1. Lag phase → Initiating phase, Slow cell division.
  2. Log phase (exponential) → Maximum growth, Fast cell division.
  3. Deaccelerating phase → Decreased in growth rate.
  4. Stationary phase → Constant growth

Functions and Examples of Plant Hormones (Part 2)

I. ABA (Abscisic acid)/ Abscisin II/ Dormin/ Dormant/ Stress hormone - Bud dormancy and resting stage Ethylene - Senescence/ Ageing
  1. ABA is mildly acidic hormone formed from Xanthophyll and Mevalonic acid.
  2. Produced in Mature stem, Leaf, Fruit and Seed.
  3. Most abundantly formed in chloroplast and are transported through all the parts of the plants by diffusion. It can be produced from Isolated chloroplast.
  4. Produced at root and shoot meristem.
  1. Synthesized from amino acid Methionine (precursor molecule). It is the only Gaseous hormone.
  2. Synthesis is promoted by O2 and Inhibited by CO2.
  3. Synthesized in large amount by tissues undergoing senescence and ripening fruits.
  4. Ethylene is connected with Climacteric respiration.

Functions:

  1. ABA is a natural growth inhibitor.
  2. Hastens the process of senescence in leaves and leads to falling of leaves (Abscission).
  3. Maintains dormancy of buds, seeds.
  4. Causes closing of stomata, so also called Anti-transpirant.
  5. It inhibits the conversion of starch to sugar by blocking the action of Gibberellins.
  6. Protect plants from frost, injury and desiccation.
  7. Induces formation of adventitious roots.
  8. Breaks dormancy of different plant organs but not of lateral buds.

Functions:

  1. Ripening and Dehiscence (Etiolation) of fruits.
  2. Ethylene generally inhibits flowering but in case of pineapple and litchi, it causes flowering.
  3. Induces Female flower in place of male flower in monoecious plant (Cucumber).
  4. It promotes abscission of plant parts.
  5. Induces epinasty or downward bending of leaves.
  6. Causes senescence (prevented by Cytokinin).
  7. Promotes transverse growth of stem.
