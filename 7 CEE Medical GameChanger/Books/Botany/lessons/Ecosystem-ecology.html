Ecology Terms and Concepts

Ecology Terms

  • Term Ecology given by:
    • i. Ernst Haeckel: Defined ecology as reciprocal relationship between living organisms and their environment.
    • ii. Reiter: First authentic use.
  • Father of Ecology = Reiter
  • Term Ecosystem = Tansley
  • Father of Ecosystem = Odum

Branches of Ecology

  • I. Autecology = Individual species (Population and its habitat)
  • II. Syncology = Community

Speciation

Type of Isolation Species Location Developed Character
Allopatry/ Geographical isolation Individuals of same species On different geographical location (with or without barrier) Different morphological characters
Sympatry/ Reproductive isolation Individuals of same species On same geographical location Different morphological characters

Ecology Terms

  • I. Ecotone:
    • Transition zone between two communities.
    • Examples:
      • Estuary = Ecotone of freshwater and saline water.
  • II. Ecesis: Establishment of a plant in a new area during secondary succession.
  • III. Ecotype:
    • Genetically different species living in a similar environment, developed similar characteristics.
  • IV. Ecads/Ecophene:
    • Genetically similar individuals living in different environments developed different characteristics.
  • V. Niche:
    • Term given by Grinnel.
    • Habit and function of every organism.
    • High competition.
  • VI. Keystone species:
    • Most influencing species of an ecosystem.
    • They determine the properties of the ecosystem.
    • Examples:
      • Kangaroo in Tiaga ecosystem.
      • Reptiles in Desert ecosystem.
  • VII. Noosphere:
    • Sphere dominated by human mind.
    • Examples:
      • Greenhouse.
      • Culture media.

Ecological Laws

  • I. Allen's law:
    • Tail, snout, ears, and legs of mammals living in colder regions are shorter.
  • II. Bergman's law:
    • Mammals of colder regions are larger.
  • III. Jordan's law:
    • Fishes of cold water have more vertebrae.

Factors Determining Distribution of Organisms (Ecological Factors)

  • I. Light:
    • Heliophytes = Sun-loving plants.
    • Sciophytes = Shade-loving plants.
    • Facultative heliophytes = Sciophytes that can grow in sunlight.
    • Facultative sciophytes = Heliophytes that can grow in shade too.
  • II. Temperature:
    • Eurythermal = Can tolerate wide temperature range.
    • Sternothermal = Can tolerate narrow temperature range.
    • Megatherms = High temperature (e.g., Desert, Tropical rainforest).
    • Mesotherms = High summer, low winter temperature (e.g., Temperate vegetation, Pond flora).
    • Microtherms = Low temperature (e.g., Conifer vegetation).
    • Hekistotherms = Extremely low temperature (e.g., Tundra vegetation).
  • III. Wind
  • IV. Humidity:
    • Relative humidity measured by Psychrometer.
  • V. Rainfall

Xerophytes

  • I. Adaptation:
    • Roots:
      • Highly developed root system with root hairs.
      • Well-developed vascular system.
    • Leaf:
      • Reduced size.
      • Sunken stomata.
      • Multilayered leaf epidermis, e.g., Ficus, Nerium.
    • Stem:
      • Reduced as Phylloclade and Cladode.
      • May store water and become succulent.
      • Water-storing parenchyma well developed.
  • II. Types:
    • On the basis of water availability and soil nature:
      • Physical xerophytes = Dry soil.
      • Physiological xerophytes = Saline soil or very low temperature.
    • On the basis of life cycle and water storage:
      • Ephemerals/Drought escapers/Drought evaders:
        • Short-lived, only during rain.
      • Succulents/Fleshy xerophytes = Drought avoiding/Drought resistant:
        • Root succulents
        • Stem succulents
        • Leaf succulents
      • Non-succulents = True xerophytes/Drought tolerant:
        • Can withstand long drought periods.

    Soil Profile

    • I. Nature and sequence of different layers of horizons in a vertical section of soil is called soil profile:
      • AOO horizon / Litter region:
        • Undecomposed organic matter.
      • AO horizon / Duff region:
        • Partially decomposed organic matter.
      • A1 horizon / Humus layer:
        • Fully decomposed organic matter.
      • A2 horizon:
        • Mineral particles along with little humus.
      • B horizon:
        • Only mineral particles.
      • C horizon:
        • Incompletely broken mineral particles.
      • R horizon:
        • Unbroken rocks.
