Study of Bryophta

  1. Study of Bryophta is called Bryology.
  2. History
    1. Father of Bryology = Hertwig
    2. Term Bryophta = Robert Braun
  3. First land plant but generally Sciophytic except
    1. Aquatics
      1. Riccia fluitans
      2. Ricciocarpus natans
      3. Riella
      4. Fontinalis antipyretica
    2. Semi-aquatic
      1. Sphagnum
  4. Known as Amphibian of plant kingdom because need water for fertilization.
  5. Chloroplast is without pyrenoids except Anthoceros.
    1. Pyrenoid is present in Anthoceros.
    2. Anthoceros is the most primitive bryophyta.
  6. The organ for attachment is Rhizoid and Scales.
    1. Rhizoid
      1. Unicellular
      2. Smooth-walled
      3. Tuberculated wall
    2. Scales
      1. Multicellular
      2. Ligulate
      3. Appendiculate
        1. Tongue-like
        2. Appendages-like
  7. General height of Bryophyte is 15 cm to 20 cm.
    1. Zoopsis is 0.5 cm to 5 cm.
      1. It is the smallest Bryophta.
    2. Dawsonia is 65 cm.
      1. It is the Largest bryophta.
    3. Fontinalis is the Longest bryophta.
  8. Reproduction
    1. Either by Vegetative or Sexual method.
    2. Vegetative reproduction
      1. By fragmentation
      2. By death and decay of older part
        1. In this method, when the older part becomes dead, then two younger parts separate and develop into new plants.
      3. By Gemma:
        1. On the dorsal surface of the plant, Gemma cup is produced. Inside the gemma cup, gemma is formed. Each gemma develops into 2 plants.
      4. Apospory
        1. Formation of Gametophyte directly from the tissue of sporophyte without the formation of Spore.
      5. Apogamy
        1. Formation of Sporophyte directly from the tissue of gametophyte without the formation of gamete.
      6. Parthenogenesis
        1. When gamete directly develops into sporophyte or fruit without fertilization.
        2. Note: Parthenocarpy is used for fruit for making seedless fruit.
      7. By Bulbil formation
        1. At the base of the plant, near rhizoids rounded structure is formed called bulbils, when separated, it develops into a new plant.
    3. Sexual reproduction
      1. Male and female reproductive organ is multicellular and jacketed called Antheridium and Archegonium.
      4. Antheridium Archegonium
      1. Antheridium produces biflagellated curved male gametes.
      2. Movement of male gamete is chemotactic.
      3. Fertilization is internal.
      4. Zygote develops into embryo which differentiates into sporophyte.      		 1. Archegonium is flask-shaped and consists of:
      - Egg cell,
      - Venter Canal Cell (VCC), and
      - Neck Canal Cell (NCC).
      2. Fertilization is internal.
      3. Embryo is protected by Venter wall called as Calyptra(n).

Marchantia

    1. Marchantia is a member of the hepaticopsida group of bryophyta and is called a Liverwort.
  2. The plant is found in Sciophytic condition.
  3. The plant is dichotomously branched and prostrate.
    1. On the ventral surface,
      1. Unicellular rhizoids (Both smooth-walled and Tuberculated)
        1. Smooth-walled
        2. Tuberculated wall
      2. Multicellular scales are found.
    2. On the dorsal surface, black spots (air pores) are present throughout the life cycle.
  4. But Gemma cup is produced during the reproductive season.
  5. The lower part is a storage part, which is compact and made up of parenchymatous tissue.
  6. The upper part is photosynthetic, consisting of smaller rows of chlorophyllous cells and air pores.
  7. Reproduction
    1. Vegetative Reproduction
      1. Fragmentation
      2. By death and decay of older parts
      3. By gemma:
        1. Each Gemma develops into two plants.
    2. Sexual reproduction (Dioecious)
      1. Male plant
        1. Consist of Antheriophore on which disc/plate-shaped male receptacle is present.
        2. Male receptacle consists of Antheridium which produces biflagellated and Curved male gametes.
      2. Female plant
        1. Consist of Archegoniophore on which Umbrella/Star-shaped female receptacle is present.
        2. Female receptacle consists of
          1. 1 Egg cell
          2. 1 Venter canal cell (VCC)
          3. 4-6 Neck canal cell (NCC)
      3. Chemotactic movement of male gametes is due to K+ and Protein.
      4. Fertilization is internal.
        1. Zygote gives Embryo which differentiate into Sporophyte.
    3. Sporophyte of Marchantia
      1. Inside the capsule, haploid spore and diploid elaters are found.
      2. Elater is hygroscopic, spindle-shaped which takes part in spore dispersal.
      3. When spore comes out then germinate to produce a new plant of Marchantia.

Funaria

    1. Funaria is a member of Bryopsida and is a true moss.
  2. The plant is found in Sciophytic condition.
  3. The plant is dimorphic.
    1. Protonema Adult plant
      The young stage is Protonema, which is haploid and filamentous. Adult plant is erect and differentiated into
      1. Rhizoid → Root-like
        1. Rhizoid is multicellular and branched.
      2. Axis and → Stem-like
        1. Axis consists of epidermis (consisting of Protoplast) and central cylinder.
      3. Phylloid → Leaf-like
        1. Phylloid consists of midrib and wing.
      1. In Axis and Phylloid, stomata is absent.
      2. Stomata is present only in Capsule.
        1. Chlorophyll is also present in the Capsule.
  4. Reproduction
    1. Vegetative reproduction:
      1. Fragmentation
      2. Bulbil formation
      3. Apospory
      4. Protonema
    2. Sexual reproduction
      1. Male plant
        1. Antheridium is protected by Perigonial leaf.
        2. Both antheridium and archegonium are protected by Paraphysis.
        3. Male axis consists of antheridium which produces biflagellated and curved male gametes.
      2. Female plant
        1. Archegonium is flask-shaped and consists of
          1. 1 Egg cell,
          2. 1 Venter Canal cell (VCC),
          3. 6-10 Neck Canal Cell (NCC)
      3. Chemotactic movement of male gamete is due to Sucrose or Cane sugar.
      4. Fertilization is internal.
      5. Zygote → Embryo which develops into sporophyte. Main part of sporophyte is Capsule.
      6. Capsule
        1. Uppermost part of Capsule is Operculum.
        2. Lowermost part of Capsule is Apophysis
          1. (consists of stomata and chloroplast).
        3. Central sterile part is Columella
          1. (helps in conduction).
        4. Uppermost part of theca is Annulus.
          1. Annulus helps in dehiscence of capsule.
          2. Annulus helps in spore dispersal.
        5. The number of peristome teeth is 32 which is arranged in 2 rows i.e., 16+16.
          1. Peristome teeth are hygroscopic in nature and take part in spore dispersal.
        6. When spores come out then germinate to produce protonema (Juvenile stage) and develop into an adult gametophytic plant.
          1. Foot → Attachment of Sporophyte to Gametophyte
          2. Seta → Dispersal of Spores
          3. Capsule → Holds the capsule
