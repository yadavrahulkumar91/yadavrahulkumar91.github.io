1. 1. Response to touch or friction is known as:
    1. Nyctinasty
    2. Thigmotropism
    3. Phototropism
    4. None
    (b) Response to touch or friction is known as thigmotropism.
  2. 2. Plant organs can detect a change in their orientation with respect to gravity:
    1. Within a few seconds
    2. At least a minimum of thirty minutes
    3. At least they have to be kept for a day
    4. At least they have to be kept for a week
    (b) Plant organs can detect a change in their orientation with respect to gravity at least a minimum of thirty minutes.
  3. 3. Both Nyctinasty and Thigmonasty are observed in:
    1. Drosera
    2. Mimosa
    3. Utricularia
    4. Cuscuta
    (b) Both Nyctinasty and Thigmonasty are observed in Mimosa.
  4. 4. Bending of stem towards light is:
    1. Photoperiodism
    2. Heliotropism
    3. Photonasty
    4. Hydrotropism
    (b) Bending of stem towards light is heliotropism.
  5. 5. Jerky lateral leaflet movements of Desmodium gyrans are:
    1. Negative geotropic movements
    2. Positive geotropic movements
    3. Hydrotropic movements
    4. None of the above
    (a) Jerky lateral leaflet movements of Desmodium gyrans are negative geotropic movements.
  6. 6. Which movement occurs due to external stimulus?
    1. Tropic
    2. Nastic
    3. Tactic
    4. All
    (d) The movement that occurs due to external stimulus includes tropic, nastic, and tactic movements.
  7. 7. Coiling of tendrils around base is called:
    1. Hydrotropism
    2. Geotropism
    3. Chemotropism
    4. Haptotropism
    (d) Coiling of tendrils around the base is called haptotropism.
  8. 8. Klinostat is used in studies on:
    1. Photosynthesis
    2. Respiration
    3. Osmosis
    4. Growth movement
    (d) Klinostat is used in studies on growth movement.
  9. 9. The leaves of Mimosa pudica drop down when touched due to:
    1. Thigmotropism
    2. Seismonasty
    3. Nyctinasty
    4. Chemonasty
    (b) The leaves of Mimosa pudica drop down when touched due to seismonasty.
  10. 10. Attraction of antherozoids towards the archegonium under the influence of chemicals is termed as:
    1. Chemotropism
    2. Chemotaxis
    3. Hydrotropism
    4. Phototropism
    (b) The attraction of antherozoids towards the archegonium under the influence of chemicals is termed as chemotaxis.
  11. Scototropic movements are induced by:
    1. Heat
    2. Light
    3. Touch
    4. Night
    (b) Scototropic movements are induced by Light.
  12. Sensation of phototropism is perceived by:
    1. Root tip
    2. Shoot tip
    3. Axillary bud
    4. Node
    (b) Sensation of phototropism is perceived by the Shoot tip.
  13. Opening of flowers involves:
    1. Epinasty
    2. Hyponasty
    3. Nutation
    4. Phototaxis
    (d) Opening of flowers involves Phototaxis.
  14. Geotropic response is perceived by:
    1. Mature roots
    2. Elongated cells
    3. Root cap
    4. Root hairs
    (c) Geotropic response is perceived by the Root cap.
  15. Rhizome is:
    1. Diageotropic
    2. Plageotropic
    3. Negatively geotropic
    4. Positively geotropic
    (d) Rhizome is Positively geotropic.
  16. Pollen tube exhibits:
    1. Haptotropism
    2. Chemotropism
    3. Anemotropism
    4. Heliotropism
    (b) Pollen tube exhibits Chemotropism.
  17. Peristome teeth of moss shows:
    1. Hydrochasy
    2. Xerochasy
    3. Hydrotropism
    4. Chemotropism
    (b) Peristome teeth of moss shows Xerochasy.
  18. An apparatus commonly used to demonstrate phototropism is:
    1. Heliotropic chamber
    2. Clinostat
    3. Arc auxanometer
    4. Potometer
    (a) An apparatus commonly used to demonstrate phototropism is a Heliotropic chamber.
  19. Circinate ptyxis present in fern leaves is due to:
    1. Epinasty
    2. Hyponasty
    3. Nutation
    4. Movement of variation
    (c) Circinate ptyxis present in fern leaves is due to Nutation.
  20. 21. Desmodium gyrans shows:
    1. Autonomic movement of growth
    2. Paratonic movement of growth
    3. Autonomic movement of variation
    4. Paratonic movement of variation
    (a) Desmodium gyrans shows autonomic movement of growth.
  21. 22. Movement of the peristomial teeth of moss is:
    1. Movement of locomotion
    2. Hygroscopic movement
    3. Hydrotropic movement
    4. None of the above
    (b) The movement of the peristomial teeth of moss is hygroscopic movement.
  22. 23. What type of movement is exhibited by the telegraph plant (Desmodium gyrans)?
    1. Hydrotropic movement
    2. Autonomic curvature movement of variation
    3. Geotropic movement
    4. Negatively geotropic movement
    (b) The telegraph plant (Desmodium gyrans) exhibits autonomic curvature movement of variation.
  23. 24. Desmodium gyrans, which shows autonomous movement of variation, belongs to:
    1. Cruciferae
    2. Malvaceae
    3. Asteraceae
    4. Papilionaceae
    (d) Desmodium gyrans, which shows autonomous movement of variation, belongs to Papilionaceae.
  24. 25. Sensitive plant (Touch-me-not plant), which shows paratonic, seismonastic movement, belongs to:
    1. Papilionaceae
    2. Caesalpiniodeae
    3. Mimosoideae
    4. Solanaceae
    (c) The sensitive plant (Touch-me-not plant), which shows paratonic, seismonastic movement, belongs to Mimosoideae.
  25. 26. Movement of a plant organ at a right angle to the influence of gravity is called:
    1. Phototropism
    2. Photonastism
    3. Plageotropism
    4. Diageotropism
    (b) The movement of a plant organ at a right angle to the influence of gravity is called photonastism.
  26. 27. The stimulus in Mimosa pudica travels in the form of:
    1. Auxin
    2. Hormone
    3. Alcohol
    4. Turgor changes
    (d) The stimulus in Mimosa pudica travels in the form of turgor changes.
