    3. (c) 3

  2. 2. The antherozoids in Cycas are:
    1. (a) Uniflagellated

    1. (a) Cenozoic era

    (b) Higher gymnosperms were predominant in the Mesozoic era.

    4. (d) Absence of vessels in xylem
    (d) Absence of vessels in xylem is not true for Cycas.
  11. In the seeds of gymnosperms, the gametophytic generation is represented by:
    1. (a) Perisperm
    2. (b) Testa
    3. (c) Embryo
    4. (d) Endosperm
    (c) The gametophytic generation in gymnosperms is represented by the embryo.
  12. The largest ovules in the plant world are found in:

    2. (b) Pinus
    3. (c) Cycas
    4. (d) Banyan
    (b) The largest ovules in the plant world are found in Pinus.
  13. Pollen grains are able to withstand extreme temperature and desiccation because their exine is composed of:
    1. (a) Cutin
    2. (b) Suberin
    3. (c) Sporopollenin
    4. (d) Callose
    (c) Pollen grains are able to withstand extreme temperature and desiccation because their exine is composed of sporopollenin.
  14. Wood of Cycas is:
    1. (a) Ring porous
    2. (b) Diffuse porous
    3. (c) Polyxylic
    4. (d) Pycnoxylic
    (c) The wood of Cycas is polyxylic.
  15. In a gymnosperm, the endosperm:
    1. (a) Develops without fertilization
    2. (b) Forms after fertilization but does not require triple fusion
    3. (c) Is diploid
    4. (d) Is short-lived
    (b) In a gymnosperm, the endosperm forms after fertilization but does not require triple fusion.
  16. Cycas undergo hypogeal germination and has 2 cotyledons but is not considered as a dicot plant because:
    1. (a) It is dioecious
    2. (b) Companion cells are absent in phloem
    3. (c) It has naked seeds
    4. (d) Vegetative propagation is rare
    (c) Cycas is not considered a dicot plant because it has naked seeds.
  17. Gymnosperms differ from angiosperms in:
    1. (a) Absence of seed
    2. (b) Absence of ovary
    3. (c) Absence of companion cells
    4. (d) Absence of sieve cells
    (b) Gymnosperms differ from angiosperms in the absence of an ovary.
  18. Turpentine oil is extracted from:
    1. (a) Oak
    2. (b) Pine
    3. (c) Deodar
    4. (d) Cycas
    (b) Turpentine oil is extracted from pine.
  19. Gymnosperms are regarded as naked-seeded plants because they lack:
    1. (a) Ovule
    2. (b) Ovary
    3. (c) Seed coat
    4. (d) Sporophylls
    (c) Gymnosperms are regarded as naked-seeded plants because they lack a seed coat.
  20. Angiosperms are distinguished from gymnosperms by:
    1. (a) Secondary growth
    2. (b) Primary growth
    3. (c) Presence of enclosed ovule

    (c) Angiosperms are distinguished from gymnosperms by the presence of an enclosed ovule.
