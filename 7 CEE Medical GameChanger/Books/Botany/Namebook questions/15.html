1. 1. All are caulimo virus except:
    1. Cauliflower mosaic virus
    2. Dahlia mosaic virus
    3. Bacteriophage virus
    4. Rhabdo virus
    (c) Bacteriophage virus is not a caulimo virus.
  2. 2. Obligate parasites:
    1. Cannot multiply outside living cell
    2. Can multiply outside living cell
    3. Can multiply in soil
    4. Can multiply on dead organic matter
    (a) Obligate parasites cannot multiply outside living cells.
  3. 3. Bacteriophage is:
    1. Bacteria
    2. Fungi
    3. Virus
    4. Algae
    (c) Bacteriophage is a type of virus.
  4. 4. TMV was isolated in crystal form by:
    1. Watson and Crick
    2. Morgan
    3. W.M. Stanely
    4. None
    (c) TMV (Tobacco mosaic virus) was isolated in crystal form by W.M. Stanley.
  5. 5. The disease influenza is:
    1. Bacterial
    2. Fungal
    3. Algal
    4. Viral
    (d) The disease influenza is caused by a virus.
  6. 6. Viruses which multiply without killing the host:
    1. Virion
    2. Lytic
    3. Retrovirus
    4. Temperate
    (d) Viruses that multiply without killing the host are called temperate viruses.
  7. 7. Retrovirus and reverse transcriptase were discovered by:
    1. Sinsheimer
    2. Stanley
    3. Temin and Baltimore
    4. Chase
    (c) Retrovirus and reverse transcriptase were discovered by Temin and Baltimore.
  8. 8. Virus cannot survive outside the host because:
    1. Lack of metabolic activity
    2. Separation in two parts
    3. Having a thick shielding proteinaceous coat
    4. Due to oxidation
    (a) Viruses cannot survive outside the host due to a lack of metabolic activity.
  9. 9. A clear zone formed in a bacterial colony due to viral action is:
    1. Plague
    2. Phage
    3. Streak
    4. Kranz
    (b) A clear zone formed in a bacterial colony due to viral action is called phage.
  10. 10. The organism causing SARS has undergone changes from its original form. The actual virus name and the process likely to have caused this change include:
    1. Corona - translocation
    2. Corona - crossing over
    3. Adeno - translocation
    4. Adeno - crossing over
    (a) The virus causing SARS is a coronavirus, and the process likely to have caused the change is translocation.
  11. Which of the following is impossible in the absence of a virus?
    1. (a) Conjugation
    2. (b) Translation
    3. (c) Transformation
    4. (d) Transduction
    (c) Transformation is impossible in the absence of a virus.
  12. TMV (Tobacco Mosaic Virus) genes are:
    1. (a) Single stranded RNA
    2. (b) Double stranded RNA
    3. (c) Single stranded DNA
    4. (d) Double stranded DNA
    (a) TMV (Tobacco Mosaic Virus) genes are Single stranded RNA.
  13. Bacteriophages are:
    1. (a) Bacteria attacking algae
    2. (b) Virus attacking fungi
    3. (c) Bacteria attacking virus
    4. (d) Virus attacking bacteria
    (d) Bacteriophages are viruses that attack bacteria.
  14. Virus is composed of:
    1. (a) Protein and nucleic acid
    2. (b) Protein and starch
    3. (c) Protein, RNA and DNA
    4. (d) Protein, starch and carbohydrate
    (a) Virus is composed of protein and nucleic acid.
  15. Influenza virus is:
    1. (a) 2 RNA
    2. (b) 2 DNA
    3. (c) 1 RNA
    4. (d) 1 DNA
    (c) Influenza virus is composed of 1 RNA.
  16. Plant virus generally contains:
    1. (a) RNA
    2. (b) DNA
    3. (c) DNA and RNA
    4. (d) Only proteins
    (a) Plant viruses generally contain RNA.
  17. The protein coat of a virus is called:
    1. (a) Capsid
    2. (b) Capsule
    3. (c) Capsomere
    4. (d) Fiber
    (a) The protein coat of a virus is called a capsid.
  18. The head of a plant virus possesses:
    1. (a) DNA
    2. (b) RNA
    3. (c) Protein
    4. (d) Lipid
    (c) The head of a plant virus possesses proteins.
