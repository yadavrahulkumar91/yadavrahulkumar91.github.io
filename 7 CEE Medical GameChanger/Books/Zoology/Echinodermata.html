PHYLUM ECHINODERMATA

General Features

  1. Triploblastic
  2. Radially Symmetrical (Adult), Often Pentamerous
  3. Larva Bilaterally Symmetrical; Sessile or Creeping
  4. Radially Arranged Parts: Antimeres
  5. Deuterostomes with Complete Digestive Tract (Except Brittle Star)
  6. Enterocoelic Coelomate
  7. Organ Grade of Organization
  8. Unsegmented Body

Habitat

  1. Gregarious

Habitat

  1. Echinoderms are Exclusively Marine
  2. All Echinoderms are Benthic (Except Few Pelagic Sea Cucumbers)
  3. Benthic: Bottom Dwellers
  4. Pelagic: Living on Surface of Water
  5. Mostly Free-Living (Except Sea Lilies)

Special Features

  1. Spiny Skinned
  2. Echinoderms are Uncephalized (Head Absent)
  3. Body Parts Organized around Oral-aboral Axis
  4. Endoskeleton Composed of Calcareous Ossicles with Spines
  5. Mesodermal Endoskeleton
  6. Spines for Protection
  7. Pedicellariae: Pincer-like Structures Preventing Foreign Particles
  8. Sea Urchin's Gut with Masticating Organ "Aristotle's Lantern"
  9. Complex Coelom: Water Vascular System, Perivisceral Coelom, Haemal System

Nervous System

  1. Diffused, Decentralized Nervous System
  2. Nerve Net
  3. Nerve Ring
  4. Radial Nerves
  5. No Brain
  6. Poorly Developed Sense Organs
  7. Tactile Tentacles
  8. Pigment Eye-spots
  9. Statocysts: Balance and Orientation

Circulatory System

  1. Open Circulatory System
  2. Heart Absent
  3. Blood Lacks Respiratory Pigment

Excretory System

  1. Excretory System Absent

Reproductive System

  1. Mostly Dioecious
  2. No Copulation
  3. External Fertilization
  4. Eggs: Homolecithal (Uniformly Distributed Yolk)
  5. Development: Indirect through Free Swimming Larval Stages
