Characteristics of Annelida

  1. The phylum 'Annelida' comprises the segmented worms.
  2. Annelidas are
    1. Bilaterally symmetrical
    2. Triploblastic
    3. Eucoelomate (With true coelom)
      1. Coelom appeared for the first time in the course of evolution
    4. Schizocoelic (Segmented worms)
    5. Body worm-like
    6. Metamerically segmented
      1. The formation of new segments in a metamerically segmented animal always takes place in the posterior part of the body, just in front of pygidium, the last segments.
      2. The segmentation in annelids is homonomous (all equal segments)
    7. With large longitudinal ventral nerve cord
    8. Tube within a tube body plan
      1. Protostomic
    9. Organ-system level of organisation

Annelida Anatomy and Physiology

  1. Alimentary canal is straight.
  2. Excretory organs = Nephridia
    1. Nephridia is ectodermal i.e., arises from ectodermal embyronic nephroblastic cell
  3. Anus is the last body segment.
    1. So a post anal tail is absent.
  4. Cuticle = Non-chitinous and albuminoid
  5. Circulatory system = closed type
    1. 1st appearance of heart
    2. RBC absent but presence of respiratory pigment haemoglobin.

Comparative Features and Examples

  1. Phylum Annelida resembles mollusca in embryonic features because both have identical conspicuous segmentation in body muscles and nervous system.
  2. 1st appearance of digestive gland.
