HEMICHORDATA

Habitat

  1. Exclusively Marine

Habit

  1. Solitary or Colonial

Body Structure

  1. Mostly Tubiculous
  2. Soft and Fragile Body
  3. Vermiform

General Features

  1. Unsegmented
  2. Bilaterally Symmetrical
  3. Triploblastic
  4. Eucoelomate
  5. Enterocoelous Coelom
  6. Divided into Three Parts Corresponding to Body Regions:
  7. Protocoel: Proboscis
  8. Mesocoel: Collar
  9. Metacoel: Trunk

Body Wall

  1. Epidermis
  2. Single-Layered with Mucous Glands
  3. Dermis
  4. Absent

Digestion

  1. Complete Digestive Tube
  2. Straight or U-Shaped
  3. Foregut Gives Hollow Buccal Diverticulum into Proboscis (Earlier Considered as "Notochord")

Gill Slits

  1. Dorso-Lateral Pharyngeal Gill Slits
  2. Shared Character with Chordata
  3. 1 to Several Pairs
  4. Ciliary Filter Feeders

Circulation System

  1. Simple and Open
  2. Dorsal Heart
  3. Two Longitudinal Vessels
  4. Dorsal and Ventral

Excretion

  1. Excretory Organ: Proboscis Gland or Glomerulus
  2. Connected to Blood Vessels

Nervous System

  1. Primitive Nervous System
  2. Sub Epineural Nerve Plexus
  3. Dorsal Collar Nerve Cord: Hollow

Reproduction

  1. Mainly Sexual
  2. Separate Sexes
  3. One to Several Pairs of Gonads
  4. External Fertilization in Water
  5. Development Direct or Indirect with Free-Swimming Tornaria Larva

Notochord

  1. Notochord Present Only in Anterior Part of Proboscis
  2. Notochord Not Homologous to Other Chordates' Notochord
  3. Called 'Stomatochord'

Classification

  1. Four Classes:
  2. Enteropneusta
  3. Balanoglossus (Acorn Worm/Tongue Worm)
  4. Saccoglossus
  5. Protoglossus
  6. Ptychodera
  7. Spengelia
  8. Pterobranchia
  9. Cephalodiscus
  10. Atubaria
  11. Planctosphaeroidea
  12. Planctosphaera
  13. Graptolita
  14. Dendrographus

Balanoglossus (Acorn Worm or Tongue Worm)

Habitat

  1. Marine animal with worldwide distribution.
  2. Found at the bottom of shallow water, with some occurring in deeper water.

Habit and Body Shape

  1. Tubiculous burrowing animal
  2. Worm-like with soft, elongated cylindrical body
  3. Length ranges from 10 cm to 2.5 m

General Features

  1. Bilaterally symmetrical

Body

  1. The body is divisible into three parts:
  2. Proboscis (Protosome)
  3. Anterior most part, conical in form, tapering anteriorly
  4. Proboscis stalk
  5. Continues into a narrowed stalk, mostly concealed under collar
  6. Preoral ciliary organ
  7. "U" shaped ciliated depression, a chemoreceptor
  8. Proboscis encloses protocoel or coelom, opens through proboscis pore
  9. Collar (Mesosome)
  10. Middle, short, thick belt-like part behind proboscis
  11. Surface marked with circular grooves or elevations
  12. Collarette
  13. Funnel-like part encircling proboscis stalk
  14. Mouth
  15. Below proboscis stalk, encloses wide aperture
  16. Mesocoel/ Collar coelom
  17. Thick musculature, sometimes divided by mesenteries
  18. Opens by collar pores into brachial sacs
  19. Trunk (Metasome)
  20. Posterior, largest part, flattened with annulations
  21. Marked by dorsal and ventral longitudinal ridges
  22. Differentiated into three regions:
  23. Anterior Branchiogenital region
  24. Presence of gill-pores on sides of mid-dorsal ridge
  25. Genital wings containing gonads, opening through gonopores
  26. Middle Hepatic region
  27. Marked by small, paired hepatic caeca
  28. Posterior Abdominal region
  29. Tapers behind, bears terminal anus

Circulation

  1. Heart located in proboscis
  2. Respiratory pigment is Vanadium

Larva

  1. Larva of Balanoglossus is called tornaria
