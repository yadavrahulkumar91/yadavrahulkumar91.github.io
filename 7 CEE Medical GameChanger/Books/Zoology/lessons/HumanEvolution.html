Introduction

  1. The maximum number of fossils of primitive man have been excavated from Africa.
  2. Evolution of man probably took place in the part of Africa between Ethiopia and South Africa.
  3. Palaeoanthropology is the study of human evolution and culture. It deals with fossil and prehistoric man.
  4. Genelogy is the sequential arrangement of stages in evolution.
  5. The fossil evidence of human evolution is patchy, and the early stages are poorly known.
  6. There has been a great deal of controversy over primate and human relationships because of the limited number of good fossils.

Order: Primates

Characters

  • Primates have a complex brain that is large in relation to body size.
  • Primates have omnivorous diet.
  • Primates have a semi-erect to erect posture.
  • Primates have Arboreal (tree-dwelling) habit.
  • Primates have the Ability to spread toes and fingers apart.
  • Primates have an opposable thumb for grasping (thumb can touch the tip of each finger).
  • Primates have bony sockets protecting eyes.
  • Primates have binocular vision resulting in depth perception.
  • Primates have well-developed eye-hand coordination.
  • Primates have a flattened face without a snout.
  • Primates have parental care, lengthy gestation, one birth at a time.
  • Primates have late sexual maturity and relatively long lifespan.

Sub-order: Prosimii (Gr. Pro=before; simia=ape)

  • Most of the Prosimians are Nocturnal and have large ears and eyes to help them see and hear at night.
  • Prosimians possess elongated snouts, an adaptation for a highly developed sense of smell.

Tarsier

  • Tarsiers are found in the Philippines and East Indies.
  • Tarsiers are insectivorous mouse-sized animals with enormous eyes suitable for nocturnal life. (Recently Tarsiers are removed from prosimii and placed in Anthropoids).
  • Pad-like finger tips are present in Tarsiers

Lemur

  • Lemurs are confined to Madagascar Island.
  • Lemurs are cat-sized animals.
  • Lemurs have long bushy tails, often striped black and white.
  • Lemurs feed on insects, small vertebrates, and fruits.

Loris

  • Lorises occur in Africa and Asia, including South India.
  • Lorises are tailless primates.

Sub-order: Anthropoidea

  • Characters:
    • The Anthropoids differ from the prosimians in the structure of their teeth, brain, skull, and limbs.
    • The Anthropoids are diurnal, that is, active during the day and have evolved color vision.
