Earthworm Anatomy and Features

Introduction

  1. Genera
    • i. Pheretima posthuma
    • ii. Megascolex = South-Indian Earthworm
    • iii. Lumbricus = European Earthworm
    • iv. Metaphire
  2. Habitat = Burrowing (Fossorial)
  3. Habit = Nocturnal

Features

  1. Bilaterally symmetrical
  2. Elongated with Metameric segmentation
    • i. External segmentation = Annuli or Grooves
    • ii. Internal segmentation = Septa

Morphology

  1. Brown/ Clay-coloured
    • i. Due to Porphyrin
      • Found in Circular muscle layer of the body wall
      • Comes from the Chlorophyll in the decaying vegetable matter
      • Protects the body from the harmful radiation of sunlight
  2. No. of segments = 100-120
  3. No. of Setae in each segment = 80-100
  4. Lacks Distinct head and sense organs like
    • i. Eyes
    • ii. Cirri (a thin tendril-like appendage)
    • iii. Tentacles
  5. Cylindrical body
  6. Setae
    • i. Parts of Setae:
      1. Neck
        • a) Projects outside the body
      2. Nodulus
      3. Base
        • a) Embedded inside the body
    • ii. Arrangement of setae
      1. Perichaetine arrangement (Setae arranged in a ring) = Pheretima
      2. Lumbricine/ Otochaetine arrangement (2 pairs of setae are present in each lateral side of the segment) = Lumbricus
    • iii. Made of Chitin = Insoluble in KOH
      1. So section containing Setae is kept in 40% KOH to separate Setae.
    • iv. Operated by 2 sets of Muscles
      1. A pair of Protractor muscle = Contraction causes Extension of Setae (Out)
      2. Single retractor muscle = Contraction causes Withdrawal of Setae (In)
    • v. Ventral and Lateral setae
      1. Ventral setae = Crawling on the ground
      2. Lateral setae = Moving in burrows
  7. Anus is located on the last segment, so a postanal tail is absent in the earthworm.
  8. Dorsal pores
    • i. Behind the 12th segment except the last
    • ii. Situated mid-dorsally in each inter-segmental groove.
    • iii. Exudate the body fluid = to keep the body wall moist.
    • iv. Communicates externally with the coelom
    • v. When earthworm is disturbed, a white milky fluid called Coelomic fluid is ejected through the Dorsal pores.
  9. Nephridiopores
    • i. Pores of Integumentary nephridia
    • ii. Found in all segments except the first six.
    • iii. 200-250 per segment
      • Clitellar segment contains 2000 nephridiopores per segment. So called "Forest of Nephridia".

Body Wall

  1. 4 Layers
    • i. Cuticle
      1. Thin, transparent, flexible, non-cellular, non-chitinous double layered
      2. Made of Collagen fibers
      3. Secreted by Epidermis (Supporting cells)
      4. Mechanical and Chemical injury protection
    • ii. Epidermis
      1. Cellular, Single layered
      2. 4 different types of cells
        • a) Supporting cell = forms the main bulk of the epidermis
        • b) Glandular cell
          1. i) Mucous cell = Secretes mucus = keeps body surface moist and slippery
          2. ii) Albumen cell = Secrete albumen = provides nutrition to the embryo
        • c) Basal cell = Replacement cell = Replaces Supporting and Glandular cells
        • d) Sensory/ Receptor cell = Receive external stimulus
    • iii. Muscular layer/ Musculature
      • 1) Circular muscle = contraction causes Elongation of the body
      • 2) Longitudinal muscle = contraction causes Shortening of the body
    • iv. Coelomic peritoneum/ Parietal peritoneum

Body Cavity

  1. True coelom (Schizocoel) = contain Milky white alkaline coelomic fluid
    • Contains Water, Salts, Minerals, and 4 types of Corpuscles
      1. Amoebocytes/ Phagocytes/ Granulocytes/ Electrocytes
        • Largest in size as well as in number
        • Engulf harmful microorganisms
      2. Chloragogen cell/ Chloro cell/ Yellow cell
        • Small, Star-shaped
        • Storage of reserve food (nutritive function) in the form of Fats and Glycogen
        • Determination of protein formation of urea and also excretory function
          • Analogous to the liver of vertebrates
          • Eliminate the silica which is consumed along with food
      3. Leucocytes/ Circular cell
        • Believed to circulate digested food
      4. Mucocytes
        • Fan-shaped
        • Produce mucous
    • Coelomic fluid acts as Hydraulic Skeleton, aids in locomotion.
    • Alkaline in nature
    • Haemoglobin is absent in the coelomic fluid of earthworm
    • Secreted by = Parietal peritoneum, Continuously produced and lost
    • Bioluminosity is found in some earthworms due to the presence of bioluminous cells in coelomic fluid
  2. Lining
    • i. Outer Parietal peritoneum/ Somatic peritoneum = lining of the body wall
    • ii. Inner Visceral peritoneum/ Splanchnic peritoneum = lining of the alimentary canal

Septa

  1. Absent septa
    • Septa are absent in the first 4 segments.
    • Septa of 9/10 = absent due to Large gizzard
  2. Thickness and shape
    • Septa of 4/5 segment = thin and membranous
    • Septa of 5/6, 6/7, 7/8, 8/9 and 10/11 = Thick, Muscular and Oblique
    • Remaining septa from 11/12 segment = Thin, Membranous and Transverse
  3. Complete and Incomplete septa
    • First nine septa (Up to 13/14 segment) = Complete (i.e., without any apertures)
    • Other remaining septa from 14/15 to posterior = Incomplete septa (Due to perforations in them)

Digestive system

S.N Parts Segments Description
a. Peristomium (Mouth) 1
b. Buccal Chamber 2-3
  • Buccal receptors = Gustato-receptor and Olfacto-receptor
  • Helps in attachment and catching of prey.
c. Pharynx 3-4
  • i. Dorsal salivary chamber
    • a. Chromophil cell/ Saliva secreting gland cell
    • Produces Saliva (Mucus and Proteases)
  • ii. Ventral Conducting chamber
  • Helps in conduction of saliva and food
d. Oesophagus 5-7
e. Gizzard 8
  • Thick walled muscular organ
  • Lined by Thick cuticle covered by thick circular muscle layer
  • Longitudinal muscle is absent
f. Stomach 9-14
  • Contains "Calciferous gland" (lime secreting gland)
  • Neutralizes the acid of soil
g. Intestine 15 onwards
  • Intestinal caeca
    • Arises from 26th segment and extends forward up to 22/23 segments
    • Secrete amylolytic ferment (Starch splitting enzyme)/ Amylase
  • Typhlosole
    • Highly glandular, Vascular longitudinal ridge, Blind and folded structure
    • Increases the area for absorption of digested food
Region Segments Description
Pretyphlosolar Region 15-25
Typhlosolar Region 27-23/25 segments in front of anus
Post-typhlosolar Region (Rectum) Last 23/25 segments Absorption of H2O from waste food and elimination of waste, also known as Rectum.

RESPIRATORY SYSTEM

Cutaneous Respiration

  1. No Respiratory Organs
  2. Exchange of Gases Through Moist Skin
  3. Absorptive Area vs. Volume
    1. Earthworm's absorptive area is larger than its volume
    2. No need for specialized respiratory organs
  4. Abundant Cutaneous Blood Supply
  5. Facilitates Gaseous Exchange
  6. Drying of Skin and Asphyxia
  7. Dry skin leads to inability to respire
  8. Results in asphyxia

Oxygen Carrying Capacity

Earthworm's Blood: 100 cc carries 10 cc of oxygen

Note: Vertebrate Blood carries 20 cc of oxygen per 100 cc.

Nephridial Tube

  • The nephridia consists of a single coiled tubule that extends throughout the nephridia called the nephridial tube.
  • On the straight lobe, 4 segments of tubules are seen:
    • Proximal limb and distal limb = 3 segments each
    • Apical loop = 2 segments
    • Neck and terminal duct = 1 segment
  • The tubule is ciliated at 4 places:
    • 2 on the straight lobe
    • 1 on each of the neck and terminal duct

Reproductive System

  1. Earthworms do not reproduce asexually.
  2. Earthworms are monoecious or hermaphrodites/bisexual.
  3. They are protandrous, and there is no self-fertilization.
    • Notes:
      1. Ovary of an earthworm is larger than the testis.
