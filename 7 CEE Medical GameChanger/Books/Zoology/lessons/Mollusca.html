MOLLUSCA

Definition and Etymology

  1. Mollusca: Meaning "Soft Body"
  2. Coined by Johnston

Branches of Study

  1. Malacology: Study of Mollusca
  2. Conchology: Study of Molluscan Shells

Habitat

  1. Marine: Most
  2. Freshwater: Some
  3. Terrestrial: Few

General Features

  1. Triploblastic
  2. Bilateral Symmetry (Except Gastropods)
  3. Gastropods: Asymmetry due to Torsion
  4. Unsegmented Body (Except Neoplina)
  5. Haemocoelomate
  6. Open Circulatory System (Sepia tendency to become closed)

Mantle and Pallial Cavity

  1. Mantle Skin Fold Covers Body
  2. Secrete Calcareous Spicules, Plates, or Shells
  3. Shells: External, Internal, Absent
  4. Calcium Carbonate Shells Protect and Support Soft Tissues
  5. Oldest and Thickest Part of Shell: Umbo in Unio
  6. Layers of Shell: Periostracum, Prismatic, Nacreous
  7. Mantle Cavity: Pair of Pulmonary Sacs

Body Wall

  1. Single-Layered Ciliated Epidermis

Body Regions

  1. Head: Mouth, Sense Organs
  2. Foot: Ventral, Locomotion, Crawling or Modified as Tentacles (Squid)
  3. Visceral Hump: Contains Digestive Tract and Organs

Digestive System

  1. Complete with Digestive Gland (Hepatopancreas)
  2. Buccal Cavity with Radula
  3. Radula: Chitinous Teeth in Transverse Rows
  4. Odontophore Helps in Straining Food
  5. Present in Pila, Cilia of Bivalves for Feeding
  6. Presence of Green Gland in Unio (Analogous to Vertebrate Liver)

Circulatory System

  1. Blood with Amoebocytes
  2. Respiratory Pigment: Copper-Containing Haemocyanin
  3. Imparts Blue Color to Blood

Respiratory System

  1. Respiratory Organs: Gills or Ctenidia (Aquatic)
  2. Monopectinate in Pila
  3. Pulmonary Sacs or Lungs (Aerial)
  4. Direct Respiration: Sometimes

Excretory System

  1. Excretory Organs: Kidneys or Organ of Bojanus
  2. Pericardial Gland or Keber's Gland

Reproductive System

  1. Sexual Reproduction
  2. Dioecious or Monoecious
  3. Fertilization: Generally External, Mostly Oviparous
  4. Development: Direct with Larval Forms (Trochophore, Veliger, Glochidium)
  5. Veliger: Marine Pelecypod Larva
  6. Glochidium: Ectoparasite on Fish Gills

Nervous System

  1. Nervous System with Ganglia, Commissures, Connectives
  2. Complete Ladder-Like Nervous System
  3. Sense Organs
  4. Eyes with Stalk (Ommatophore)
  5. Statocysts for Equilibrium
  6. Tentacles
  7. Osphradia: Chemoreceptor for Testing Water
