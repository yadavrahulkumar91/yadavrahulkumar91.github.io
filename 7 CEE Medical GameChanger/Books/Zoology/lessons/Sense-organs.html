    2. Eyelids/ Palpebra

      • a. Glands

        Gland of Moll Sweat gland
        Gland of Zeis Sebaceous gland Stye
        Tarsal glands/ Meibomian glands Chalazion

      • b. Palpebral Fissure

        Opening between the upper and lower eyelid.

    4. Conjunctiva

      • a. Palpebral Conjunctiva

        Lining the eyelids/ Palpebra. Pallor for Anaemia is seen here.

      • b. Bulbar Conjunctiva

        Lining the eyeball (sclera only). Jaundice for hepatitis is seen here.

    5. Lacrimal Gland
      • a. Secrete tear which contains salts and lysozyme (bactericidal) and is isotonic to blood and normal saline (0.9% NaCl solution).
      • b. Opens into the nasal cavity by the nasolacrimal duct.
        1. Lacrimal gland
        2. Lacrimal duct
        3. Lacrimal punctum
        4. Lacrimal canal
        5. Lacrimal sac
        6. Nasolacrimal duct
        7. Nasal cavity
      • c. Inflammation of the lacrimal gland is called Dacrocystitis/ Dacroadenitis.
    6. Extrinsic Eye Muscles
      • a. Superior rectus
      • b. Inferior rectus
      • c. Lateral rectus
      • d. Medial rectus
      • e. Superior oblique
      • f. Inferior oblique
    7. Nerves
      • a. 6 out of 12 cranial nerves (II, III, IV, V, VI, and VII) play a role in eye operation.
      • b. 3 of them control extrinsic muscles: LR6 (Lateral Rectus by CN VI), SO4 (Superior Oblique by CN IV), Rest 3 (The rest by CN III).

  • II. Eyeball

    1. 1. Tunica Fibrosa

      • a. Cornea - Anterior Part
        1. Transparent layer made up of 5 layers.
        2. Lined by Non-keratinized stratified squamous epithelium.
        3. Major site of refraction.
        4. Avascular layer, used in eye donation (Keratoplasty).
        5. Inflammation of Cornea is called Keratitis.
      • b. Sclera - Posterior Part
        1. Mesodermal in origin.
        2. Made up of:
          • In Frog - Cartilage.
          • In Rabbit and Human - White, hard, opaque thick fibrous connective tissue.

    2. 2. Tunica Vasculosa

      • a. Iris
        1. Gives color to the eye due to melanin pigment produced by Melanocytes.
        2. Aperture formed by Iris is Pupil.
        3. Muscles of Iris:
          • Outer Radial Muscle - Contraction causes Dilation of pupil (Mydriasis).
          • Inner Circular Muscle - Contraction causes Constriction of pupil (Miosis).
      • b. Ciliary Body
        1. Ciliary muscle.
        2. Ciliary process produces Aqueous humor.
        3. Suspensory ligament/ Zonules of Zinn hold the lens.
        4. Lens of the eye separates the eyeball into two chambers: Aqueous chamber and Vitreous chamber.
      • c. Choroid

        Pigmented vascular layer which prevents the internal reflection of light inside the eyeball.

      • d. Tapetum Lucidum

        The eyes of carnivores like cat, dog, lion, seal, etc. glow at night due to Tapetum lucidum, a reflecting layer between retina and choroid.

    3. 3. Tunica Nervosa/ Retina

      • Ectodermal in origin.
      • Contains multiple layers including Pigmented layer and Nervous tissue layer.

    1. Disorder

      Defects

      Cause

      Correction

    2. Myopia/ Short-sightedness
      • Can see near objects only.
      • Can't see far objects.
      • Decrease in focal length.
      • Increased eyeball size.
      • Concave lens.
    3. Hypermetropia/ Long-sightedness
      • Can see far objects only.
      • Can't see near objects.
      • Increased focal length.
      • Decreased eyeball size.
      • Convex lens.
    4. Astigmatism
      • Blurred vision.
      • Irregular curvature of Cornea.
      • Cylindrical lens.
    5. Presbyopia
      • Blurred vision.
      • Loss of accommodating power in old age.
      • Bifocal lens.
    6. Cataract
      • Increased opacity of lens.
    7. Glaucoma
      • Increased intraocular pressure due to increased aqueous humor.
      • Blockage of Canal of Schlemm.
    8. Trachoma (IOM 2004)
      • Inflammation of Conjunctiva due to Chlamydia trachomatis.
    9. Xerophthalmia/ Dry eye
      • Keratinization of conjunctiva and cornea.
      • Vitamin A deficiency.
      • Decreased tear production.
    10. Photophobia

      Difficulty seeing in bright light.

Skin of Rabbit

E. Skin of Rabbit

  • 1. Characteristics of Skin

    • Soft, thick, dry, and covered with a thick fur called Pelage.
    • Compactly attached to underlying muscle layer by a continuous layer of areolar tissue.
    • Consists of two main layers: Epidermis and Dermis

    • a. Epidermis

      • Consists of 5 layers: Stratum corneum, Stratum lucidium, Stratum granulosum, Stratum spinosum, and Stratum germinativum.
      • Epidermis doesn't possess blood vessels but has nerve fibers.
      • Contains melanin pigment in cells called melanocytes or melanophores.

    • b. Dermis

      • Mesodermal in origin.
      • Two or three times as thick as epidermis.
      • Composed of fibrous connective tissue, unstriped muscle fibers, blood capillaries, nerve fibers, and fat cells.
      • Presence of hair follicles, sweat glands, sebaceous glands, etc.

    • c. Notes

      • Skin derivatives like hair, claws, nails, hoofs, certain glands called cutaneous glands develop from the epidermal cells.
      • Hairs are epidermal in origin and are found only in the skin of mammals.
      • The unstriped erector muscles called arrector pili are attached to the hair follicle and the stratum germinativum.
      • The color of hair is black or brown due to the presence of melanin in its cortex.

  • 2. Cutaneous Glands

    • a. Sweat glands:
      • Confined to the areas around the lips.
      • Mostly merocrine (eccrine), but those present in the axillary regions are apocrine.
    • b. Sebaceous gland:
      • Produce sebum.
      • Are holocrine.

  • 3. Modification of Cutaneous Glands

    • a. Meibomian glands:
      • Modified sebaceous gland.
      • Found in the dermis of the eyelid near its edge and close to eyelashes.
    • b. Zeis gland:
      • Modified sebaceous gland.
      • Found in the dermis of the eyelid but open into hair follicles of the eyelashes.
    • c. Ceruminous or Wax gland:
      • Modified sweat gland.
      • Present in the external auditory meatus.
    • d. Mammary glands:
      • Modified sweat glands.
      • Produce milk in female mammals.
    • e. Perineal glands:
      • Modified sweat gland.
      • Found associated with reproductive organs of mammals.

  • 4. Receptors

    • a. Pain receptors
    • b. Tactile receptors:
      • i. Meissener's corpuscles
      • ii. Merkel's disc
      • iii. Pacinian corpuscles
      • iv. Genital corpuscles
      • v. Vibrioreceptors on vibrissae.
    • c. Thermoreceptors:
      • i. End bulbs of Krause (Frigidoreceptor) - Cold receptor
      • ii. End bulbs of Ruffini (Caloreceptor) - Heat receptors
