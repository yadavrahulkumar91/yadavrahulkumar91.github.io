Body Pores

Aspect Ostia Osculum
i. Ostia act as mouth of Sponge. Osculum acts as Anus of Sponge.
ii. Ostia are many in number. Osculum is single in number.
iii. Ostia are without oscular fringes. Osculum is provided with Oscular fringes/ Valvular fringe.
iv. Ostia is Non-valvular. Osculum is Valvular.
iv. Ostia is also called Incurrent pore. Osculum is also called Excurrent pore.
v. Opening and Closing controlled by Myocytes. Osculum opening is mainly controlled by Myocytes.

Inner layer/ Choanoderm/ Gastral epithelium

  • Choanocyte/ Collar cells/ Flagellated cells
    • Choanocytes or Collar cells are only present in Sponges.
    • The water current through the body of the sponge is maintained by Choanocytes.
    • Choanocytes help in Nutrition.
      1. Food coming through the incoming water is ingested by Choanocytes.
      2. Choanocyte feeds holozoically.
    • The cavity common to all sponges is Spongocoel or Paragastric cavity lined with flagellated choanocytes.

Water Canal System/ Aquiferous System

  1. Sponges have a canal system also known as Water Canal System or Aquiferous system.
  2. Canal system is a characteristic feature of Sponges.
  3. Sponges need a continuous current of water flowing through their bodies for
    • Nutrition
    • Respiration
    • Excretion
    • Reproduction
  4. Types of Canal System
    • Ascon type - Simplest type
    • Sycon type
    • Leucon type
    • Rhagon type
      • Rhagon is Leucon type of Canal system found in some larval stages and will be transformed to leucon type during adulthood.

    • Water ‚ü∂ Ostia ‚ü∂ Spongocoel ‚ü∂ Osculum ‚ü∂ Out
    • Incurrent canal is lined by Pinacocytes.

      • Gemmule passout through Prosopyle.
    • Radial canals are lined by Choanocytes.
  6. Examples: Leucosolenia (Ascon type), Sycon (Sycon type), Spongilla (Leucon type)

Regeneration Capacity

  • All Porifera without exception show high power of Regeneration.
  • Sponges have great regeneration power due to archaeocytes which are highly totipotent cells.
  • Two types of Regeneration capacity are found in Sponges.
    • Epimorphosis - Regeneration of lost part. Example: Sponges, Starfish
    • Morpholaxis - Each cut piece grows into a new organism. Example: Porifera, Coelenterata
  • Regeneration of limbs of amphibians involves
    • Epimorphosis
    • Morpholaxis
    • None

Nutrition

  • All sponges are filter feeders.
  • Note: Few important filter feeders are
    1. Ciliates
    2. Sponges
    3. Mollusca ‚ü∂ Bivalvia e.g: Unio
    4. Hemichordata
    5. Protochordata
  • Digestion is intracellular and holozoic.
  • Digestion of food takes place partially in choanocytes and partially in wandering amoebocytes.
  • Digestion of food takes place in Food vacuoles.

Respiration and Circulation

  • Respiration is performed by both Choanoderm and Body surface, but mainly by Choanoderm.
  • Circulation takes place by Wandering of Amoebocyte cells.

Excretion and Gas Exchange

  • Excretion (primarily Ammonia) and gas exchange occur by Simple diffusion across the Choanoderm.
