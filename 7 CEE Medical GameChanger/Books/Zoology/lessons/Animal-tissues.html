2. Fluid Connective Tissue

a) Blood

  1. Introduction
    • Blood measurement
      • % of blood by weight = 7-8% of the total body weight.
      • Volume of blood:
        • Male = 5-6 litres
        • Female = 4-5 litres
    • Nature = Alkaline (pH = 7.4)
      • pH of blood in Arteries > Veins (Acidic)
      • Acidity of blood makes haemoglobin carry less oxygen.
      • pH of the blood is maintained by balancing the ratio of Sodium bicarbonate and Carbonic acid.
      • Main buffer of the blood is Sodium bicarbonate.
  2. Blood cells (55%)
    • Haemopoiesis (Process of formation of blood cells)
      • During Embryonic and Fetal life:
        • Haemopoiesis takes place in Yolk sac, Liver, Spleen, Thymus gland, Lymph nodes, and Bone marrow.
      • During Adult:
        • Myeloid tissue = Red bone marrow
        • Lymphoid tissue = Spleen, Tonsils, Lymph nodes
        • RBCs + Granular leucocytes + Platelets = By Myeloid tissue
        • Agranular leucocytes = By Lymphoid tissue + Myeloid tissue
    • Erythrocytes (Red Blood Cells, RBCs)
      • Hematocrit is the percentage of blood volume occupied by RBCs.
      • Hematocrit value/Packed cell volume is the relative volume of RBCs as a percentage of total blood volume.
      • Hemocytoblasts in red bone marrow give rise to mature blood cells.
      • Overall
        • Largest RBC = Amphiuma and Proteus
          • Among vertebrates, Amphibia have the largest RBCs
          • Frog has Oval, Biconvex, and Nucleated RBCs
        • Smallest RBC = Musk deer
        • RBC of Mammal
          • RBC of Mammal have Round, Biconcave, Non-nucleated, and without other cytoplasmic organelles
          • RBC of Mammal lose nucleus during the Reticular stage of development
          • Camel and Llama have Oval and Nucleated RBCs
        • Haemocytometer counts RBCs
        • Life span of RBC
        • Vitamin B9 and B12 stimulate the maturation of RBC
        • Haemolysis = Bursting of RBC
        • Spleen/Graveyard (RBC destroys here) / Blood bank (Reservoir of Blood)
        • Enzyme present in RBC is carbonic anhydrase (which is the fastest enzyme)
        • Source of energy for RBC = Anaerobic glycolysis of Glucose
        • Haemoglobin (33% of RBCs)
          • Composition of haemoglobin
            • Haeme: (Iron + Porphyrin)
            • Globin: (Protein made of 4 polypeptide chains)
              • HbA
              • HbAz
              • HbF
                • Main adult Hb
                • Minor adult Hb
                • Fetal Hb
    • Leucocytes (White Blood Cells, WBCs)
      • Introduction
        • Leukemia (Blood cancer) = Abnormal increase in the number of leucocytes
        • Leucopenia = Decrease in the number of Leucocytes
        • Leucocytosis = Increase in the number of Leucocytes
        • Leucoderma = Vitiligo, an autoimmune disorder
      • Granulocytes
        • With cytoplasmic granules.
        • They have a lobed nucleus.
        • Monocytes
        • Eosinophils/ Acidophils
        • Basophils
        • Neutrophils
      • Agranulocytes
        • Without granules.
        • Usually have a spherical nucleus.
        • B-lymphocytes/ Humoral immunity
          • Involved in the production of antibodies
        • T-lymphocytes/ Cell-mediated immunity
          • Protects from pathogens
  • Thrombocyte (Blood platelets)
    • Without nucleus
    • Formed by fragmentation of cells called 'Megakaryocytes' from bone marrow.
    • Sources of Thromboplastin, necessary for blood clotting.
    • Blood clotting factors
      • Factor I: Fibrinogen
      • Factor II: Prothrombin
      • Factor III: Thromboplastin
      • Factor IV: Calcium ions
      • Factor V: Labile factor (Pro-accelerin)
      • Factor VII: Stable factor (Pro-convertin)
      • Factor VIII: Anti-Haemophilic factor A / Anti-Haemophilic Globulin (AHG)
      • Factor IX: Anti-Haemophilic factor B / Plasma Thromboplastin Component (PTC) / Christmas factor
      • Factor X: Stuart-Power factor
      • Factor XI: Anti-Haemophilic factor C / Plasma Thromboplastin Antecedent (PTA)
      • Factor XII: Hageman factor
      • Factor XIII: Fibrin Stabilizing Factor (FSF)
      • Vitamin K is essential for the synthesis of Factors II, VII, IX, and X.
    • Anti-coagulants
      • Heparin
      • Anti-coagulants used by blood banks
        • CPD (Citrate Phosphate Dextrose)
        • ACD (Acid Citrate Dextrose)
        • EDTA (Ethylene Diamine Tetra Acetic Acid)
  • Plasma (45%)
    • Plasma protein (8%)
      • Plasma proteins mostly synthesized by the Liver.
      • Major plasma proteins are
        • Albumin
          • Smallest, Most abundant, and Most important
          • Maintains Normal blood pressure and Osmotic pressure of the blood.
        • Globulin
        • Immunoglobulin
          • Act as antibodies and help in the body's defense.
        • Prothrombin and
        • Fibrinogen
          • Prothrombin and Fibrinogen → Blood clotting
