General Characteristics

Introduction

  • Animals having coelenteron cavity (hollow cavity), also called Gastrovascular cavity, is called Coelenteron.
  • Members of Coelenteron are commonly called Cnidarians.
  • They are called Cnidarians due to the presence of Cnidoblast/Nematoblast or Stinging cells.
  • Study of Cnidarians is called Cnidology.

Body plan

  • Digestion and Circulation occur in Gastrovascular cavity.
  • Thus, it has a Blind-Sac body plan.

Grade of Organization:

  • Cell-tissue grade of body organization.
  • For example, nerve cells form a network of tissue.

Symmetry

  • All Cnidarians are Radially symmetrical except
  • Sea anemone, which is Biradially symmetrical.

Tentacles

  • Mouth is surrounded by tentacles.
  • Tentacles are Hollow in Hydra and Solid in Obelia.
  • Rhopallium or Tentaculocysts are modified tentacles that serve as organs of equilibrium and maintain water circulation.

Body Wall and Cells

  • Diploblastic
  • Ectoderm:
    • Derived from Epidermis
  • Mesoglea:
    • Transparent jelly-like substance is found between Ectoderm and Endoderm, known as Mesoglea.
    • Mesoglea is Non-Cellular.
    • Note:
      1. No teeth are present in the lower jaw of a frog.
      2. No cell is present in Mesoglea.
  • Endoderm:
    • Derived from Gastrodermis
    • Endoderm is twice as thick as epidermis.
Epidermis/ Ectoderm Gastrodermis/ Endodermis
  1. Cnidoblast/ Cnidocyte/ Nematoblast/ Stinging cell
    • Features of Cnidoblast:
    • a. Universal Character of all coelenterates.
    • b. Derived from interstitial cells of Epidermis.
    • c. Present only in Epidermis.
    • d. Maximum in tentacles.
    • e. Absent in Pedal disc/ Basal disc.
    • f. A group of Cnidoblasts is known as Cnidome.
    • a. But, the group of Cnidoblasts on the tentacles are especially called Battery.
    • g. Opening of nematocyst is Operculum.
    • h. Just below the operculum, narrow neck is butt.
    • i. Inside the butt, triangular shaped structure is barb.
    • k. So Nematoblast takes part in Locomotion, Food capture, Offence, and Defence.
    • a. Nematocyst or Stinging capsule is a part of the cell called Cnidoblast or Cnidocyte.
    • i. Nematocyst is known as Independent effector.
    • 1) Discharge of Nematocyst is completely independent of nervous control so called as Independent effector.
    • ii. Discharge of Nematocyst is Enzymatic action.
    • iii. Discharge of Nematocyst involves both Mechanical and Chemical Stimulus.
    • iv. On stimulation the nematocyst shoots out within 0.3-0.5 seconds.
    • v. The action of nematocyst depends on enzymes, which bring about increased osmotic pressure inside the capsule of Cnidoblast.
    • vi. Once flagellum is used, then never withdrawn. So, once used nematocyst is never used again.
    • b. Lasso, bars (3 pairs on large spines) and barbules (50 pairs of smaller spines).
    • 1) Lasso helps in Checking the emerging of nematocyst.
    • c. The trigger needle of Cnidoblast is called Cnidocil.
    • 1) Cnidocil is the stimulus receiving part of Nematoblast/ Cnidoblast.
  2. Interstitial Cell
    • a. Interstitial cell is the totipotent cell.
    • b. So, interstitial cell help in regeneration called Morpholaxis.
    • c. Interstitial cell can transfer into all other types of cells including germ/ sex cell, glandular cell, and Cnidoblast.
    • a. Reproductive
    • b. Glandular
    • c. Stinging
    • d. Brien said that all cells arise from Interstitial cells.
    • e. Interstitial cell help in Formation of External bud.
    • f. Interstitial cells are uninucleated.
    • g. Interstitial cells are absent in Epidermis of Pedal disc and tentacles.
    • h. Interstitial cell can cross the Mesoglea.
  3. Epithelio-muscular cell
    • 1. Epithelio-muscular cell forms the bulk of Epidermis.
    • 2. Muscle tail or Myoneme is present in it.
    • a. The no. of myonemes in muscle tail of epithelio-muscular cells is two.
    • 3. The contraction of muscle fibres in the epithelio-muscular cells shortens the body and tentacles.
  4. Epithelio-gland cells/ Glandulo-muscular cell
    • (1) Glandulo-muscular cell is a modification of epithelial-muscular cells.
    • (2) Glandulo-muscular cell is maximum in epidermis, pedal/basal disc.
    • (3) Functions:
    • a. Glandulo-muscular cell help in Attachment.
    • b. Glandulo-muscular cell creates gas bubble that makes it float. (Floating movement)

1) Epithelial part is towards the epidermis side. 1) Muscular part is towards the Mesoglea side.
2) Epithelial part helps in Cuticle formation. (i.e, Cuticle-Epidermis-Mesoglea-Endodermis). 2) Muscular part helps in Shortening and bending of body i.e, Body movement.
3) Epithelial part also helps in Protection.
Type of Cnidoblast Features and Function
a. Penetrant/ Stenotels - Largest Nematocyst i. Penetrant when discharged, releases thread tube by which poisonous fluid, hypnotoxin is injected.
1. Hypnotoxin either paralyzes or kills the prey like Cyclops and Daphnia (Aquatic arthropods).
2. Hypnotoxin is made of Protein+Phenol.
ii. So, Cnidarians are carnivorous.
iii. Most complex type of Nematoblast.
b. Volvents/ Desmoneme - Smallest Nematocyst i. Volvents when discharged tightly coil thread around small projections such as hair or bristles of prey.
ii. Volvents Coil and Capture the prey.
c. Holotrichous isorhiza/ Streptoline glutinant – Largest glutinant i. Holotrichous Stick the prey.
d. Atrichous isorhiza/ Steroline glutinant - Smallest glutinant i. Atrichous help in Looping movement.
a. Looping movement is the Common mode of locomotion in hydra.
b. Looping movement is Leech-like movement in Hydra.
c. Somersaulting movement is the fastest mode of locomotion.

Reproduction

  • Reproduction is both asexual and sexual.
  • Asexual reproduction
    1. Budding
    2. Regeneration
    3. Fragmentation
  • Sexual reproduction
    • In Hydra, there is a Single ovary and Many testes.

Special Points

  • Hydra have Direct development but a larva-like structure is present called Hydraula or Hydratuba.
  • Common larva of Cnidarians is Planula, especially in Jellyfish, Obelia, and Sea anemone.
  • Relation between Sea anemone (Metridium) and Hermit crab is Commensalism (+, 0). (Anemone le bodyguard ko jasto ra Hermit crab Katrina.)
