Development of Frog

1. Breeding

  • Frog breeds in the rainy season, June to September.
  • Male frogs produce croaking sound (mating call) by vocal sacs.
  • Amplexus is the sexual embrace of male and female frogs.

2. Ovulation

Ovulation is the release of eggs from the ovary into the body cavity.

3. Spawning

  • Spawning is the laying of eggs by the female frog during amplexus.
  • Eggs are surrounded by jelly coats for protection.

4. Structure of Egg

Frog's egg is mesolecithal and telolecithal, with animal and vegetal hemispheres.

5. Fertilization

Fertilization is external and takes place in water.

6. Cleavage

Cleavage involves mitotic cell divisions of the zygote.

  • First cleavage is meridional and equal.
  • Subsequent cleavages are unequal.
  • Several cleavage divisions occur to form blastomeres.

7. Gastrula

Gastrulation is the process converting blastula into a three-layered embryo.

  • Formation of ectoderm, mesoderm, and endoderm.
  • Gastrulation includes epiboly, invagination, and involution.
  • Archenteron and blastopore are formed.

8. Neurula

Neurulation involves the formation of the neural tube, notochord, and gut.

9. Metamorphosis of Tadpole to Adult Frog

Thyroxin hormone secreted by thyroid gland triggers metamorphosis in tadpoles.

Tadpole undergoes changes like tail regression, development of lungs, modified blood vascular system, formation of limbs, and more.
