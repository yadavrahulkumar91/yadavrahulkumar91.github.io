Introduction

  1. Paramecium is a holotrichous ciliate protozoan.
  2. Hill discovered Paramecium.
  3. The most common species is Paramecium caudatum.
  4. It is a free-living and aquatic form.
  5. In the laboratory, Paramecium is cultured by the 'Hay-infusion method'.
  6. It is found in fresh stagnant water containing abundant organic matter.
  7. Paramecium is commonly called 'Slipper animalcule'.

Cellular Structure

Body of Paramecium

    It is distinguished into

  1. An Oral surface/ Ventral surface and
  2. An Aboral surface/ Dorsal surface.

Pellicle

  1. Body of Paramecium is covered with a thin, firm and flexible membrane called Pellicle.
  2. Pellicle is made by a regular arrangement of microtubules.
  3. Pellicle gives shape to the body.

Cilia

  1. Entire body surface of Paramecium is covered by numerous cilia, the locomotory organelles.
  2. Cilia in the posterior end are longer called Caudal tuft.
  3. Paramecium moves by cilia.
  4. There are special groups of Cilia.
    1. Ventral penniculus
    2. Dorsal quadrulus and
    3. Dorsal penniculus
  5. Each cilium arises from basal granule or Kinetosome.
  6. Movement of cilia
    1. The cilia of longitudinal row move simultaneously one by one known as Metachronous rhythm whereas
    2. Cilia of transverse row beat in Synchronous rhythm.
    3. ATP plays a good role in the movement of cilia.

Trichocysts

  1. Trichocysts are peculiar bottle-shaped organelles present in the ectoplasm of Paramecium.
  2. Trichocysts are organs of attachment earlier considered as organs of Offence and Defence.
  3. The outer membrane of trichocysts is conical in shape and is called the Spike.
    1. A cap üß¢ is always over the Spike.
  4. The anterior end becomes very long in the discharged state and is called the Shaft.
    1. Discharged trichocyst is ten times longer than undischarged one.

Contractile vacuoles

  1. There are 2 contractile vacuoles that permanently open on the dorsal surface.
  2. Each vacuole is with 6-10 Radiating canals/ Accessory vacuoles.
  3. Contractile vacuoles help in Osmoregulation.

Nucleus

  1. Paramecium is heterokaryotic (dimorphic nuclei).
  2. Macronucleus
    1. Macronucleus is
      1. Shape
        1. One
        2. Large
        3. Polypoid
        4. Kidney-shaped
      2. Function
        1. Regulates daily metabolic activities
    2. Macronucleus consists Trophochromatin.
  3. Micronucleus
    1. Number
      1. Paramecium caudatum - 1
      2. Paramecium aurelia - 2
      3. Paramecium multi-micronucleatum - many
    2. Function
      1. Micronuclei are genetic reserve of the cell.
      2. Micronuclei control reproductive activities.
    3. Micronucleus consists Idiochromatin.

Nutrition

  1. Nutrition of food intake in Paramecium is Holozoic.
  2. Paramecium is a Filter feeder.
  3. Most favourite food of Paramecium is Tetrahymena.
    1. Tetrahymena is another ciliate protozoa.
    2. Tetrahymena is a protozoan used in biological research.
  4. Oral groove/ Peristomal groove
    1. Paramecium takes food through Oral groove/ Peristomal groove at the left side of anterior end of the body.
  5. The cyclosis ensures that digested food material is distributed uniformly.
  6. Digestion is intracellular.
  7. The food vacuole is digested in the cell body in acidic to alkaline media.
    1. Acidic is blue green in colour and
    2. Basic is yellow in colour.
  8. Cytopyge or Cytoproct
    1. Egestion of undigested food takes place through Cytopyge or Cytoproct.
    2. Cytopyge or Cytoproct is temporarily formed Anus.
    3. Cytopyge is called as Cell anal spot.
    4. Cytopyge is situated on the ventral surface of the oral groove.

Reproduction

  1. Asexual reproduction (By Binary fission)/ Transverse fission
    1. At first, Paramecium stops feeding and Oral groove disappears.
    2. The binary fission is called Transverse fission because division takes place right angle to the body axis.
    3. In binary fission,
      1. macronucleus divides Amitotically and
      2. micronucleus Mitotically.
    4. Each Paramecium divides into two daughter Paramecia.
      1. Anterior one is called as Proter.
      2. Posterior one is called as Opisthe.
    5. Binary fission occurs during favorable conditions.
    6. It is Faster multiplication.
      1. It takes about half an hour to 2 hours to complete binary fission.
      2. In a single day, about 2 to 3 binary fissions occur.
  2. Sexual reproduction (By conjugation)
    1. Conjugation is a temporary union of two individuals of different mating types for the exchange of micronuclear material.
    2. Conjugation overcomes aging or senescence which is caused due to repeated binary fissions.
    3. Conjugation brings about nuclear reorganization.
      1. In Paramecium, nuclear reorganization takes place for Rejuvenation.

iv. Process of Conjugation

Process of Conjugation

  1. During conjugation, two Paramecia of different mating types unite ventrally by their oral grooves. They are called as Conjugants.
  2. Macronucleus disintegrates and gets hydrolysed by enzymes and ultimately absorbed by the cytoplasm.
  3. Micronucleus divides twice, one of the divisions is reductional division, thus, 4 haploid daughter nuclei are formed out of which 3 disintegrate and 1 remains functional.
  4. The functional one divides unequally and forms 2 nuclei, one larger stationaly known as female pro-nucleus and the other smaller migratory male pro-nucleus.
  5. The male pronuclei are exchanged between the conjugants and fusion between the exchanged male pronucleus and stationary female pronucleus occurs.
    • a) A diploid zygote nucleus is formed which is also called as Synkaryon.
    • b) The complete fusion of nuclei of two different individuals is termed as Amphimixis.
  6. The conjugants get separated and are now known as ex-conjugants.
    • a) The zygote nucleus of each ex-conjugant divides 3 times by mitotic division and 8 daughter nuclei are formed.
    • b) Out of 8 daughter nuclei, 4 are larger and called macronuclei, and the other 4 are smaller and called micronuclei.
      1. i) Out of 4 micronuclei, 3 disintegrate. The remaining micronucleus divides by mitosis and forms 2 micronuclei.
      2. ii) At the same time, binary fission occurs in each ex-conjugant and thus 2 daughter paramecia with 2 macronuclei and 1 micronucleus are produced.
      3. iii) Again, binary fission occurs after which each micronucleus divides into two micronuclei. Thus, each ex-conjugant gives 4 Paramecia.
  7. Thus, 8 daughter Paramecia are produced in conjugation.

Note on Reproduction:

  1. Autogamy
    1. Micronucleus of a single individual divides and fuses to form micro and macronuclei.
    2. Autogamy is Self-fertilization.
    3. Autogamy occurs in Paramecium aurelia.
    4. Autogamy results in the production of two daughter Paramecia from each.
  2. Cytogamy
    1. Two individuals come in contact; no breakdown of cell membrane and no exchange of nuclear material.
    2. Cytogamy occurs in Paramecium caudatum.
    3. The two cytogamonts do not exchange their male pronuclei.
  3. Endomixis (Parthenogenesis)
    1. Macronucleus disappears, micro divides and without fusion gives new micro and macronuclei in a single individual.
    2. Endomixis occurs in Paramecium aurelia.
    3. Endomixis is a form of asexual reproduction.
    4. In Endomixis, 1 individual produces 4 daughter Paramecia.
  4. Hemixis = purification act = reconstitution of macronucleus without any changes in micronucleus

Note on Particles

  1. Paramecium has Kappa(Œö), Lamda(Œª), Mu(Œº) and Pi(œÄ) particles in the cytoplasm.
  2. Kappa particle
    1. In some Paramecium, there is the presence of a special particle in the cytoplasm known as Kappa particles (k-particles).
      1. Paramecium containing Kappa particles secretes a toxic substance called Paramecin.
      2. These Paramecium are called killer strains.
    2. Features of Kappa particles
      1. Kappa particles are very minute.
      2. Kappa particles are capable of self-replicating.
      3. Kappa particles contain nucleic acids.
      4. Kappa particles are also concerned with cytoplasmic inheritance.
    3. If an individual with k-particles (Killer strain) happens to conjugate with an individual without K-particles (Sensitive strain), the individual without K-particles will die.
