1. Which of the following animals belongs to Echinodermata?
    1. (a) Sea urchin (Echinus)
    2. (b) Sea horse
    3. (c) Sea hare
    4. (d) Sea mouse
    (a) Sea urchin (Echinus). Sea urchins belong to the phylum Echinodermata. They are marine animals characterized by their spiny spherical bodies. Sea urchins are part of a diverse group of echinoderms that also includes starfish, sea cucumbers, and sea lilies.
  2. What is the locomotory organ in starfish?
    1. (a) Tentacles
    2. (b) Feet
    3. (c) Water vascular system
    4. (d) Cilia
    (c) Water vascular system. The water vascular system is the locomotory organ in starfish. It is a network of water-filled canals that extend throughout the body of a starfish. The water vascular system helps in movement, feeding, and respiration in starfish.
  3. Where is a well-developed water vascular system found?
    1. (a) Mollusca
    2. (b) Echinodermata
    3. (c) Porifera
    4. (d) Pisces
    (b) Echinodermata. A well-developed water vascular system is found in echinoderms. The water vascular system is a unique characteristic of echinoderms and is involved in locomotion, respiration, and feeding. It consists of a network of fluid-filled canals and tube feet.
  4. Which phylum is exclusively marine?
    1. (a) Porifera
    2. (b) Coelenterata
    3. (c) Mollusca
    4. (d) Echinodermata
    (d) Echinodermata. The phylum Echinodermata is exclusively marine, meaning all its members live in the marine environment. Echinoderms include organisms like starfish, sea urchins, sea cucumbers, and brittle stars.
  5. Which of the following characteristics is common in organisms attached to the substratum?
    1. (a) Asymmetrical body
    2. (b) Radial symmetry
    3. (c) Cilia on surface to create water current
    4. (d) One single opening of digestive canal
    (b) Radial symmetry. Organisms attached to the substratum generally possess radial symmetry. Radial symmetry allows these organisms to interact with their environment from all sides equally, which is beneficial for filter-feeding or obtaining nutrients from the surrounding water.
  6. In which class of echinodermata is the bipinnaria larvae found?

    1. (a) Freshwater

  34. Uric acid is the main nitrogenous waste product in:
    1. (a) Fish
    2. (b) Birds
    3. (c) Amphibians
    4. (d) Mammals
    (b) Birds. Uric acid is the main nitrogenous waste product in birds. It is produced as a result of the breakdown of purine nucleotides, and it is excreted as a semi-solid or solid waste material. This adaptation helps birds conserve water, as uric acid requires less water for excretion compared to ammonia or urea.

  36. When a bird is transferred from 30°C to 10°C, its body temperature:
    1. (a) Is maintained at the original level due to increased dissipation of heat
    2. (b) Rises above the normal level due to increased production and conservation of heat
    3. (c) Is maintained at the original level due to increased conservation and production of heat
    4. (d) Declines due to increased dissipation of heat
    (d) Declines due to increased dissipation of heat. When a bird is transferred from a higher temperature (30°C) to a lower temperature (10°C), its body temperature will decline. Birds are warm-blooded animals that maintain a relatively constant internal body temperature, but they rely on external sources of heat, such as sunlight, to help regulate their body temperature. In colder environments, birds will lose heat more rapidly, leading to a decline in body temperature.
  37. Owls can move freely during the night because they have:
    1. (a) Only cones in their retina
    2. (b) Only rods in their retina
    3. (c) Plenty of vitamin A
    4. (d) Adjustable pupil
    (d) Adjustable pupil. Owls have an adjustable pupil that can dilate or constrict to allow more or less light into their eyes. This adaptation helps them see clearly in low light conditions, such as during the night. Additionally, owls have specialized adaptations in their retinas and other parts of their eyes that enhance their vision in dim light, including a higher density of rod cells, which are more sensitive to light than cone cells.
