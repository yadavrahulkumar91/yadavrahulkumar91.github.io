1. The age of fossils of Dryopithecus on the geological time scale is:
    1. (a) 2.5 x 106 years back
    2. (b) 50 x 106 years back
    3. (c) 75 x 106 years back
    4. (d) 25 x 106 years back
    (c) 75 x 106 years back. The age of fossils of Dryopithecus on the geological time scale is estimated to be around 75 million years back. Dryopithecus is an extinct genus of primates that lived during the Miocene epoch. Fossil remains of Dryopithecus have been found in Europe and Africa, and they provide important insights into the evolution of primates and the origins of human ancestors.
  2. Artistic sense was first developed in:
    1. (a) Cro-magnon man
    2. (b) Neanderthal man
    3. (c) Java-Ape man
    4. (d) Australopithecus
    (a) Cro-magnon man. Artistic sense was first developed in Cro-magnon man. Cro-magnon refers to a group of early modern humans that lived in Europe during the Upper Paleolithic period. They are known for their advanced cultural and artistic achievements, including the creation of intricate cave paintings and sculptures. The artistic expressions of Cro-magnon man provide valuable insights into their cognitive abilities and cultural development.
  3. Which one of the following is the probable mode of present men (Homo sapiens sapiens)?
    1. (a) Homo erectus - H. erectus erectus - H. sapiens sapiens
    2. (b) Homo habilis - H. habilis sapiens - H. sapiens sapiens
    3. (c) Homo erectus - H. sapiens neanderthalensis - H. sapiens sapiens
    4. (d) Homo habilis - H. sapiens fossilis - H. sapiens sapiens
    (c) Homo erectus - H. sapiens neanderthalensis - H. sapiens sapiens. The probable mode of present humans (Homo sapiens sapiens) involves a common ancestry with Homo erectus, followed by a branching off into Homo sapiens neanderthalensis (Neanderthals), and finally leading to Homo sapiens sapiens (modern humans). This model suggests a complex evolutionary history with multiple hominin species and populations that eventually led to the emergence of modern humans.
  4. Forelimbs of horse and human arm are:
    1. (a) Analogous organs
    2. (b) Homologous organs
    3. (c) Atavistic organs
    4. (d) Vestigial organs
    (b) Homologous organs. The forelimbs of a horse and a human arm are considered homologous organs. Homologous organs are those that have a similar underlying structure and evolutionary origin, although they may serve different functions in different species. In the case of horse forelimbs and human arms, both have a similar bone structure (humerus, radius, and ulna) and share a common evolutionary history, despite the differences in their appearance and function.
  5. Most important affinity of present man with the extinct Cro-magnon is due to:
    1. (a) Bipedal habit
    2. (b) Similar facial features
    3. (c) Size of the brain
    4. (d) Facial structure
    (c) Size of the brain. The most important affinity of present humans with the extinct Cro-magnon is due to the size of the brain. Cro-magnon, as an early modern human group, had a brain size similar to that of present-day humans. This indicates a significant level of cognitive development and intellectual capacity, which is a defining characteristic of Homo sapiens. While bipedal habit and similar facial features may also be shared traits, the size of the brain is considered the most important affinity.
  6. Origin of species was derived by:
    1. (a) Hugo de Vries
    2. (b) Lamarck
    3. (c) Charles Darwin
    4. (d) Mendel
    (c) Charles Darwin. The origin of species was derived by Charles Darwin. In his groundbreaking work "On the Origin of Species," Darwin proposed the theory of evolution by natural selection. He presented compelling evidence for the descent of species from common ancestors and explained how natural selection drives the process of adaptation and the formation of new species over time. Darwin's theory revolutionized our understanding of the natural world and laid the foundation for modern evolutionary biology.
