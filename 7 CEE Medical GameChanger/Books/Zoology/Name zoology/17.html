  11. Aerial mode of animal adaptation is known as:
    1. (a) Cursorial
    2. (b) Fossorial
    3. (c) Scansorial
    4. (d) Volant
    (d) Volant. The aerial mode of animal adaptation is known as volant. Volant adaptation refers to the ability of animals to fly or glide through the air. This adaptation is observed in various animals such as birds, bats, and certain insects. These animals have specialized structures and physiological adaptations that allow them to move and navigate effectively in the air.
  12. Claw of rabbit is a modification of:
    1. (a) Epidermis
    2. (b) Dermis
    3. (c) Gastrodermis
    4. (d) Keratin
    (a) Epidermis. The claw of a rabbit is a modification of the epidermis. Claws are hard, keratinized structures that extend from the tips of the digits or toes in certain animals. In the case of rabbits, the claws are derived from the epidermal tissue and serve various functions such as digging, defense, and grooming.
  13. Some animals change their color in accordance with change in climate. This is due to the presence of:
    1. (a) Chromatophores
    2. (b) Keratin
    3. (c) Melanin
    4. (d) Eledin
    (a) Chromatophores. Some animals change their color in accordance with changes in climate due to the presence of chromatophores. Chromatophores are specialized cells found in the skin of certain animals, such as reptiles, amphibians, and fish. They contain pigments that can be expanded or contracted, allowing the animal to alter its coloration for various purposes, including camouflage, communication, and thermoregulation.
  14. Animals capable of running on digits are:
    1. (a) Arborea
    2. (b) Cursorial
    3. (c) Fossorial
    4. (d) Pelagic
    (b) Cursorial. Animals capable of running on digits are cursorial. Cursorial adaptation refers to the adaptation of animals for swift running or locomotion on land. Animals with cursorial adaptations have elongated limbs and digits that allow them to cover long distances quickly. Examples of cursorial animals include cheetahs, antelopes, and ostriches.
  15. In ecological sense, the desert animals are termed as:
    1. (a) Arboreal
    2. (b) Benthos
    3. (c) Cursorial
    4. (d) Xerocles
    ( d) Xerocles. In ecological sense, desert animals are termed as xerocles. The term "xerocles" refers to animals that are adapted to arid or desert environments. These animals have specific adaptations that allow them to survive in the harsh desert conditions, such as water conservation mechanisms, heat tolerance, and adaptations for obtaining water and nutrients from limited resources.
  16. Lateral line organs are found in:
    1. (a) Amphibia
    2. (b) Gnathostomata
    3. (c) Osteichthyes
    4. (d) Reptilia
    (c) Osteichthyes. Lateral line organs are found in osteichthyes. The lateral line system is a sensory organ found in most fish and some amphibians. It consists of a series of sensory receptors called neuromasts, which are arranged in lines along the sides of the fish's body. The lateral line system allows fish to detect changes in water pressure and movement, enabling them to navigate, detect prey, and communicate with other fish.
