1. Mouth of an earthworm is called:
    1. (a) Prostomium
    2. (b) Peristomium
    3. (c) Clitellum
    4. (d) Gizzard
    (b) Peristomium. The mouth of an earthworm is called the peristomium. It is the first body segment located at the anterior end of the worm. The peristomium contains the mouth opening and helps in ingestion of food.
  2. Nephrostomes are absent in:
    1. (a) Integumentary nephridia
    2. (b) Septal nephridia
    3. (c) Pharyngeal nephridia
    4. (d) Both (a) & (c)
    (d) Both (a) & (c). Nephrostomes are absent in both integumentary nephridia and pharyngeal nephridia of an earthworm. Nephrostomes are funnel-like openings in the nephridia that help in the filtration of waste materials from the coelomic fluid.
  3. In the 15th segment of Pheretima, which type(s) of nephridia is/are found?
    1. (a) Septal and integumentary
    2. (b) Septal and pharyngeal
    3. (c) Integumentary and pharyngeal
    4. (d) Septal nephridia only
    (b) Septal and pharyngeal. In the 15th segment of Pheretima, both septal and pharyngeal nephridia are found. Nephridia are excretory organs in earthworms that help in the elimination of metabolic wastes from the coelomic fluid.
  4. Which of the following is exo-nephric nephridia in an earthworm?
    1. (a) Septal
    2. (b) Pharyngeal
    3. (c) Integumentary
    4. (d) Protonephridia
    (c) Integumentary. The exo-nephric nephridia in an earthworm are the integumentary nephridia. These nephridia are located in the body wall (integument) and help in the removal of nitrogenous wastes from the coelomic fluid.
  5. Female genital aperture in an earthworm is found in:
    1. (a) 18th segment
    2. (b) 14th segment
    3. (c) 13th segment
    4. (d) 12th segment
    (b) 14th segment. The female genital aperture in an earthworm is found in the 14th segment, mid-ventrally. It is the opening through which the eggs are released during reproduction.
  6. Nephridia bring all the waste products from:
    1. (a) Gut
    2. (b) Coelom and blood
    3. (c) Clitellum
    4. (d) Prostomium
    (b) Coelom and blood. Nephridia in an earthworm bring all the waste products from the coelomic fluid and blood. They function as excretory organs and help in the removal of metabolic wastes from the body.
  7. Setae are found in all segments except:
    1. (a) 1st, last, and clitellar
    2. (b) 1st, 2nd, and last
    3. (c) 1st and 2nd
    4. (d) 1st, 2nd, last, and clitellar
    (c) 1st and 2nd. Setae, which are bristle-like structures, are found in all segments of an earthworm except the 1st and 2nd segments. They help in locomotion and provide grip and support while the earthworm moves through the soil.
  8. Subpharyngeal nerves in an earthworm are:
    1. (a) One pair
    2. (b) Two pairs
    3. (c) Three pairs
    4. (d) Four pairs
    (b) Two pairs. Earthworms have two pairs of subpharyngeal nerves. These nerves are associated with the pharynx and play a role in the control and coordination of feeding and swallowing.
  9. Earthworm has four pairs of hearts in the following segments:
    1. (a) 7, 9, 12, 13
    2. (b) 7, 9, 10
    3. (c) 7, 8, 10, 12
    4. (d) 7, 8, 11, 13
    (c) 7, 8, 10, 12. Earthworms have four pairs of aortic arches, which function as hearts. These hearts are located in segments 7, 8, 10, and 12 of the earthworm's body and help in the circulation of blood.
  10. Nephridia are not found in Pheretima posthuma in:
    1. (a) 1st, 2nd, and last segments
    2. (b) Only the 1st, 2nd, or 3rd segments
    3. (c) The 1st and last segments only
    4. (d) The 14th, 15th, and 16th segments
    (b) Only the 1st, 2nd, or 3rd segments. In Pheretima posthuma, commonly known as the Indian earthworm, nephridia are absent in the first, second, and third segments. The nephridia start appearing from the fourth segment onwards.

  21. 20. Earthworm, the clitellum helps in:
    1. (a) Cocoon formation
    2. (b) Excretion
    3. (c) Pigmentation
    4. (d) Movement
    (a) The clitellum in earthworms is involved in cocoon formation. It is a specialized glandular region that secretes a protective cocoon around the fertilized eggs of the earthworm.
  22. 21. Nerve cord of earthworm is:
    1. (a) Dorsal and hollow
    2. (b) Ventral and hollow
    3. (c) Dorsal and solid
    4. (d) Ventral and solid
    (a) The nerve cord of an earthworm is dorsal and hollow. It runs along the length of the body on the dorsal side and contains a central canal that is responsible for transmitting nerve impulses.
  23. 22. The function of clitellum in Pheretima is:
    1. (a) Cocoon formation
    2. (b) Fertilization
    3. (c) Nutrition
    4. (d) Copulation
    (a) In Pheretima, the clitellum is involved in cocoon formation. It secretes a mucous cocoon that contains the fertilized eggs of the earthworm.
  24. 23. Clitellum is present in segments:

    3. (c) 14, 15, 16
    4. (d) 15, 16, 17

  25. 24. Earthworm appears brown due to presence of:
    1. (a) Creatinine
    2. (b) Porphyrin
    3. (c) Chloragogen cells

  26. 25. How many spermathecae are found in Pheretima?
    1. (a) 8 pairs
    2. (b) 6 pairs
    3. (c) 4 pairs
    4. (d) 2 pairs
    (c) Pheretima, an earthworm, has 4 pairs of spermathecae. Spermathecae are sac-like structures in the reproductive system of female earthworms that store sperm for fertilization.
  27. 26. What is common in man and earthworm?
    1. (a) Ammonotelic
    2. (b) Uricotelic
    3. (c) Uriotelic
    4. (d) Nephrotelic
    (b) Both man and earthworm are uricotelic, meaning they excrete waste nitrogen primarily in the form of uric acid. Uric acid is relatively insoluble and requires less water for excretion compared to other nitrogenous waste products.
  28. 27. Which of the following organisms has red blood but does not have red blood cells?
    1. (a) Cockroach
    2. (b) Earthworm
    3. (c) Frog
    4. (d) Fish
    (b) The earthworm has red blood but does not have red blood cells. The red color of earthworm blood is due to the presence of a respiratory pigment called hemoglobin, which is dissolved directly in the blood plasma.
