1. 1. Trocophore larva occurs in:
    1. (a) Annelida and Porifera
    2. (b) Annelida and Mollusca
    3. (c) Mollusca and Coelenterata
    4. (d) Coelenterata and Annelida
    (b) The trochophore larva is a characteristic larval stage found in both Annelida (segmented worms) and Mollusca (mollusks).
  2. 2. Which of the following animal is a mollusca?
    1. (a) Silverfish
    2. (b) Cuttlefish
    3. (c) Flying fish
    4. (d) Starfish
    (b) Cuttlefish is a mollusk belonging to the class Cephalopoda. Mollusks are a diverse group of invertebrates that include animals like snails, clams, and octopuses.
  3. 3. Which of the following is a cephalopod?
    1. (a) Starfish
    2. (b) Cuttlefish
    3. (c) Silverfish
    4. (d) Crayfish
    (b) Cuttlefish is a cephalopod, which is a class of mollusks known for their advanced sensory abilities and agile locomotion.
  4. 4. Which animal has a modified mantle cavity to expel water with force?
    1. (a) Squid
    2. (b) Snail
    3. (c) Chiton
    4. (d) Oyster
    (a) The squid has a modified mantle cavity called a siphon or funnel, which it uses to forcefully expel water, allowing for rapid propulsion.
  5. 5. Which of the following is an excretory organ in Unio?
    1. (a) Nephridia
    2. (b) Flame cells
    3. (c) Keber's organ
    4. (d) Malpighian organ
    (c) Keber's organ (also known as the organ of Bojanus or pericardial organ) is the excretory organ in mollusks belonging to the genus Unio.
  6. 6. Ink gland is found in:
    1. (a) Octopus
    2. (b) Oyster
    3. (c) Cuttlefish
    4. (d) Hydra
    (c) The ink gland is found in cuttlefish, along with other cephalopods like squid and octopus. It produces ink as a defense mechanism.
  7. 7. Which of the following belongs to mollusca?
    1. (a) Cuttlefish
    2. (b) Goldfish
    3. (c) Silverfish
    4. (d) Starfish
    (a) Cuttlefish belongs to the class Cephalopoda, which is a part of the phylum Mollusca.
  8. 8. Pila belongs to:
    1. (a) Arthropoda
    2. (b) Coelenterata
    3. (c) Mollusca
    4. (d) Porifera
    (c) Pila belongs to the phylum Mollusca. It is a genus of freshwater snails.
