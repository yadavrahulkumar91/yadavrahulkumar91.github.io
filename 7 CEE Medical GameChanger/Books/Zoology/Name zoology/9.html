  11. 11. Leech obtains continuous blood-stream from its victim by pouring in:
    1. (a) Heparin
    2. (b) Insulin
    3. (c) Pepsin
    4. (d) Hirudin
    (d) Leech obtains a continuous blood-stream from its victim by pouring in hirudin. Hirudin is a potent anticoagulant secreted by leeches, which prevents blood clotting and allows the leech to feed on its host's blood for an extended period.
  12. 12. "Sea mouse" belongs to phylum:

    2. (b) Mollusca
    3. (c) Annelida

    (c) "Sea mouse" belongs to the phylum Annelida. It is a marine polychaete worm characterized by its bristly appearance due to the presence of numerous setae on its body.
  13. 13. Which is not a feature of annelid?
    1. (a) Metamerism
    2. (b) Nephridia
    3. (c) Pseudocoelom
    4. (d) Clitellum
    (c) Pseudocoelom is not a feature of annelids. Annelids have a true coelom, which is a fluid-filled body cavity lined with mesodermal tissue. Pseudocoelom is found in some other phyla, such as Nematoda.
  14. 14. Chloragogen cells resemble the following function:
    1. (a) Collared cells
    2. (b) Flame cells
    3. (c) Plasma cells
    4. (d) Mesophyll cells
    (d) Chloragogen cells resemble mesophyll cells in function. Chloragogen cells are found in the digestive system of certain annelids, such as earthworms, and are involved in the storage and metabolism of nutrients, as well as in the detoxification of metabolic waste products.
  15. 15. The main excretory organ of Annelida is:
    1. (a) Flame cell
    2. (b) Malpighian tubule
    3. (c) Kidney
    4. (d) Nephridium
    (d) The main excretory organ of Annelida is the nephridium. Nephridia are tubular structures found in each segment of an annelid's body. They function in excretion by filtering waste products from the coelomic fluid and eliminating them from the body.
  16. 16. The basis of classification of Phylum 'Annelida' is:
    1. (a) Shape of the body
    2. (b) Sense organ
    3. (c) Locomotory organ
    4. (d) Clitellum position
    (d) The basis of classification of Phylum Annelida is the position of the clitellum. The clitellum is a specialized reproductive structure found in certain annelids, such as earthworms, and its position and characteristics are used to classify different groups within the phylum.
