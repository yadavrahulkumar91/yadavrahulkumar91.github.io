1. Total number of Paramecia formed after conjugation is:
    1. (a) 2
    2. (b) 4
    3. (c) 8
    4. (d) 16
    (c) After conjugation, a total of 8 Paramecia are formed.
  2. Paramecium moves by:
    1. (a) Cilia
    2. (b) Flagella
    3. (c) Pseudopodia
    4. (d) None
    (a) Paramecium moves by cilia.
  3. 'Pellicle' is the covering of:
    1. (a) Paramecium
    2. (b) Diatoms
    3. (c) Dinoflagellates
    4. (d) Amoeba
    (a) The pellicle is the covering of Paramecium.
  4. The process of reconstitution of macronucleus in Paramecium without any change in micronucleus is called:
    1. (a) Cytogamy
    2. (b) Hemixis
    3. (c) Endomixis
    4. (d) Autogamy
    (c) The process of reconstitution of macronucleus in Paramecium without any change in micronucleus is called endomixis.
  5. Which organism exhibits nuclear dimorphism?
    1. (a) Amoeba
    2. (b) Euglena
    3. (c) Plasmodium
    4. (d) Paramecium
    (d) Paramecium exhibits nuclear dimorphism.
  6. Which of the following is true about conjugation in Paramecium?
    1. (a) Macronucleus forms micronucleus
    2. (b) Crossing macronuclei takes place
    3. (c) Macronucleus breaks and gets transferred
    4. (d) Micronucleus gives rise to male and female portion
    (c) In Paramecium, conjugation involves the breaking and transfer of the macronucleus.
  7. Defense action of Paramecium is known by:
    1. (a) Cilia
    2. (b) Trichocysts
    3. (c) Nematocysts
    4. (d) Trochophores
    (b) Paramecium exhibits defense action through trichocysts.
  8. How many daughter Paramecia are formed from conjugation in Paramecia?
    1. (a) 6
    2. (b) 4
    3. (c) 8
    4. (d) 2
    (b) Four daughter Paramecia are formed from conjugation in Paramecia.
  9. The main function of the contractile vacuole in Paramecium is:
    1. (a) Locomotion
    2. (b) Water storage
    3. (c) Osmoregulation
    4. (d) Circulation
    (c) The main function of the contractile vacuole in Paramecium is osmoregulation.
  10. Which of the following shows nuclear dimorphism?
    1. (a) Plasmodium
    2. (b) Paramecium
    3. (c) Hydra
    4. (d) Amoeba
    (b) Paramecium exhibits nuclear dimorphism.
  11. The generic name of Paramecium caudatum was given by:
    1. (a) Rosenhoff
    2. (b) John Hill
    3. (c) Forde
    4. (d) Charles Laveran
    (a) The generic name of Paramecium caudatum was given by Rosenhoff.
