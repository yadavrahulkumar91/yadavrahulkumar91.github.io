1. 1. Which of the following statements is true about the alimentary canal of Roundworms?
    1. (a) No mouth
    2. (b) Only have anus
    3. (c) Both mouth and anus
    4. (d) None of the above
    (c) Roundworms have both a mouth and an anus in their alimentary canal. The mouth is used for ingestion, and the anus is the opening through which undigested waste is eliminated.
  2. 2. What is the special modification of Ascaris to its parasitic mode of life?
    1. (a) Straight and uncoiled alimentary canal
    2. (b) Segmented body
    3. (c) Resistant cuticle on the body surface
    4. (d) Cylindrical body
    (c) Ascaris has a special modification of a resistant cuticle on its body surface. This cuticle protects the worm from the host's digestive enzymes and allows it to survive in the harsh environment of the host's intestine.
  3. 3. Filaria is a:
    1. (a) Protozoa
    2. (b) Virus
    3. (c) Helminth
    4. (d) Bacteria
    (c) Filaria is a helminth. It refers to a group of parasitic worms that cause diseases such as lymphatic filariasis, commonly known as elephantiasis.
  4. 4. Which one of the following is true for Ascaris?
    1. (a) Outside -> intestine -> liver - lungs -> trachea -> oesophagus -> intestine
    2. (b) Outside - stomach -> heart -> lungs - trachea -intestine
    3. (c) Outside -> intestine -> heart -> lungs -> trachea -> intestine
    4. (d) Outside -> intestine
    (a) The migration route of Ascaris is from outside the host to the intestine, then to the liver, lungs, trachea, esophagus, and back to the intestine. This complex migration pattern is part of Ascaris' life cycle and enables it to complete its development.
  5. 5. Amphid and phasmid glands are found in:
    1. (a) Platyhelminthes
    2. (b) Annelida
    3. (c) Nematode
    4. (d) Cockroach
    (c) Amphid and phasmid glands are found in nematodes. These glands play a role in chemoreception and sensory perception, helping the nematodes detect their environment and locate potential hosts.
  6. 6. The wandering journey of Ascaris takes:
    1. (a) 10 days
    2. (b) 12 days
    3. (c) 14 days
    4. (d) 20 days
    (c) The wandering journey of Ascaris takes approximately 14 days. During this time, the larvae migrate through various organs and tissues, such as the liver and lungs, before returning to the intestine to mature into adult worms.
  7. 7. Which of the following diseases is caused by nematodes?
    1. (a) Filariasis
    2. (b) Amoebiasis
    3. (c) Leprosy
    4. (d) Malaria
    (a) Filariasis is a disease caused by nematodes. It is transmitted to humans through the bites of infected mosquitoes and can lead to severe swelling of body parts, particularly the limbs.
  8. 8. The space between the body wall and the alimentary canal of Ascaris is known as:
    1. (a) Haemocoel
    2. (b) Coelom
    3. (c) Pseudocoel
    4. (d) Enterocoel
    (c) The space between the body wall and the alimentary canal of Ascaris is known as the pseudocoel. It is a fluid-filled cavity that serves as a hydrostatic skeleton, providing support and enabling movement for the worm.
  9. 9. The animal in which sex can be differentiated from external morphology is:
    1. (a) Taenia
    2. (b) Krait
    3. (c) Ascaris
    4. (d) Sea anemone
    (c) Ascaris is an animal in which the sex can be differentiated from external morphology. Male and female Ascaris worms have distinct differences in their size and shape, with females being larger and having a rounded posterior end.
  10. 10. Filaria can be eradicated if the population of the following is exterminated:
    1. (a) Housefly
    2. (b) Anopheles mosquito
    3. (c) Cockroach
    4. (d) Culex mosquito
    (b) Filaria can be eradicated if the population of the Anopheles mosquito is exterminated. Anopheles mosquitoes are the primary vectors for transmitting filarial parasites to humans through their bites. Controlling the mosquito population and implementing measures to prevent mosquito bites are crucial in preventing the spread of filariasis.
  11. Amphids present in ventrolateral lips of Ascaris are:
    1. (a) Chemoreceptors
    2. (b) Gustatoreceptors
    3. (c) Olfactoreceptors
    4. (d) Tactoreceptors
    (a) Chemoreceptors. The amphids present in the ventrolateral lips of Ascaris are specialized sensory organs that function as chemoreceptors. They help the worm detect and respond to chemical signals in its environment, including cues related to food sources and potential hosts.
  12. Which one has no intermediate agent to cause disease?
    1. (a) Tapeworm
    2. (b) Roundworm
    3. (c) Plasmodium
    4. (d) Liverfluke
    (a) Tapeworm. Tapeworms do not require an intermediate agent to cause disease. They have a direct life cycle, where the eggs are passed in feces and ingested by the host, leading to infection. Intermediate agents, such as mosquitoes or snails, are involved in the life cycles of other parasites.
  13. The correct matching about the body wall of Ascaris is:
    1. (a) Flame cell and circular body
    2. (b) Pseudocoelom and longitudinal body muscle
    3. (c) Longitudinal muscle and syncytial epidermis
    4. (d) Syncytial epidermis and diploblastic body
    (c) Longitudinal muscle and syncytial epidermis. The body wall of Ascaris consists of longitudinal muscle layers that provide movement and a syncytial epidermis, which is a specialized type of epithelium formed by the fusion of multiple cells. This body wall structure enables Ascaris to adapt to its parasitic lifestyle.
  14. Which of the following parasites causes iron (Fe) deficiency anemia?
    1. (a) Pinworm
    2. (b) Roundworm
    3. (c) Hookworm
    4. (d) Tapeworm
    (c) Hookworm. Hookworm infection can lead to iron (Fe) deficiency anemia. Hookworms attach to the intestinal wall and feed on the host's blood, causing chronic blood loss. This can result in a deficiency of iron, an essential nutrient for red blood cell production.
  15. Which of the following penetrate through human skin?
    1. (a) E. histolytica
    2. (b) Roundworm
    3. (c) Hookworm
    4. (d) Tapeworm
    (c) Hookworm. Hookworm larvae have the ability to penetrate through human skin. They typically enter the body by burrowing into the skin of the feet when walking barefoot on contaminated soil. Once inside the body, the larvae migrate through the bloodstream and eventually reach the intestines.
  16. In hookworm, ingested blood is prevented from clotting by formed secreted by:
    1. (a) Esophageal gland
    2. (b) Salivary gland
    3. (c) Pharyngeal gland
    4. (d) Tonsilar gland
    (a) Esophageal gland. Hookworms have specialized glands, known as esophageal glands, that secrete anticoagulant substances. These substances prevent the ingested blood from clotting, allowing the hookworms to feed on the blood without interference.
  17. The alimentary canal of nematode is:
    1. (a) only mouth
    2. (b) only anus
    3. (c) mouth & anus
    4. (d) No intestine
    (c) mouth & anus. The alimentary canal of a nematode (roundworm) consists of both a mouth and an anus. The mouth is used for ingestion of food, while the anus is responsible for the elimination of waste materials after digestion.
  18. Hookworm infection causes the deficiency of:
    1. (a) vitamin B12
    2. (b) Vitamin A
    3. (c) Iron
    4. (d) Folic acid
    (c) Iron. Hookworm infection can cause iron deficiency anemia. The hookworms feed on the host's blood, leading to chronic blood loss. This can result in a decrease in iron levels in the body, leading to anemia.
  19. Filariform larva is the infective stage of:
    1. (a) Ascaris
    2. (b) Threadworm
    3. (c) Tapeworm
    4. (d) Hookworm
    (d) Hookworm. The filariform larva is the infective stage of hookworms. It is the third-stage larva that is capable of penetrating the skin and causing infection in the human host. Once inside the body, the filariform larva migrates to the intestines and develops into an adult hookworm.
  20. Which organism is NOT a blood parasite?
    1. (a) Plasmodium
    2. (b) Trypanosoma
    3. (c) Trichinella
    4. (d) L. donovani
    (c) Trichinella. Trichinella is not a blood parasite. It is a parasitic roundworm that causes the disease trichinellosis. Trichinella larvae invade the muscles of the host, rather than the bloodstream, and the infection is primarily transmitted through the consumption of raw or undercooked meat containing the larvae.
  21. After how many moulds does a round worm grow into an adult in the human intestine? [KU 2014]
    1. (a) 1
    2. (b) 2
    3. (c) 3
    4. (d) 4
    (a) The round worm grows into an adult in the human intestine after one moult.
  22. Anemia may be caused by? [KU 2012]
    1. (a) Hookworm
    2. (b) Roundworm
    3. (c) Earthworm
    4. (d) Tapeworm
    (a) Anemia may be caused by hookworm infestation.
