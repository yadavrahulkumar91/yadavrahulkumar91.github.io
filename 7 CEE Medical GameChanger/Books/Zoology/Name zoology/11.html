1. Centrolecithal eggs are found in:
    1. (a) Rabbit
    2. (b) Frog
    3. (c) Cockroach
    4. (d) Parrot
    (a) Rabbit. Centrolecithal eggs are eggs in which the yolk is concentrated in the center of the egg. This type of egg is found in animals like rabbits.
  2. Metamorphosis in insects is controlled by the hormone:
    1. (a) Pheromones
    2. (b) Thyroxine
    3. (c) Ecdysone
    4. (d) Juvenile
    (c) Ecdysone. Metamorphosis in insects is controlled by the hormone ecdysone. This hormone triggers the molting and development of different life stages in insects, such as larvae, pupae, and adults.
  3. Spider belongs to class:
    1. (a) Crustacea
    2. (b) Insecta
    3. (c) Arthropoda
    4. (d) Arachnida
    (d) Arachnida. Spiders belong to the class Arachnida, which includes other arachnids like scorpions, ticks, and mites. Arachnids are characterized by having eight legs and two main body segments.
  4. Which one is a parasite and also a vector?
    1. (a) Plasmodium
    2. (b) Flea
    3. (c) Housefly
    4. (d) Silkworm
    (a) Plasmodium. Plasmodium is a parasite that causes malaria in humans. It is transmitted through the bite of infected mosquitoes, making the mosquito a vector for the disease.
  5. Head of cockroach is formed by the fusion of:
    1. (a) 4 segments
    2. (b) 3 segments
    3. (c) 6 segments
    4. (d) 7 segments
    (b) 3 segments. The head of a cockroach is formed by the fusion of three segments, known as the cephalic segments. These segments contain the mouthparts and sensory organs of the cockroach.
  6. Haemocoel is found in:
    1. (a) Insects
    2. (b) Arachnida
    3. (c) Crustacea
    4. (d) Mollusca
    (a) Insects. Haemocoel is the main body cavity of insects, where the internal organs are bathed in hemolymph (the insect equivalent of blood). It functions in nutrient transport, waste removal, and circulation.
  7. Tracheal system of respiration is found in:
    1. (a) Insects
    2. (b) Crayfish
    3. (c) Millipede
    4. (d) Spider
    (a) Insects. The tracheal system is a network of tubes that carry air directly to the tissues in insects. It is a highly efficient respiratory system that allows for rapid gas exchange without the need for lungs or gills.
  8. Haemoglobin is not found in the blood of:
    1. (a) Frog
    2. (b) Cockroach
    3. (c) Rabbit
    4. (d) Earthworm
    (c) Rabbit. Haemoglobin is a protein found in the red blood cells of many animals, including humans, frogs, cockroaches, and earthworms. However, rabbits have a different type of respiratory pigment called myoglobin, which serves a similar function.
  9. Spiders' webs are webbed from:
    1. (a) Mouth
    2. (b) Leg
    3. (c) Spinnerets
    4. (d) Saliva
    (c) Spinnerets. Spiders produce their webs from specialized silk-producing organs called spinnerets, located at the posterior end of their abdomen. Spinnerets allow spiders to create different types of silk for various purposes, including building webs, capturing prey, and making egg sacs.
  10. Green glands are found in:
    1. (b) Insects
    2. (a) Crustaceans
    3. (d) Tadpole
    4. (c) Arachnids
    (b) Crustaceans. Green glands, also known as antennal glands or maxillary glands, are excretory organs found in many crustaceans. They play a role in osmoregulation and waste excretion by filtering waste products from the blood and releasing them into the surrounding water.
