1. 1. Diphyllobothrium of phylum Platyhelminthes is known as:
    1. (a) Fish tapeworm
    2. (b) Pork tapeworm
    3. (c) Dog tapeworm
    4. (d) Dwarf tapeworm
    (a) Diphyllobothrium of phylum Platyhelminthes is known as fish tapeworm.
  2. 2. Liver-rot is caused by:
    1. (a) Fasciola
    2. (b) Schistosoma
    3. (c) Taenia
    4. (d) Echinococcus
    (a) Liver-rot is caused by Fasciola.
  3. 3. Tapeworm using pig as an intermediate host is:
    1. (a) Taenia solium
    2. (b) Taenia saginata
    3. (c) Hymenolepis nana

    1. (a) Proglottid
    2. (b) Scolex

    1. (a) Annelida
    2. (b) Platyhelminthes
    3. (c) Porifera

  6. 6. Larva of tapeworm in pork meat is:
    1. (a) Rhabditiform
    2. (b) Cercaria

  11. 11. If a person has eaten not properly cooked pork there is a chance that he may suffer from?
    1. (a) Amoebiasis
    2. (b) Elephantiasis
    3. (c) Taeniasis
    4. (d) Cholera
    (a) If a person consumes improperly cooked pork, there is a risk of contracting amoebiasis. Amoebiasis is caused by the parasite Entamoeba histolytica, which can be present in undercooked pork.
  12. 12. Tape worm falls in which class?
    1. (a) Turbellaria
    2. (b) Trematoda
    3. (c) Cestoda
    4. (d) None
    (c) Tape worm falls in the class Cestoda. Cestodes are a class of parasitic flatworms commonly known as tapeworms.
  13. 13. What is the common name of Taenia solium?
    1. (a) Dog tape worm
    2. (b) Pork tape worm
    3. (c) Fish tape worm
    4. (d) Beef tape worm
    (b) The common name of Taenia solium is "Pork tape worm." Taenia solium is a parasitic flatworm that infects pigs and humans.
  14. 14. In which canal of the male is the female Schistosoma lodged?
    1. (a) Inguinal canal
    2. (b) Laurer's canal
    3. (c) Gynophoric canal
    4. (d) None
    (c) The female Schistosoma is lodged in the "Gynophoric canal" of the male. Schistosoma is a genus of trematodes, or flukes, that cause the disease schistosomiasis.
  15. 15. Where are Vitelline glands found?
    1. (a) Nereis
    2. (b) Pila globosa
    3. (c) Taenia solium
    4. (d) Fasciola hepatica
    (d) Vitelline glands are found in "Fasciola hepatica." Fasciola hepatica, also known as the common liver fluke, is a parasitic flatworm that infects the liver of various mammals, including humans.
  16. 16. Which parasite will show both alternation of generation and alternation of host?
    1. (a) Fasciola
    2. (b) Taenia
    3. (c) Ascaris
    4. (d) Ancyclostoma
    (a) The parasite "Fasciola" exhibits both alternation of generation and alternation of host. Fasciola is a genus of liver flukes that have a complex life cycle involving multiple hosts and generations.
  17. 17. What is a well-developed system in parasitic animals?
    1. (a) Reproductive system
    2. (b) Digestive system
    3. (c) Respiratory system
    4. (d) All of the above
    (d) Parasitic animals possess a well-developed "Reproductive system," "Digestive system," and "Respiratory system." These systems are adapted to their parasitic lifestyle and ensure their survival and reproduction.
  18. 18. In which class of Platyhelminthes is the alimentary canal absent?
    1. (a) Trematoda
    2. (b) Turbellaria
    3. (c) Cestoda
    4. (d) Anthozoa
    (b) The class Turbellaria of Platyhelminthes lacks an alimentary canal. Turbellarians are free-living flatworms, and their digestion occurs through a branching gastrovascular cavity.
  19. 19. What is correct about the hexacanth larva of a tapeworm?
    1. (a) It consists of 6 hooks for absorption
    2. (b) It consists of 6 hooks for boring cyst wall and release to grass
    3. (c) It bears 6 hooks for attachment to the intermediate host
    4. (d) Hooks are useless in the hexacanth larva
    (c) The hexacanth larva of a tapeworm bears 6 hooks for attachment to the intermediate host. These hooks help the larva to establish itself in the host's tissues and continue its development.
  20. 20. In which phylum is the pseudocoelom found?
    1. (a) Cnidaria
    2. (b) Nematoda
    3. (c) Platyhelminthes
    4. (d) Annelids
    (b) The pseudocoelom is found in the phylum "Nematoda." Nematodes, commonly known as roundworms, have a pseudocoelom that serves as a body cavity for various organ systems.

  21. Ectoparasite of fish falls under:
    1. (a) Monogenea
    2. (b) Turbellaria
    3. (c) Trematoda
    4. (d) Cestoda
    (a) Monogenea. The ectoparasites of fish are classified under the phylum Monogenea. They are characterized by having a simple life cycle and a direct association with their fish hosts. These parasites often attach to the skin or gills of fish and can cause various health issues.
  22. Which larval form of tapeworm is present in pork?
    1. (a) Rhabditiform
    2. (b) Metacercaria
    3. (c) Cysticercus
    4. (d) Cercaria
    (c) Cysticercus. The larval form of tapeworm present in pork is called Cysticercus. This stage of the tapeworm's life cycle is found in the muscles of the pig and can cause a parasitic infection known as cysticercosis if consumed by humans. Proper cooking of pork can kill the cysticerci and prevent infection.
  23. Which one of the following animal phyla does not possess a coelom?
    1. (a) Platyhelminthes
    2. (b) Mollusca
    3. (c) Annelida
    4. (d) Echinodermata
    (b) Mollusca. Unlike the other options, the phylum Mollusca does not possess a true coelom. Instead, they have a cavity called a "hemocoel" that functions as a circulatory system and is not lined by mesodermal epithelium. Mollusks include various animals like snails, clams, squids, and octopuses.
