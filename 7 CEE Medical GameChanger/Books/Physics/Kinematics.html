Definition of Motion in a Straight Line

  1. If a body is in uniform motion (velocity = constant) or body has acceleration and velocity acting at 0° or 180°, then the path followed is a straight line and the motion is Rectilinear motion.

Basic Formulas

  1. Distance and Displacement

    1. The length of the path followed by a moving body is called distance.

      It is a scalar quantity.

      It can never be zero or negative.

      For a moving body, distance always increases with time.

    2. The shortest distance between the initial and final position with direction for a moving body is called displacement.

      It is a vector quantity.

      It can be zero, negative, or positive.

      For a moving body, displacement may increase or decrease with time.

    Distance ≥ Displacement

The time rate of change in distance for a moving body is called speed.

It is a scalar quantity.

"$\text{speed} = \frac{\text{length of actual path (=distance)}}{\text{time taken}}$"

The speed of a body can never be negative or zero.

Uniform and Non-uniform Speed

Uniform speed Non-uniform speed

A body is said to be moving with uniform speed if it covers equal distances in equal intervals of time.

A body is said to be moving with a variable speed if it covers equal distances in unequal intervals of time or unequal distances in equal intervals of time.

Average and Instantaneous Speed

a) Average Speed - Instantaneous Speed

Average Speed Instantaneous Speed
  1. Average speed = $\frac{\text{distance covered in given time}}{\text{time taken}}$
  2. Formula: $v_{\text{av}} = \frac{x - x_0}{t - t_0} = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}$
  3. It is defined for a period of time.
  1. Instantaneous speed is the limiting value of average speed as the time interval tends to zero.
  2. Formula: $v_i = \lim_{x \to 0}\left(\frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}\right) = \frac{dx}{dt}$
  3. It is defined for a point of time.
