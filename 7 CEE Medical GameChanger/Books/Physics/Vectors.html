Grade 11 Vector Lesson

Triangle, Parallelogram, and Polygon Laws of Vectors

In vector algebra, we often encounter various shapes and their corresponding vector properties. Three important laws related to vectors in these shapes are the triangle law, parallelogram law, and polygon law.

  • Triangle Law: According to the triangle law of vectors, if two vectors are represented by two sides of a triangle, then the resultant vector is represented by the third side of the triangle taken in the opposite direction.
  • Parallelogram Law: The parallelogram law states that if two vectors are represented by two adjacent sides of a parallelogram, then the diagonal passing through their common point represents the resultant vector.
  • Polygon Law: The polygon law is an extension of the parallelogram law. It states that if vectors are represented by consecutive sides of a polygon taken in order, then the closing vector represents the resultant vector.

Resolution of Vectors; Unit Vectors

Vector resolution involves breaking down a vector into its components along specific axes or directions. By using trigonometry and the concept of right triangles, we can determine the magnitude and direction of these components. This process is essential for analyzing vectors in multiple dimensions.

Unit vectors are vectors that have a magnitude of 1. They are commonly used to represent directions. Unit vectors along the x, y, and z axes are denoted as {i}, {j}, and {k}, respectively.

Scalar and Vector Products

Scalar and vector products are two fundamental operations involving vectors.
