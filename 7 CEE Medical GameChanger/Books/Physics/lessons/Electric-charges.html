  2. Charges are produced due to actual transfer of electrons.

    2. Negative charge means excess (gain) of electrons.<

            3. For conductor, \( \epsilon_r = \infty \), therefore \( q_{\text{induced}} = -q_{\text{inducing}} (1 - 1/\infty) = -q_{\text{inducing}} \)
          2. Conclusion: \( q_{\text{induced}} \leq -q_{\text{inducing}} \)
          3. The negative sign indicates the opposite nature of induction
        3. In the inducing process, the net induced charge on a body will be zero.
        4. In the inducing process, both the charge and mass of the charged body remain the same, but the potential of the charging body decreases.

    5. Coulomb's Law

    1. The force of attraction or repulsion between two point charges is directly proportional to the product of charge and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them:
      1. \( F \propto \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \)
      2. \( F = \frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_0} \cdot \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \)
      3. \( \epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{C}^2 \, \text{N}^{-1} \, \text{m}^{-2} \) (Permittivity of free space)
      4. \( F_{\text{medium}} = \frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon} \cdot \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} = \frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_0 \epsilon_r} \cdot \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \)
      5. \( \epsilon_r = \frac{F_{\text{air}}}{F_{\text{medium}}} = \frac{\epsilon}{\epsilon_0} \) (Relative permittivity of free space or Dielectric constant of the medium)
      6. When two charges are held in air at distance \( r \), then:
      7. \( F_{\text{air}} = \frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_0} \cdot \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} = 9 \times 10^9 \cdot \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \)
    2. Coulomb's force is valid only for point charges.
    3. The magnitude of Coulomb's force at the location of two charges is always equal even if charges are equal or different, but acts in opposite directions (Newton's third law):
      • \( F_1 = -F_2 \)
    4. Coulomb's force doesn't depend on the mass of charge; the magnitude of Coulomb's force at the location of two charges is always the same even if the masses of the charges are different.
