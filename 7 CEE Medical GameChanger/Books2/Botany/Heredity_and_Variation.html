Phenotype and Genotype

  • A. Any distinguishable characteristics possessed by an individual/ organism is called Phenotype.
  • B. All phenotypes are not visible and some require special test for determination.
    • Eg: ABO blood group, IQ, RQ, etc
  • Phenotype is
    1. Character possessed by an organism
    2. Character possessed by an individual
    3. Character possessed by an species
    4. Character possessed by an population
    5. Character possessed by an community
Homozygous and Heterozygous individual
Homozygous individual Heterozygous individual
  1. An individual containing identical alleles of a gene or factors of a character on its homologous chromosomes.
  2. It gives rise to offspring having the same character on self breeding.
  3. It is of two types.
    1. Homozygous dominant (TT)
    2. Homozygous recessive (tt)
  1. An individual containing two contrasting factors of a character of two different alleles of a gene on its homologous chromosomes.
  2. It is not pure and is called hybrid for that character.
  3. Heterozygous does not breed true on self-fertilization. (Tt)
  • Human is hemizygous for Haemophilia and Colour blindness.
  • Total no. of chromosomes or total no. of genes preset in a haploid cell is called Genome.
  • Total no. of genes present in a population is called Gene pool.
  • Percentage of a particular gene in a gene pool is called as frequency of that particular gene.
  • Homozygous individual of a particular character constitute Pure line.
  • Mendel produced the pure line of Pisum sativum by Selfing.
