Marchantia: A Liverwort

Marchantia is a member of the hepaticopsida group of bryophyta and is commonly known as liverwort. It is found in Sciophytic condition and has dichotomous branching and prostrate growth.

Ventral Surface

On the ventral surface, Marchantia has both smooth-walled and tuberculated unicellular rhizoids and multicellular scales.

Types of Rhizoids Description
Smooth-walled Single-celled and smooth
Tuberculated wall Single-celled with a tuberculated wall

Dorsal Surface

On the dorsal surface, black spots (air pores) are present throughout the life cycle. However, during the reproductive season, gemma cups are produced.

The lower part of Marchantia is the storage part, which is compact and made up of parenchymatous tissue. The upper part is photosynthetic, consisting of smaller rows of chlorophyllous cells and air pores.

Reproduction

Marchantia reproduces both vegetatively and sexually. It is dioecious in nature.

Vegetative Reproduction

  • Fragmentation
  • By death and decay of older parts
  • By gemma: Each Gemma develops into two plants.

Sexual Reproduction

Marchantia reproduces sexually and is dioecious in nature.
