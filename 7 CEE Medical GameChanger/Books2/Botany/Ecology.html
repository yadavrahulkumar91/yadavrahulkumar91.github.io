Ecology

Ecology is the intricate web of relationships that bind together the living and non-living components of our planet, shaping the world we live in.

A. Terms given

  1. Term Ecology given by =

    1. Ernst Haeckel
      • Defined ecology as reciprocal relationship between living organism and their environment.
    2. Reiter
      • First authentic use

  2. Father of Ecology = Reiter

  3. Term Ecosystem = Tansley

  4. Father of Ecosystem = Odum

B. Branches of Ecology

  1. Autecology = Individual species (Population and its habitat)

  2. Syncology = Community

C. Speciation

  1. Patry

    • Species
    • Location
    • Developed character

  2. Alloopatry/ Geographical isolation

    • Individuals of same species
    • On different geographical location (with or without barrier)
    • Different morphological characters

  3. Sympatry/ Reproductive isolation

    • Individuals of same species
    • On same geographical location
    • Different morphological characters

Ecological laws

Allan's law

  • Tail, snout, ears and legs of mammals living in colder region are shorter.

Bergman's law

  • Mammals of colder region are larger.

Jordan's law

  • Fishes of cold water have more vertebrates.

Factors determining distribution of organisms - Ecological factors

Light

  1. Heliophytes = Sun loving plants
  2. Sciophytes = Shade loving plants
  3. Facultative heliophytes = Sciophytes that can grow in sunlight.
  4. Facultative sciophytes = Heliophytes that can grow in shade too.

Temperature

  • On the basis of tolerance
    • Eurythermal = Can tolerate a wide range of temperature.
    • Sternothermal = Can tolerate a narrow range of temperature.
  • On the basis of Degree of temperature
    • Megatherms = High temperature
      1. Desert
      2. Tropical rainforest
    • Mesotherms = High summer temperature and low winter temperature.
      1. Temperate vegetation
      2. Pond flora
    • Microtherm = Low temperature
      1. Conifer vegetation
    • Hekistotherms = Extremely low temperature
      1. Tundra vegetation

    N. Soil profile

    I. Nature and sequence of different layers of horizons in vertical section of soil is called soil profile.

    1. AOO horizon/ Litter region:
      • Having undecomposed organic matter.
    2. AO horizon/ Duff region:
      • Having partially decomposed organic matter.
    3. A1 horizon/ Humus layer:
      • Having fully decomposed organic matter.
    4. A2 horizon:
      • Having mineral particles along with little humus.
    5. B horizon:
      • Having only mineral particles.
    6. C horizon:
      • Having incompletely broken mineral particles.
    7. R horizon:
      • Having unbroken rocks.

    All these horizons constitute top soil which is 15-20 cm thick. C and R horizons collectively constitute sub-soil.
