Leaf

Roots are the silent but essential architects of life, anchoring plants to the ground and providing them with water and nutrients.

A. Introduction

  1. a. Leaf is a green, exogenous lateral outgrowth which is borne on the node of a stem or its branches.
  2. b. Leaves have twisted growth (as their growth is by intercalary meristem).
    • i. Growth of a leaf is limited as it grows by intercalary meristem that is consumed during growth.
  3. c. Three major function:
    • i. Photosynthesis
    • ii. Gaseous exchange
    • iii. Transpiration
  4. d. Prophyll = First leaf of plant
    • i. The first leaf originated from shoot meristem as leaf primordia.
  5. e. Phyllome = All types of leaves present in a plant.
  6. f. Plant with no leaf üçÉ
  7. g. Plant with single leaf = E.g: Monophyllea
    • i. It is cotyledonary leaf
  8. h. Plant with 2 leaves only = E.g: Welwitschia
  9. i. Plant with absorbing leaf = Lamina of Insectivorous plant
  10. j. Dicot leaf = Dorsiventral and horizontal
    • i. Abaxial surface/ Dorsal surface/ Lower üëá surface = Away from stem axis
    • ii. Adaxial surface/ Ventral surface/ Upper surface = Toward the stem axis
  11. k. Monocot leaf = Isobilateral and Vertical

B. Leaf classification

  1. I. On the basis of insertion
    • i. Radical
      1. Leaves arising above root system from condensed shoot
      2. Length of internodes short
      3. Eg
        • a) Radish
        • b) Carrot
        • c) Turnip
    • ii. Cauline
      1. Leaves born on the main stem
      2. Inflorescence stalk bears cauline leaves
      3. Eg
        • a) Mustard
        • b) Maize
        • c) Sugarcane
    • iii. Ramal
      1. Leaves found on the nodes of stem branches
      2. Eg
        • a) Mango
        • Peepal

      • Types of Leaves

      II. On the basis of life span

      • i. Caducous
        1. Leaf fall down soon after their appearance
        2. Eg.
          • a) Opuntia
      • ii. Deciduous:
        1. Leaf fall occurs simultaneously at the end of growing season, i.e., leaves attached for a single growing season
        2. Eg.
          • a) Acacia catechu
          • b) Dalbergia sisso
          • c) Mulberry
          • d) Populus
      • iii. Persistent
        1. Leaf live more than one season
        2. Eg.
          • i) Evergreen trees
            • a. Pinus
            • b. Ficus
            • c. Mango

      III. On the basis of origin and function

      • i. Foliage leaf
        1. These are normal green leaves i.e., photosynthetic leaf
      • ii. Reproductive leaf
        1. Producing adventitious root and shoot (bud) for regeneration.
        2. Eg.
          • a) Begonia
          • b) Bryophyllum
      • iii. Cotyledonary leaf
        1. Derived from cotyledon
        2. Stores food, provides nutrient to seedling
        3. It comes up during epigeal germination
        4. Eg.
          • a) Cucumber
          • b) Tamarindum
          • c) Castor
          • d) Beans
      • iv. Sporophyll
        1. Spore bearing leaf
        2. Eg.
          • a) Frond (Leaves of ferns)
      • v. Tropophyll
        1. With spore bearing leaf
      • vi. Cataphyll
        1. Cataphyll
          1. Protective leaves on underground stem
          2. Protects vegetative bud
          3. E.g.
            • Rhizome
            • Corm
        2. Scale leaves
          1. Reduced leaves found in cladode
          2. E.g.
            • Pinus
        3. Hypsophyll/ Bract
          1. Bracts of aerial part
          2. Protects floral bud
