Tissue System

Tissue systems provide the framework for plant growth and development, from the root to the leaf.

A. TISSUE SYSTEM

I. Epidermal tissue system

Forms outer covering of plants

It includes:

  1. i. Epidermis:

    • Epi means upon + derma means skin and hence it is a continuous outer layer interrupted by Stomata and Lenticels.
    • Generally single layered but multilayered epidermis is found in:
      • Phylloclades of Casuarina
      • Leaves of Nerium and Ficus
      • Hygroscopic roots of epiphytes
        • Velamen tissue found on the surface of hygroscopic root is multiple epidermis whose cells are dead.
    • Layer of cuticle is found on the epidermis of stem and leaves but absent in root.
      • Cuticle layer acts as protection to epidermis and checks excessive transpiration.
    • Epidermal cells are living and generally don't possess chloroplast except shade plants (Sciophytes), hydrophytes and some ferns, etc.
      • Further chloroplasts are present in guard cells (Specialized epidermal cells).
        • Note: Guard cells (living cell) even having chloroplast is heteromorphic due to the absence of RUBISCO.
    • Bulliform cells or Motor cells or Bubble like cells are found in upper epidermis of isobilateral leaves (monocot). It helps in rolling and unrolling of leaves (mainly rolling).
    • Outermost layer of root is known as Epiblema or Piliferous layer or Rhizodermis which is not cutinised or suberised and also don't contain stomata.
    • Stomata and lenticels are confined to Epidermis.
    • In grasses and Equisetum, silica is present in the epidermal cells.
    • Epidermal cells containing cystoliths (CaCO3) are called lithocysts.

  2. ii. Stomata:

    • Minute pores occur on epidermal surface of leaves and also some herbaceous stem.
    • Each stoma/pore is guarded by 2 specialized kidney shaped or bean shaped epidermal cells, called Guard cells.
      • Guard cells are also guarded by specialized epidermal cells called subsidiary cells/ accessory cells.
      • In some monocots like doob grass (Cynodon dactylon) and maize ðŸŒ½ etc. guard cells are dumb-bell or barbell-shaped.
  • In dicot, guard cells are kidney shaped.
  • In dicot leaves, stomata are scattered whereas in monocots these are scattered in rows. Further in dicots these are present generally on lower epidermis whereas in monocots on both surfaces.
  • In free-floating hydrophyes (eg. Lotus, Nymphea), stomata are present on upper epidermis, whereas in submerged hydrophytes, these are absent or non-functional (in algae and fungi, stomata are totally absent).

II. Ground tissue system:

  • Between epidermis and vascular tissue.
  • It comprises:
    • Hypodermis
      • Outermost layer of Cortex.
      • Collenchymatous in herbaceous dicot stem.
      • Sclerenchymatous in monocot stem.
    • Cortex
      • Present inside hypodermis.
      • It is usually parenchymatous with abundant intercellular space.
      • Innermost layer of cortex is called as Endodermis.
    • Endodermis/ Starch sheath:
      • Endodermis is single layered which separates cortex from stele.
      • Endodermis is a part of extrastelar ground tissue system.
      • The cells of endodermis are
        • barrel-shaped,
        • without inter-cellular spaces,
        • living and
        • containing starch.
        • Hence, known as starch sheath.
      • Radial and tangential wall (but mainly radial wall) of endodermal cells possess thickenings of lignin, suberin and cutin in the form of stripes or Casparian bands.

      Pericycle

      1. Single or Few layered
        1. In Smilax root (monocot), pericycle is multilayered and sclerenchymatous.
        2. In some cases like Sunflower stem, pericycle is heterogenous i.e, composed of both thick walled and thin walled cells.
      2. Thin walled parenchymatous or Rarely thick walled sclerenchymatous cells inside the endodermis.
      3. Pericycle is present in most of the roots except in roots of parasitic plants and hydrophytes.
      4. Thick-walled pericycle is mechanical in function whereas thin walled pericycle is storage in function.
      5. In dicot root, pericycle can also form Cambium.

      Medulla (pith) and medullary rays (pith rays)

      1. The core of plant axis is occupied by a mass of parenchymatous tissue and called as Medulla or Pith.
      2. Medulla radiated out and present between vascular bundle is called as Medullary rays or Pith rays.
      3. Medulla helps in storage of food while medullary rays help in lateral transportation.
      4. Pith is distinct in Dicot stem and Monocot root, scattered in Monocot stem but indistinct in Dicot root.
      5. In some monocots, as in Triticum, the central region of the stem is hollow and is called Pith cavity. Pith cavity is seen in Ricinus among dicots
      6. Pith is developed in Dicot stem and Monocot root.

      Conjunctive tissue

      1. Parenchymatous tissue present between Xylem and Phloem bundles.

      Part of ground tissue present outside endodermis is called Cortex and inside endodermis is called as Stele.

      • Stele includes all tissue inside the endodermis.
      • Stele in monocot is atactostele and in dicot is eustele.

      Endodermis is absent in monocot stem. Thus, ground tissue of monocot stem is not differentiated into cortex and stele.

      Undifferentiated ground tissue is met within

      1. Maize stem
      2. Cucurbita stem
      3. Sunflower stem
      4. Pisum stem

      III. Vascular tissue system:

      1. Central column of axis(root and stem) is called 'Stele' which is made of number of vascular bundles, which constitutes vascular tissue system.
      2. Stelar region includes all tissues inside pericycle.
      3. The vascular bundle is having Xylem, Phloem and Cambium (if present).
        • Cambium is a stripe of Primary meristem.
      4. Vascular bundle is mainly 3 types.
        1. Radial:
          • Xylem and Phloem occur in separate bundles, present in different radii, alternate to each other and equal in number.
          • Eg: Roots (Dicot and Monocot both).
        2. Conjoint:
          • Xylem and Phloem in same bundle and in same radius. E.g: Stem
          • 2 types
            1. Collateral:
              • Phloem lies towards outer side or pericycle and xylem towards the centre or pith.
                1. Open - Xylem and Phloem separated by Cambium layer. - Dicot stem
                2. Closed - Cambium is absent - Monocot stem
              • Conjoint, Collateral and Open - Dicot stem
              • Conjoint, Collateral and Closed - Monocot stem
            2. Bicollateral:
              • Phloem is present on both the sides of xylem.
              • The bundle is always Open.

      B. Root-stem transition:

      • I. Conversion of radial and exarch vascular bundle into conjoint and endarch vascular bundles is called as Root-stem transition.

      • II. Swinging of xylem occurs during this transition and Phloem remains stationary.

      • III. Root-stem transition zone is present in Hypocotyl.

      C. Origin of Lateral roots and Lateral branches of Stem:

      • I. Origin of lateral root is endogenous and is originated from pericycle.

      • II. Seat of origin of lateral root is situated just against Protoxylem.

      • III. Origin of lateral branches of stem is Exogenous.

      • IV. Lateral branches are originated from outer part of Cortex.

      1. Wide cortex made up of thin-walled cells.
      2. Vascular bundle is radial fashion.
      3. Exarch xylem.
      4. Distinct endodermis and passage cells.

      F. Histology of Leaf:

      1. In dicots, leaf lie horizontally and upper part facing sunlight is darker than the lower part. So, known as Dorsiventral leaf.

      2. In monocots, leaf lies almost vertically and so all surfaces are equally exposed to sunlight, hence are known as Isobilateral leaf.

      3. Anatomically, leaves can be divided into upper and lower epidermis, mesophyll and vascular bundle.

      4. Inverted vascular bundles i.e., xylem in the upper part and phloem in the lower part.

      5. Vascular bundles are

        1. Conjoint,
        2. Collateral and with Mesarch xylem
