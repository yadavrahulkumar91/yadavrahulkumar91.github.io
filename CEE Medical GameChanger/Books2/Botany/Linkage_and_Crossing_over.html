Sex Linked Inheritance

The inheritance of sex linked traits (traits on sex chromosome) is called sex-linked Inheritance.

X-Linked trait was 1st observed by T.H. Morgan in Drosophila seeing white eye fly among red eye flies.

The pattern of inheritance of X-linked or sex-linked gene is the criss-cross inheritance.

X-linked gene is transmitted from father to grandson through daughter.

In human beings, sex linked inheritance are:

X-linked Inheritance

  1. Colour blindness (Red-Green Blindness)/ Daltonism

    It is the disease due to defective recessive gene on X-chromosome.

    Unable to distinguish Red and green colour due to absence of colour - sensitive cells in retina, they appear grey.

    The disease is transmitted from colour blind man through his daughter to half of persons.

  2. Haemophilia (Bleeder's Disease)/ Royal Disease

    Haemophilia is due to the presence of recessive gene on X-chromosome.

    Lacking of clotting factor in the blood. i.e. (factor VIII).

    Normal blood clotting time is (2-8) minutes but in haemophilic patients it takes about 30 min - 24 hrs.

    Common in royal families of England and Russia.

    The pattern of inheritance of haemophilia was described by Nasse which is called as Nasse's law.

    The haemophilic son is non-viable (doesn't attain sexual maturity).

    In haemophilia, males are the sufferers whereas females are the carriers.

  3. Muscular distrophy
