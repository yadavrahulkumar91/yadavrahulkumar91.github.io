Pteriodophyta

Bryophyta are the pioneers of the plant world, colonizing even the most barren of landscapes with their hardy and adaptable forms.

Pteridophyta

1. Pteridophyta is 1st vascular land plant.

Vascular bundle

a. Due to absence of Cambium, secondary growth is absent except Isoetes.

  • i. Secondary growth is present in Isoetes.

b. Phloem is without companian cell.

c. Sieve tube is -ve and sieve cell is +ve.

3. Main plant body is diploid and sporophytic (Both homosporous and Heterosporous).

Habitat

It is found on Land except:

  1. a. Azolla
    • i. Smallest pteridophyta
    • ii. Leaf is biofertilizer
  2. b. Marsellia
  3. c. Salvinia
    • i. Salvinia is rootless pteridophyta.

5. Pteridophyta is known as Reptiles of plant kingdom/ Botanical snakes.

6. Size

  • They are Small shaped herb like except,
  • a. Alsophylla = Largest/Tallest pteridophyta
  • b. Cythea = Tree fern

Plant differentiation

a. Root

  • i. Root is generally adventitious type.

b. Stem and

  • i. Underground Rhizome.
  • Note:
    1. Rhizomorph = Root like structure helps in absorption found in Fungi.
    2. Rhizines = Lichen
    3. Rhizoid = Root like found in Bryophyta.
    4. Rhizophore =
      • a) It is axis present between root and stem and found in Selaginella.
      • b) Rhizophore is organ of Suigeneris.
        • ◊ Sui generis is a Latin phrase that means "of its/his/her/their own kind", "in a class by itself", therefore "unique".

c. Leaf

  • i. Tropophyll = Photosynthetic leaf
  • ii. Sporophyll = Spore producing leaf
  • Sporophyll contain Sporangium.
