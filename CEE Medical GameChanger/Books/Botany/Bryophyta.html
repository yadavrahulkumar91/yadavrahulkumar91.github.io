Bryophyta

Bryophyta are the pioneers of the plant world, colonizing even the most barren of landscapes with their hardy and adaptable forms.

Study of Bryophta is called Bryology.

Reproduction

Either by Vegetative or Sexual method.

Vegetative reproduction

  1. By fragmentation
  2. By death and decay of older part
    • In this method, when older part becomes dead then two younger parts separated and develops into new plants.
  3. By Gemma:
    • On the dorsal surface of plant, Gemma cup are produced. Inside the gemma cup, gemma are formed. Each gemma develops into 2 plants.
  4. Apospory
    • Formation of Gametophyte directly from the tissue of sporophyte without formation of Spore.
  5. Apogamy
    • Formation of Sporophyte directly from tissue of gametophyte without formation of gamete.
  6. Parthenogenesis
    • When gamete directly develops into sporophyte or fruit without fertilization.
    • Note:
      • Parthenocarpy is used for fruit for making seedless fruit.
  7. By Bulbil formation
    • At the base of plant, near rhizoids rounded structure is formed called bulbils, when separates, it develops into new plant.

Sexual reproduction
