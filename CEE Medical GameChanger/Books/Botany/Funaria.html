Funaria

A fascinating branch of biology that studies the structure, function, and behavior of cells.

Funaria Information

  1. Funaria is member of Bryopsida and is true moss.
  2. Plant is found in Sciophytic condition.
  3. Plant is dimorphic.
    • Protonema
      • The young stage is Protonema, which is haploid and filamentous.
    • Adult plant
      • Rhizoid
        • Rhizoid is multicellular and branched.
        • It is root-like in nature.
      • Axis
        • Axis consist of epidermis (consist of Protoplast) and central cylinder.
        • It is stem-like in nature.
      • Phylloid
        • Phylloid consist of midrib and wing.
        • It is leaf-like in nature.
      • In Axis and Phylloid, stomata is absent.
      • Capsule
        • Stomata is present only in Capsule.
        • Chlorophyll is also present in Capsule.
  4. Reproduction
    • Vegetative reproduction:
      • By fragmentation
      • By bulbil formation
      • By Apospory
      • By protonema

Sexual reproduction

Antheridium Archegonium

  • Antheridium

    Antheridium is protected by Perigonial leaf.

    1. Both antheridium or archegonium protected by Paraphysis.
    2. Male axis consist antheridium which produces biflagellated and curved male gametes.
      • a. Male
    1. Chemotactic movement of male gamete is due to Sucrose or Cane sugar.

  • Archegonium

    Archegonium is protected by Perichaetial leaf.

    1. Both antheridium or archegonium protected by Paraphysis.
    2. Archegonium is flask shaped and consist of
      1. 1 Egg cell,
      2. 1 Venter Canal cell (VCC) &
      3. 6-10 Neck Canal Cell (NCC)

Fertilization is internal.

Zygote → Embryo which develops into sporophyte. Main part of sporophyte is Capsule.

Capsule

  1. Uppermost part of Capsule is Operculum.
  2. Lowermost part of Capsule is Apophysis
    1. (consist of stomata and chloroplast).
  3. Central sterile part is Columella
    1. (helps in condution).
  4. Uppermost part of theca is Annulus.
    1. Annulus helps in dehiscence of capsule.
    2. Annulus helps in spore dispersal.
  5. The no. of peristome teeth is 32 which is arranged in 2 rows i.e., 16+16.
    1. Peristome teeth is hygroscopic in nature and takes part in spore dispersal.
  6. When spores comes out then germinate to produce protonema (Juvenile stage) and develops into adult gametophytic plant.
    1. Foot → Attachment of Sporophyte to Gametophye
    2. Seta → Dispersal of Spores
    3. Capsule → Holds the capsule
