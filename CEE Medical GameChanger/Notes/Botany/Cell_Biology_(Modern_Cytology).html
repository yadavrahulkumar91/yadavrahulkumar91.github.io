Cell Biology

A fascinating branch of biology that studies the structure, function, and behavior of cells.

What is Cell Biology?

Cell Biology, also known as Cytology, is the scientific study of cells. It focuses on the structure, function, and behavior of cells, which are the basic units of life. Cell Biology is an important field of study because cells are the building blocks of all living organisms, from the simplest bacteria to complex multicellular organisms like humans.

Cell Biology encompasses a wide range of topics, including cell division, cellular respiration, cell signaling, and cell metabolism. It also involves the study of cell organelles like mitochondria, ribosomes, and the endoplasmic reticulum, as well as the study of cell membranes and the cytoskeleton.

E. Cell Dividing capacity

a. Cellular totipotency (Latin: Totus = all; potens = power)

  • i. Capacity of individual or single, nucleated, living vegetative plant cell to regenerate whole plant.
  • ii. Idea of cellular totipotency was firstly given by Haberlandt.
  • iii. Phenomenon of cellular totipotency was proved experimentally by Steward and his co-worker.
    2. He used phloem tissue from roots of carrot as an explant.
    3. Used coconut milk in his experiment as liquid media.
      • a) Coconut milk naturally contain cytokinin which induce cell division.
      • i) So, cytokinin(zeatin) is also called coconut milk factor.
      • ii) Zeatin/ Cytokinin/ Kinetin is also rich in immature maize grain.

b. Pluripotency:

  • i. Pluripotency is the potential ability of an animal cell to develop into any type of the cell in the animal body but not into whole animal.
  • ii. Stem cells are only totipotent in animal.

c. Cell Ghosts:

  • i. Erythrocyte empties; named so because they are difficult to observe under light microscope due to absence of contents.
  • ii. Erythrocyte contents come out when a drop of blood is placed in water.
  • iii. It causes rupturing of erythrocytes or haemolysis.
