Cycas

Cycas is a member of Cycadopsida.

  • Cycas revoluta = Native to Nepal

The plant is found in physical xerophytic conditions.

  • Physical xerophytic = Hot â™¨ climate/ Tropical

The plant body is an unbranched tree and differentiated into:

  • Root
    • Coralloid root is -ve geotropic or apogeotropic and consists of Nostoc and Anabaena symbiotically.
  • Stem
    • Stem is solid, cylindrical and unbranched called as Caudex type.
    • Due to persistent leaf base, leaf scars are found around the stem.
    • Wood is porous and soft which is commercially less important.
  • Leaf
    • Leaf shows xerophytic conditions like thick cuticle and sunken stomata.
  1. Male plant consist of Male cone where microspore or pollen grain is produced.
  2. Female plant doesn't contain female cone but megasporophyll are loosely arranged in rossate form.
  3. Ovules of cycas is Orthotropous, Unitegmic and Largest among plant kingdom.
  4. Ovule consist of
    1. Pollen chamber
    2. Archegonial chamber
    3. Archegonia without Neck Canal Cell (NCC)
      1. 2 Neck cell (Cover cell) is +ve.
  5. Male gamete of Cycas is top-shaped, multiflagellated and largest among plant kingdom.
      1. 4-5 spiral bands of cilia.
  6. Pollination is anemophilous type which take place at 3-celled stage.
    1. 1 prothallial cell (PC)
    2. 1 Generative cell (GC) and
    3. 1 Tube cell (TC)
  7. Fertilization is zooidiogamous type. (Intermediate between zooidiogamy and Siphonogamy found).
  8. Pollen tube is formed but not carried the male gamete. Only work as Haustorium (absorb liquid).
  9. After fertilization, ovule develops into 2 cotyledons.
