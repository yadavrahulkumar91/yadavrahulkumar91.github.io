Pinus

A fascinating branch of biology that studies the structure, function, and behavior of cells.

2. Plant body

Plant body of Pinus is sporophyte divided into Root, Stem and Leaves.

a. Root

  • Tap root and Lateral root both are developed.
  • Vascular bundle
    • Diarch to tetrach
    • Radial type
    • Protoxylem = Exarch
      • Resin canal found in between patches of protoxylem form
    • Pith absent or Reduced. (Protostele)

b. Stem

  • It has one main axis of growth i.e Growth pattern = Monopodial
  • Main stem is monopodial and excurrent.
  • Two types of branches/ Stem of Pinus is dimorhpic
    • Long branch/ Branch of unlimited growth
    • Short branch/ Dwarf branch/ Branch of limited growth → falls at maturity
      • Needle like foliage leaves only found on dwarf shoots.
        • No. of needles = 1-5 per dwarf shoot
        • Have Thick, Waxy cuticle with sunken stomata.
      • Cataphylls/ Scale leaves = Found on both long and dwarf shoots.
        • Small, brown.
    • Wavy due to presence of leaf bases.
    • Layers from outside to inside → Cuticle, Epidermis, Cortex zone, Endodermis, parenchymatous
      • Pericycle → Multilayered
      • Pith → Parenchymatous
    • Vascular bundle
      • Conjoint
      • Collateral
      • Open endarch
    • Wood
      • Monoxylic and Pycnoxylic (Compact and hard)
      • Stele = Eustele
      • Secondary growth is normal
      • Tracheids = Posses bordered pits

      Male Cone

      Male cone develop in the axils of scale leaves found in Clusters.

      Female Cone

      Female cone arise in the axils of scale leaves at the tip of new strong shoots at some distance from the male cones. They grow in about 3 years.

      Female cone is considered as Modified inflorescence due to presence of bract.

      Microsporangia produced on lower side (abaxial side) of microsporophyll. Development of sporangium is Eusporangiate.

      Megasporophyll of female cone consists:

      • Ovuliferous scale
        • Each ovuliferous scale bears 2 naked ovules towards the basal region on upper(adaxial) side.
        • Ovules/ Megasporangia = Unitegmic and Anatropous type.
        • Megaspores are formed by meiosis in megaspre mother cell (MMC).
        • Functional megaspore germinate to form female gametophyte bearing archegonia (2 to 5 in no).
        • Archegonium consists of 4+4=8 neck cell
          • 1 Venter Canal Cell (VCC)
          • 1 Egg cell
          • 0 Neck Canal cell(NCC)
      • Bract scale

      1. Pollen tube acts as pollen carries.

      2. Pollen grains are winged (wings develop from exine).

      • Winged Pollen grain = Pinus (Develops from exine)
      • Winged seed = Moringa
      • Winged fruit = Hiptage & Shorea

      3. Pollination takes place at 4-celled stage (2 prothallial cell, 1 generative cell, 1 tube cell).

      4. During pollination the atmosphere becomes yellowish called Shower of sulphur.

      5. Body cell divides to form 2 passive male gametes.

      6. Pollen tube bursts, the two gametes are liberated only one of these fuses with the egg nucleus, the other degenerates.

      Male gamete is non-motile, Winged.
