Mendels Laws of Inheritance

Heredity and variation drive the endless diversity of life, from the tiniest microbe to the largest mammal.

G.J. Mendel

Birthplace: Austria (now Czeckoslovakia)

Duration of experiment: 1856-1864 (8 yrs)

Experimental material: Pisum sativum i.e., Garden pea (2n=14)

Mendel did unsuccessful experiment on Hawkweed (Hieracium).

  1. This plant was cross pollinated plant and reproduced by Parthenogenesis.

Mendel presented his work in Brunn Natural history society.

His paper was entitled as "Experiments in plant hybridization".

His work was rediscovered in 1900 by 3 scientists independently:

  1. Hugo de Vries
  2. Correns
  3. Tschermark

His work was republished in "Flora" (in 1901).

Mendel selected following 7 pairs of Contrasting character of Pisum sativum for his study.

Contrasting characters of Pisum sativum
Trait/ Character Dominant character Recessive character
Height of plant/ Stem length Tall Dwarf/ Short
Pod colour Green Yellow

Cotyledon colour Yellow Green

Flower position Axial Terminal

Reasons for choosing pea plant:

  1. Well defined distinct contrasting characters
  2. Self-fertilization
  3. Hermaphrodite
  4. Short-life span
  5. Hybridisation is easy

Mendel was successful in formulating the laws of inheritance whereas his predecessors were not because:

  1. He considered single trait at a time
  2. The characters he considered were present on separate chromosomes and shows no linkage. [IOM 1996]
    • a. But now, it has been proved (by Blext) that the 7 traits are present on only 4 different chromosomes (chromosome 1, 4, 5 and 7).
    • b. Due to greater distance between the genes, there was no linkage.
  3. He made statistical analysis.

B. Experiments of Mendel

I. Monohybrid cross

  1. If the cross involves the single pair of contrasting character, it is considered as monohybrid cross.
  2. Eg: Cross between tall plant and Dwarf plant i.e., plant height.
  3. Law of dominance and Law of Segregation can be explained on the basis of monohybrid cross.
  5. In Monohybrid cross
    • Phenotypic ratio= 3:1
    • No. of phenotypes = 2 (tall and Dwarf)
    • Genotypic ratio = 1:2:1
    • No. of genotypes = 3 (Pure tall, hybrid tall, Pure dwarf)
  6. Facts
    • No. of homozygous dominant as well as no. of homozygous recessive always remains 1 in F2 generation of any type of hybridization cross.
    • Proportion of homozygous dominant or homozygous recessive in F2 generation = 1/4n.
  7. Law of dominance
    • When two plants having one pair of contrasting characters are crossed only one of the 2 parental characters appears in heterozygous genotype of the offsprings of F1 generation.
    • Phenomenon of dominant can't be studied in haploids. (Eg. Algae, Fungi and Bryophytes)
    • Exception of Law of Dominance
      1. Incomplete dominance
      2. Co-dominance

1. Law of Segregation/ Law of Splitting of hybrid/ Law of purity of gamete/ Mendel's first law

  1. a. Dominant and recessive factor which stay together in hybrid offspring of F1 generation do not mix or contaminate or blend with each other and gets segregated from each other during anaphase I of meiosis. This is called as Law of segregation.
  2. b. Also known as Purity of gametes because each gamete is pure after segregation i.e., either T or t.
  3. c. Evidence is production of 2 types of phenotypes in F2 generation.
  4. d. Considered is a Universal law.
  5. e. Law of segregation justifies that gametes never become hybrid.
  6. f. Law of purity of gametes justifies that genes are particulate in nature.
    • Segregation of dominant or recessive factors in Pisum sativum occurs during
      1. Gamete formation
      2. Spore formation
      3. Zygote formation
      4. Fertilization
    • Segregation of dominant or recessive factors in Pisum sativum occurs during
      1. Gamete formation
      2. Zygote formation
      3. Parthenogenesis
      4. Fertilization
    • Segregation of dominant or recessive factors in Pisum sativum occurs during
      1. Metaphase-I
      2. Anaphase-I
      3. Anaphase-II
      4. Both b and c

II. Dihybrid cross

  1. If the cross involves the two pairs of contrasting characters, it is considered as dihybrid cross.
  2. 2 alleles and 4 characters
  3. In Dihybrid cross
    • a. Phenotypic ratio = 9:3:3:1
    • b. No. of phenotypes = 4
    • c. Genotypic ratio = 1:2:1:2:4:2:1:2:1
    • d. No. of genotype = 9
    • e. Results of Dihybrid cross in F2 generation:
      1. Ratio between non-recombinant to recombinant offsprings = 5:3
      2. No. of offspring having both the traits dominant = 9
      3. Genotypic ratio of the offspring having both the traits dominant = 1:2:2:4
      4. No. of the offspring having genotype like their parents: 4
      5. Ratio of parental offsprings: 10:16
      6. Ratio of recombinant offspring: 6:16
      7. Ratio of homozygous offspring: 4:16

      Law of Independent Assortment/ Mendel's 2nd law

      • It is conclusion of dihybrid cross.
      • According to law of independent assortment, when two pairs of contrasting characters are considered in hybridization, all characters assort (preparation in group) in an independent manner.
      • Exception of this law is Linkage.
      • Note:
        • Recombinant produced in dihybrid cross of Mendel are result of Independent Assortment. But, recombinant produced in linkage are result of crossing over.
        • Mendelian principles cannot be applied on prokaryotes where sexual reproduction is absent.

      C. Special points

      I. When two non-allelic genes are present in separate non-homologous chromosomes they are called as independently assorting gene.

      II. Independently assorting gene gives the result of independent assortment.

      III. When two non-allelic genes are present on same chromosome, they are called as Linked gene.

      IV. Linked gene gives the result of Linkage. [IOM 2009]

      V. When linked genes are present far apart i.e., frequency of crossing over becomes 50%, linked gene start to give the result of independent assortment in place of linkage.

      VI. Penotypic ratio = Genotypic ratio in test cross →2^n

      1. Monohybrid cross = 1:1

      2. Dihybrid cross = 1:1:1:1

      VII. Formulae and Methods as follows

      1. No. of offsprings in F2 generation = 4^n

      2. Type of gamete produced from homozygous genotype → Always one

      a. Eg: AABBCC → 1

      b. Aabbccdd → 1

      3. Type of gamete produced from heterozygous genotype = 2^n.

      a. Eg: AaBbCc→2^3=8

      b. AaBBCcddEe→2^3=8

      4. No. of homozygous dominant gamete as well as homozygous recessive gamete produced from any type of hybrid is always 1.

      5. Proportion of homozygous dominant and homozygous recessive gamete =1/2^n

      6. Phenotypic ratio in F2 generation →(3:1)^n, {n→type of cross}

      Importance of Mendelism

      • Different breeds in animals and different varieties in plant are possible due to Mendelism.
      • Science of Eugenics (Development of superior progeny) is based on Mendelism.
      • Heterosis or Hybrid vigour has been utilized in plants and animals is based on Mendelism.

      Crosses

      Reciprocal cross:

      1. Two crosses in which one dominant character is taken from Female(♀) parent and recessive character from male (♂) and second cross is vice-versa.
      2. Reciprocal cross gives same result. It proves that sex has no effect on phenotype.
      3. Exceptions:
        • When reciprocal cross gives different result
          1. Sex linked inheritance
          2. Cytoplasmic inheritance/ Uniparental inheritance/ Maternal inheritance

      Back cross

      1. Cross between F1 hybrid and parent (either dominant or recessive) i.e., between Tt and TT or Tt and tt. [MOE 2009]

      Test cross:

      1. Back cross of hybrid with homozygous recessive is called as test cross or cross between F1 hybrid and homozygous recessive parent i.e., between Tt and tt.
      2. Test cross is used to differentiate heterozygous from homozygous dominant.
      3. Selfing can be used in place of test cross to test purity of an individual having dominant character.
      4. Animal breeders use back cross of hybrid with homozygous dominant to produce pure line.
      5. Pure lines consists of homozygous individuals of a particular character.

      Out cross:

      1. Cross between F1 hybrid and dominant parent i.e., cross between Tt and TT.
      2. Cross of a hybrid tall with dwarf pea plant is called as
        1. Back cross
        2. Self cross
        3. Test cross
        4. Reciprocal cross
        5. Hybrid cross
