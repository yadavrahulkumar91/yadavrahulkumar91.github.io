Lichens

Thallophyta are a diverse group of organisms that include everything from single-celled algae to complex multicellular seaweeds.

Introduction

Study of Lichens is called as Lichenology.

Lichens are symbiotic association between Algae (Green or Blue green algae) and Fungi (Ascomycetes and Basidiomycetes).

  • Algal component/partner called Phycobiont.
    1. E.g: Blue green algae, Green algae
    2. Note: Chlorophyll of algal component absorbs SO2 by replacing Mg++ ions and forms a toxic substance called "Phycophytin" which caused death of lichen.
      • Lichens do not grow near big cities because lichens are very sensitive to air pollution or Smoke or especially SO2 pollution.
    3. Lichens are also good absorbers of Radioactivity.
  • Fungal component/partner called Mycobiont.
    1. E.g.: Ascomycetes, Basidiomycetes

Lichens have distinct structure different from algae and fungal partner.

Lichens shows very slow growth.

Lichens are composite or dual organisms.

Lichens also called as "Fungi parasitizing algae".

The relationship of Fungi and Algae as Master and Slave respectively is called Helotism.

  • Accordingly, fungus performs
    1. Absorption,
    2. Protection and
    3. Reproduction while
  • Algae only do photosynthesis.

The plant body in lichen is a thallus, irregular shaped and differently coloured.

Classification of Lichens

Based on Habitat:

  • Saxicolous: Lichens which grow in rocks/stones.
  • Terricolous: Lichens which grow on soil.
  • Corticolous: Lichens which grow on bark of trees.
  • Lignicolous: Lichens which grow on wood.

Based on Fungal Partner/ Component:

  • Ascolichen: Fungal partner belongs to ascomycetes.
    • Majority of lichens are ascolichens.
    • Gymnocarpae: When fruiting body is Apothecium type (cup-shaped).
    • Pyrenocarpae: When fruiting body is Perithecium type (flask-shaped).
    • Note: In ascolichens, algal partner is mostly member of green algae and rarely blue-green algae.
  • Basidiolichens: Fungal partner belongs to basidiomycetes.
    • In basidiolichens, algae partner is generally "blue-green algae".
  • Special Points:
    • In 80% case of lichen, algal partner is member of green algae or chlorophyceae and in 20% cases blue-green algae or myxophyceae.
    • Important members of green algae in lichens are:
      1. Trebauxia
      2. Cladophora
      3. Pleurococcus
    • Common blue-green algae are:
      1. Anabaena
      2. Nostoc
      3. Scytonema
    • Most of lichens are association of Green algae and Ascomycetes.

Structure of Thallus and Its attachment

Crustose:

  1. Entire thallus is attached to the substratum so that it cannot be separated without damaging it.
  2. Examples:
    1. Graphis
    2. Lecanora
    3. Dermatocarpon
    4. Rhizocarpon

Foliose:

  1. Thallus is lobed (leafy) and is attached to the substratum with the help of Rhizines.
  2. Examples:
    1. Parmelia (Rock flower)
    2. Peltigera (Dog lichen)
    3. Physcia
  3. Uses:
    1. Parmelia is used as Curry powder.
    2. Peltigera is used as medicine for Hydrophobia.
    3. Parmelia is also used to treat Epilepsy. (IOM 2009)

Fructicose Lichens

Fructicose lichens are much branched lichens with a bushy appearance and are attached to the substratum by a disc. Examples include:

  1. Usnea - Old man's beard
    • Usnic acid, an important broad-spectrum antibiotic, is obtained from Usnea (old man's beard).
    • Usnea is responsible for spreading fires in forests in hot seasons.
  2. Cladonia - Reindeer moss
    • Cladonia in arctic regions serves as food for reindeer.
    • Cladonia is used as medicine for Whooping cough.
  3. Cetraria - Iceland moss
    • Cetraria (Iceland moss) is edible in Iceland.
    • Cetraria is used as medicine for Diabetes.

Note: Racodium is a filamentous lichen.

Algal component

  1. Chlorophycophilous lichens: algal partner belongs to chlorophyceae (green algae).
  2. Cyanophycophilous lichens: algal partner belongs to cyanophyceae (blue green algae).

Anatomy to thallus

  1. Homoimerous:
    1. Algal and fungal partners are uniformly distributed throughout the lichen thallus.
    2. They are rare.
    3. E.g: Crustose.
  2. Heteromerous:
    1. Thallus consisting distinct algal and fungal zone.
    2. Most of the lichens.
    3. E.g: Foliose, Fructicose

Some special structure associated with lichen thallus

  1. Breathing pores:
    1. Loosely arranged area in upper cortex are breathing pores and they help in gaseous exchange.
  2. Cyphellae:
    1. Loosely arranged areas in lower cortex are cyphellae and these also helps in gaseous exchange.
    2. Cyphellae are analogous to stomata of higher plants.
  3. Cephalodia:
    1. Cephalodia retain moisture.
  4. Isidia:
    1. Isidia increase the surface area of lichen and are means of Vegetative reproduction.

Reproduction in Lichens:

  1. Vegetative reproduction:
    1. Fragmentation
    2. Isidia:
      1. Isidia is outgrowth of lichen thallus which after detachment can form new thallus.
    3. Soredia:
      2. These are granular structure produced in large number at a particular patch of lichen thallus called Soralium.
        • Soredia is algal cells surrounded by fugal hyphae. Soredia after separation from parent thallus can regenerate new body.
      3. Q. A group of algal cells imprisoned in fungal mycelium serving for vegetative multiplication of a lichen, is called
        1. Isidium
        2. Soredium
        3. Cephalodium
        4. Helotism
  2. Asexual reproduction:
    1. By the formation of pycnidiospores or pycniospores which are produced inside pycnidium which is a flask-shaped structure opening through a pore called Ostiole.
  3. Sexual reproduction:
    1. Sexual reproduction is a function of fungal partner only, algae has no role.
    2. Male sex organ is called Spermatogonium and inside it a number of non-motile sperms or spermatia produced.
    3. Female sex organ is carpogonium which is having lower coiled portion called Ascogonium and upper straight portion coming out though upper cortex called Trichogyne.
    4. After fertilization, fruiting body is formed. Fruiting body is either apothecium type (Cup 🥤 shaped) or perithecium (flask shaped).
