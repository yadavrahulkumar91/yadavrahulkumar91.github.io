Respiratory Quotient

Respiratory quotient is defined as the ratio of volume of CO2 evolved to O2 consumed in respiration.

R.Q. = (Volume of CO2 evolved) / (Volume of O2 consumed).

Respiratory quotient is measured by Ganong's respirometer.

Respiratory quotient depends on respiratory substrate:

  1. RQ = 1 for carbohydrates. [Floating respiration]
  2. RQ < 1 for lipid, fats (0.7) and Proteins (0.8-0.9) [Protoplasmic respiration]
  3. RQ > 1 for organic acids like
    1. Malic acid (COOH)
    2. Oxalic acid
  4. RQ = 0 for Succulent plants like
    1. Bryophyllum
    2. Opuntia
    3. Agave
    4. Aloe
  5. RQ = ∞ for Anaerobic respiration because O2 is not used. Example:
    1. Yeast
