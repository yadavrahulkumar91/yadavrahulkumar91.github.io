Dryopteris

A fascinating branch of biology that studies the structure, function, and behavior of cells.

Life Cycle

  1. Dryopteris is member of Pteropsida group of Pterdiphyta and called as Fern.
  2. The plant is found in Sciophytic condition = Shaded area.
  3. The main plant body is diploid and sporophytic (homosporous).
  4. Plant is differentiated into
    • Root
    • Stem
    • Leaf
  5. Root
    • Root is adventitious type.
  6. Stem
    • Stem is underground and dichotomously branched called as Rhizome.
    • Stele = Dictyostele
  7. Leaf
    • Leaf is large sized, feather like pinnately compound type called as Fronds.
    • Brown colour hair like structure(brownish scaly leaf) present on rhizome and rachis called as Ramenta.
    • Young leaf shows twisting called as "Circinate vernation" or "Circinate ptyxis".

Reproduction

  1. Vegetative reproduction
    • Fragmentation
    • Apogamy
      1. Apogamous sporophyte of fern is haploid.
  2. Sexual reproduction
    • Plant is sporophytic. So sporangium are produced on leaf.
    • Sporangium are found in group called Sorus.
      1. Sorus is protected by kidney shaped structure outgrowth of placenta called as Inducium.
        • In case of Pteris, coenosporus present which is protected by false inducium.

b) Inside the leptosporangiate sporangium, homosporous spores are found.

c) Sporangium wall breaks from stomium region due to annulus.

d) When spores come out then germinate to produce heart ‚ù£ shaped prothallus.

  • ii) Prothallus is green in colour except Rhizoid which is brown in colour.
  • iii) Prothallus is monoecious gametophyte.
  • iv) Antheridium     Archegonium
    • 1. Near rhizoid, dome shaped antheridium is present which produces multi-flagellated and coiled male gamete.
    • 2. Just below the apical notch, flask shaped archegonium is present with bent neck.
    • 3. Archegonium consist of
      • 1. 1 egg cell
      • 2. 1 Venter canal cell (VCC) and
      • 3. 1 binucleate Neck canal cell (NCC).
  • v) Zygote is formed after fertilization.
  • vi) Zygote develops into embryo which differentiate into diploid sporophytic plant of fern.
