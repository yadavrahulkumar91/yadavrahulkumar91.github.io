Stem

Roots are the silent but essential architects of life, anchoring plants to the ground and providing them with water and nutrients.

A. Introduction

I. Ascending part of plant.

II. Arises from Plumule of embryo

III. Stem hairs are commonly multicellular.

IV. Stem is differentiated into nodes and internodes.

V. Produce branches (exogenously - from surface meristem in the cortex), leaves, flowers, etc.

B. Classification

Aerial weak stem

  • Unable to stand
  • Have weak mechanical tissue.

Twiners

Plants main stem coil around the support without forming special organ for attachment.

  1. Direction of coiling is specific and genetically determined.
    • Left handed coiling/ Sinistrose twiner/ Anticlockwise twiners
    • Right handed coiling/ Dextrose twiners/ Clockwise twiners

Climbers

Use accessory organs for creeping

  1. Root climbers
  2. Tendril climbers
  3. Scramblers/ Ramblers
    • If plant use hooks for creeping then it is called Stragglers
    • Adventitious root for climbing

  7. Petiole modified = Nepenthes (Pitcher plant)
