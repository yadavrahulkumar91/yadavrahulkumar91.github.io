Stem

Roots are the silent but essential architects of life, anchoring plants to the ground and providing them with water and nutrients.

A. Introduction

I. Ascending part of plant.

II. Arises from Plumule of embryo

III. Stem hairs are commonly multicellular.

IV. Stem is differentiated into nodes and internodes.

V. Produce branches (exogenously - from surface meristem in the cortex), leaves, flowers, etc.

Modification of Stem

I. Phylloclade (@POEM)

  1. Green, photosynthetic, succulent stems of indefinite growth.
  2. Leaves are caducous.
  3. Formation of phylloclades helps the plants to grow in dry habitat.
    • Flattened:
      • Muehlenbeckia (Cocoloba)
      • Opuntia (Cactus)
    • Cylindrical:
      • Euphorbia
      • Casuarina

II. Cladode/ Cladophyll (@CAR)

  • Somewhat similar to phylloclade with the difference that they are of definite growth (usually one or two internode long).
  • It is also photosynthetic like phylloclades.
  • Example:
    • Asparagus
    • Ruscus
    • Lemna

III. Thalamus

  • Flower is a modified shoot which possess a highly condensed axis called Thalamus.
  • The edible part of an apple üçé (false fruit) is thalamus.
