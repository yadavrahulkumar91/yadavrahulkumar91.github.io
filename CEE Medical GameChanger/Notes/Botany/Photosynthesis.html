Photosynthesis

Water relations are crucial to plant survival, as they balance the competing demands of hydration and photosynthesis.

A. Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is simply defined as, formation of Carbohydrates from CO2 and H2O by illuminated green cells of plants.

B. Reaction

6CO2 + 12H2O18 →Light Chorophyll C6H12O6 + 6O218↑ + 6H2O

  1. CO2 and H2O → Raw material
  2. C6H12O6 → Required product
  3. O2 and H2O → By product
  4. Main/Ultimate source of energy is Sunlight.
    1. Maximum photosynthesis occurs in White light/ Sunlight.
    2. Best spectrum for photosynthesis(Effective) = White light > Red > Blue > Green
    3. Best absorption spectrum for photosynthesis = White light > Blue > Red > Green
  5. O2 evolved during photosynthesis comes from H2O and not from CO2.
    • This was experimentally confirmed by Ruben and Kamen, while working on Chlorella alga (unicellular green alga) using radioactive isotopes oxygen i.e, O218.
  6. O2 present in glucose molecule and water comes from CO2.
  7. About 90% of total photosynthesis in world is done by algae (mainly by BGA) in oceans and in freshwater. [IOM 2010]
  8. Volume of CO2 absorbed in photosynthesis is almost equal to O2 liberated. [IOM 2006]

C. The process of Photosynthesis is

  1. Reductive,
    • Photosynthesis is an oxidation and reduction process.
    • Photosynthesis is
      1. Photochemical process
      2. Oxidation process
      3. Oxidation-reduction process
      4. Reduction process

      The best answer is reduction than oxidation because glucose is formed by reduction of CO2.

  2. Endergonic (Energy requiring) and
  3. Anabolic.

Photosynthesis

D. During photosynthesis, light energy is converted into chemical energy in presence of chloroplast.

E. A leaf parenchyma contains 25-50 chloroplasts in each cell.

F. The photosynthetic pigments mostly found in the grana are associated with the thylakoid membrane.

  1. Reduction of CO2 to glucose → Dark reaction in both dark and light.
  2. Oxidation of H2O to O2 → Light reaction in light.
  3. So, O2 is produced only during day but C6H12O6 is produced during both day and light.
  4. In bacteria, H2S is oxidized. Hence in place of O2, sulphur is produced.
    1. In bacterial photosynthesis, the hydrogen donor as H2S.

G. Photosynthetic pigments

  1. Chlorophyll:
    1. Chl-a
    2. Chl-b
    3. Chl-c
    4. Chl-d
    5. Chl-e

    Chlorophyll is a magnesium porphyrin compound which contains hydrophilic porphyrin head and lypophilic phytol tail. The whole structure resembles a tadpole larva.

    Note:

    • When chlorophyll is burnt, the leftover material is Mg.
    • Mg-porphyrin head contains 4 pyrole ring.
    • Phytol tail is located at 7th carbon of 4th pyrole ring.
    • As compared to chlorophyll a, bacteriochlorophyll has 1 pyrole ring with 2 hydrogen.
    • Bacterial photosynthesis differs from that of higher plants in not liberating oxygen.
