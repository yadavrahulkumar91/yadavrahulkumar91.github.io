  3. Compound microscope was invented by Janssen.

  1. Light microscope
    1. About
      • 2. Light microscope
      • Light microscope is commonly used to examine histological sections by transillumination.
      • Transillumination is the technique of sample illumination by transmission of light through the sample.
    2. Special types of Light microscope
      • Types of Microscope Features
      • Phase contrast microscope
        • Phase contrast microscope is used to study the living cells and tissues without fixing and staining them.
        • E.g.:
          1. Chromosomes in the living cell.
          2. Culture of living cells
      • Dark-field microscope
        • Dark-field microscope is used for visualizing the objects which do not reflect i.e., transparent object.
      • Interference microscope
        • Interference microscope is used for qualitative study of cell component and cell constituents.
      • Polarizing microscope
        • Polarizing microscope is used to study the spindle fibre/ centriole where plane polarized light is used.
      • Fluorescent microscope
        • High wavelength UV light is used in fluorescent microscope.
        • A fluorescence microscope is an optical microscope that uses fluorescence and phosphorescence instead of, or in addition to, reflection and absorption to study properties of organic or inorganic substances.
        • It is used to investigate membrane qualities of the phospholipid bilayers of the cell.
  1. Electron microscope
    • Electron microscope was developed by Knolll and Ruska in Germany.
    • Light source in electron microscope is beam of electrons.
    • Ultra-structure of a cell organelle can be best studied through Electron microscope.
    • Electron microscope used 3 lenses
      1. Occular/ Eye piece
      2. Objective
      3. Condenser
    • Electron microscope works on the principle of de Broglie waves (or matter waves) i.e., wave nature of electrons.
    • The electron microscope is better than a light microscope because electron microscope has high resolving power and high magnification i.e., it uses higher energy electrons or less wavelength electrons.
    • It has revealed many small cell organelles like ribosomes, centrioles, microbodies, etc.
    • 8. In electron microscopy, ultra-thin specimens are impregnated with heavy metals.
    • Scanning electron microscope üî¨ is used to prepare 3D image of the structure.

D. Separation

a. Poly Acryl-Amide Gel Electrophoresis (PAGE)

PAGE is used for the Separation of Proteins.

b. X-ray diffraction/ Crystallography

i. It is a technique to determine the 3-Dimensional Positions of atoms in the molecules like Protein and Nucleic acids (DNA), i.e., structure of proteins and nucleic acids.

ii. The first protein whose structure was determined by X-ray diffraction is Myoglobin.

c. Autoradiography

i. Autoradiography is used to study the synthesis of molecules and to trace metabolic pathways.

ii. Autoradiography is the technique used for knowing the pathway of carbon assimilation.

iii. Hershey and Chase (1952) used 32P and 35S to prove that DNA is the genetic material.

iv. In the experiment of Melson and Stahl to demonstrate semiconservative mode of DNA replication, the radio-isotope used was N15.

d. Radioimmunoassay

i. This technique is used for estimation of minimum amount of hormones and drug.

e. Sub-cellular components are separated by Cell-fractionation/ Centrifugation.

i. Mitochondria are separated from cell be Differential centrifugation.

f. Southern blotting is the technique used for separating DNA fragments.

g. Northern blotting is the technique used for separating RNA fragments.

i. Note: Western blotting is the confirmatory test/ diagnostic test for HIV.

  1. But, the screening test for HIV is ELISA (Enzyme Linked Immuno Sorbent Assay)
