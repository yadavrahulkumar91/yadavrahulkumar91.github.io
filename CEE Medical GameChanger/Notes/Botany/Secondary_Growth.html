Secondary Growth

Tissue systems provide the framework for plant growth and development, from the root to the leaf.

Increase in thickness of plant body due to addition of secondary tissue by the activity of vascular and cork cambium in stelar and extrastelar region respectively is called Secondary growth.
It occurs in Dicot.

A. Secondary growth in Dicot stem:

  • Cells of medullary rays, become meristematic and form interfascicular cambium to join intrafascicular cambium to form a complete ring called Cambium ring.
  • The cambium ring consists of 2 types of cells, the fusiform initials and the ray initials.
    • Fusiform initials form secondary phloem on the exterior and secondary xylem on the interior side.
      • That present in secondary xylem is known as Wood or Xylem ray and that is secondary phloem is called Phloem ray.
    • Ray initials form parenchymatous medullary rays.
  • In spring (favourable season), cambium produces conducting elements of wider diameter called Spring or Early wood.
  • In winter, it produces elements of narrow diameter known as Autumn or Late wood.
  • Each year's growth is distinct due to sudden stem appear as concentric rings known as Growth rings/ Annual rings and represent total wood formed in a year.
    • Each year's growth is distinct due to sudden transition from autumn wood to spring wood of next year. The transition from spring to autumn wood is gradual.
    • Annual rings are used to study the climate of that area known as Dendroclimatology.
    • Annual rings are formed due to unequal activity of cambium in autumn and spring season.
    • Annual rings are absent in plants growing in places having uniform climate.

The age determination of trees by counting the annual growth rings is known as Dendrochronology.

Heartwood/Duramen Sapwood/Alburnum
The dead dark coloured portion of the wood at the centre due to the deposition of organic compounds like oil, aromatic substances, gums, resins, tannins, etc. The peripheral light coloured, wetter, water conducting wood.
Heart wood helps in mechanical support. Functional xylem of dicot tree is Sapwood.
The substances deposited in the heartwood are collectively called Extractives. Which decay faster if exposed freely to the air?
Heart wood increases in diameter during secondary growth. a) Heart wood
Heartwood is much more durable and resistant to micro-organisms and insects, pests, etc. than sapwood. b) Sap wood
Oils, resins, gums and tannins are deposited in the cells of heartwood which provide colour to the heartwood. These are also antiseptic, so heartwood is hard and durable. c) Wood with lot of fibres
The vessels of heartwood get plugged by the ingrowths of balloon like projections of adjacent parenchyma into their cavities called Tyloses. d) Soft wood

The wood of older stems (eg. Dalbergia) gets differentiated into outer lighter and functional sapwood or alburnum and inner darker or non-functional heartwood or Duramen.

Stumps of broken branches also get embedded in secondary xylem (produced by secondary growth) to form Knots.

Process by which plant becomes woody is called Lignification.

Pressure is created by addition of tissues and a meristematic zone of cork cambium (Phellogen) in the outer (extrastelar) zone. It originates either from outer cortical cells or collenchyma or epidermis.

Cork cambium gives rise to secondary cortex (or phelloderm) on the inner side and cork (or Phellem) outside.

All new tissues, cork cambium, secondary cortex and cork formed in peripheral region are together known as periderm and all the dead tissue lying outside vascular cambium is called Bark; they possess lenticels, for gaseous exchange.
