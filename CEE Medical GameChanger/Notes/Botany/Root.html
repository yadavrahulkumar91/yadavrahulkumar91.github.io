Root

Roots are the silent but essential architects of life, anchoring plants to the ground and providing them with water and nutrients./p>

Parts of Root

  1. Root is:

    • Underground
    • Non-green
    • Descending part of plant (+Vely Geotropic and -vely Phototropic)
      • Exception Apogeotropic (bending up or away from the ground)/ -vely geotropic
        • Coralloid roots of Cycas
        • Pneumatophores of Mangroove plants

  2. Root helps in:

    • Fixation
    • Absorption
    • Conduction

    Some plantless roots perform the function of:

    • Assimilation
    • Reproduction

I. Modification of Tap roots

  1. 1. Fleshy tap root (Storage root)

    Shape Example
    Fusiform root 1. Raphanus sativus (Radish)
    Napiform root 1. Brassica rapa (Turnip)
    Conical root 1. Daucus carota (Carrot)
    Tuberous root 1. Mirabilis jalapa (4 O'clock plant)

  2. 2. Nodulated/ Tuberculated tap root (Root nodules)

    1. Primary and secondary roots of Legumes plants have root nodules.
    2. Nodulated roots make the plant rich in protein due to Nitrogen fixation by Rhizobium bacteria present in it symbiotically.
    3. Inside nodules, pigment called Leghaemoglobin is present to protect the enzyme Nitrogenase and Supply O2 to vigorously respiring bacteria

Pneumatophores/ Respiratory roots/ Breathing roots

  1. -vely geotropic roots
  2. In Mangroove plants, i.e, plants growing in saline marshes.
    1. Rhizophora
    2. Avicenia
  3. They have minute pores on their exposed portion called Pneumatothodes to take O2.
    1. Pneumatothodes are anatomically and morphologically similar to lenticels.
  4. Buttress root
    1. Laterally compressed horizontal root at the base of main stem to give additional support to the plant.
    2. These roots are formed jointly by base of trunk and tap root.
    3. Example
      1. Ficus religiosa (Peepal)
      2. Ficus elastica (Rubber plant)
      3. Bombax ceiba (Simal)
  5. Mycorrhizal roots:
    1. Root cap and Root hair are absent.
    2. Fungal hyphae covers the entire root.
    3. Fungal hyphae provides phosphorus nutrient to host when necessary.
    4. Eg:
      1. Pinus
      2. Betula (as ectomycorrhiza)
      3. Orchid (as endomycorrhiza)
  6. Reproductive root:
    1. These tap root develop adventitious buds and helps in vegetative propagation.
    2. Example
      1. Dalbergia
